The headline is "slightly below normal" — and I already know how that's going to get used. Clients will hear "below normal" and take their foot off the gas. That's a mistake. Ian was a below-normal season. Wilma was a below-normal season. One storm makes all the difference, and right now we have exactly **38 days** before June 1 to prepare for it.

Here's what I'm telling every service provider I work with right now: this is the best two months of the year to have the documentation conversation with your clients and referral partners. Pre-loss photos, roof age records, inventory, policy review — none of it costs anything, and all of it makes a claim dispute dramatically easier to win. After June 1, everyone is reactive. Right now, you have the luxury of being proactive.

If you're a roofer, plumber, HVAC tech, or contractor who works on South Florida properties — you are on the front lines. You see conditions homeowners don't. You know when a roof is marginal, when a water heater is 15 years old, when a drain stack is one bad storm away from a problem. That knowledge is valuable. A referral relationship with a public adjuster means your clients get better claim outcomes, and it means you get calls back when the work starts.
