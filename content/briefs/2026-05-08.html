HCI Group Delivers Record Q1 Results; Premium Growth Signals Market Confidence

Tampa-based HCI Group, now in its 20th year as a company and 18th as a public entity, reported strong first-quarter 2026 results: net income of $85 million, diluted EPS of $5.45 (beating consensus by $0.19), and gross premiums earned of $326 million. Three of its four insurance carriers—Homeowners Choice, TypTap Insurance, and Tailrow Insurance Exchange—posted gross premium growth in Q1. The company is executing a $80 million share repurchase program through February 2027.

Why it matters for service providers: Strong carrier profitability and premium growth indicate confidence in the Florida market and capacity to handle claims activity. Service providers can expect steady claims volume and timely payments from carriers backed by solid earnings. The reinsurance renewal at $3.5 billion (30% increase on prior year) further solidifies HCI's capacity heading into hurricane season.

Sources: Insurance Journal—HCI Q1 Results | HCI Press Release | Artemis—Reinsurance Renewal

Extreme Heat Through Weekend; HeatRisk Index Shows MAJOR to EXTREME Risk

South Florida is experiencing a prolonged heat event with temperatures rising through Friday and into the weekend. High temperatures are forecast in the upper 80s to mid-90s, with heat index values reaching the upper 90s to lower 100s—unusually extreme for this early in the season. The National Weather Service has issued an experimental HeatRisk designation showing MAJOR risk across most areas, with pockets of EXTREME risk in the western suburbs of the Southeast Miami-Dade metro area.

Why it matters for property owners & service providers:
  • Heat stress on HVAC systems will spike demand for emergency air conditioning repairs; contractors should monitor call volumes and staffing.
  • Roof and attic ventilation systems may fail prematurely under sustained heat; inspections and upgrades are likely needed.
  • Pool equipment, hot water systems, and electrical panels are at risk of thermal stress; property managers should conduct cooling-system checks.
  • Property insurance inspectors should document heat-related wear during coverage reviews.

Sources: NWS Miami Forecast | NWS HeatRisk Information

INSURANCE & REGULATION

Auto Insurance Rate Cuts Confirmed; USAA Delivers $125M in Annual Savings

Florida's Office of Insurance Regulation has confirmed that major auto carriers are delivering on promised rate reductions for 2026. USAA has approved an average 7% rate cut effective by May 2026, resulting in more than $125 million in estimated annual savings for Florida members. Florida's top five auto insurance groups are currently indicating an average -8% rate change for 2026.

The Office of Insurance Regulation reports that homeowners' insurance rate-reduction requests are also accelerating: the 30-day average request is now -2.3% (compared to +0.5% one year ago), and the 180-day average is -0.7% (compared to +7.9% one year ago). OIR has received more than 185 residential filing requests for rate decreases or 0% increases.

Sources: OIR Auto Rate Approval | Live Insurance News—2026 Rate Cuts
