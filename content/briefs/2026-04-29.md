## Lead Stories

### Public Adjuster Fraud Case: $600K Scheme Highlights Licensing Concerns
A significant fraud case underscores both the opportunity and the risk in public adjusting. Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus of All Elements Public Adjusters in Miramar faces charges of fraud, grand theft, and misappropriation of funds for diverting more than $600,000 in insurance settlements owed to 10 policyholders. His Florida PA license was suspended in 2015 and again in 2024; his firm operates unlicensed. **Implications for new adjusters:** Homeowners increasingly seek legitimate, licensed public adjusters as they learn about fraudulent schemes—demand is high, but licensing and credibility matter. Policyholders who hire reputable PAs receive settlements 747% higher than those handling claims alone. [Insurance Journal](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southeast/2026/04/01/864289.htm)

### South Florida Real Estate Market Accelerates: Inventory Growing, High-End Dominates
Miami-Dade residential sales rose 6.6% year-over-year in March 2026, with single-family sales up 10.6% and condo sales up 2.9%. Premium segment (>$5M) jumped 27%; cash sales surged 23.9%—strong investor confidence. Meanwhile, the share of million-dollar homes in Miami-Dade jumped from 8% (2019) to 28% (Q1 2026), signaling wealth concentration. Broward County saw modest growth (1.4% YoY) but premium properties (>$1M) climbed 7.4%. **Market context:** Growing inventory (up 21% YoY in Miami) and a 4.8-month supply in Broward single-family homes indicate a shifting seller's market. Only 21 of 2,397 condo buildings in South Florida are FHA-approved—a major financing bottleneck. ### Insurance Rate Relief Materializes: Broward Down 14%, Miami-Dade 14%, Palm Beach 12%
Florida's insurance market reform is delivering results. Broward County homeowners are seeing an average 14.1% rate reduction, Miami-Dade 14.0%, and Palm Beach 11.9%—driven by litigation reforms (elimination of one-way attorney fees and abusive assignment-of-benefits practices). Citizens Property Insurance is cutting an average 8.7% statewide in Spring 2026. Major carriers (State Farm, USAA, GEICO, Allstate, Progressive) all filed decreases of 7–10%. **Cost baseline:** Average annual homeowners insurance in Florida is $7,136; State Farm is cheapest at $4,654. [Governor's Office Press Release](https://www.flgov.com/eog/news/press/2026/governor-ron-desantis-announces-major-insurance-rate-relief-floridas-reforms)

## Residential Service Industries

### Roofing Demand High; Labor Shortage and Cost Pressure Persist
- **Market drivers:** Aging roofing systems, storm preparedness, rising insurance premiums, humidity/heat/salt spray corrosion in South Florida
- **Trend:** Homeowners investing in protective upgrades (tile, shingle, metal, flat-roof systems rated for Category 5 conditions)
- **Cost landscape:** Roof replacement costs rising due to insurance, code changes, and labor demand. Labor shortage is acute—experienced roofers retiring faster than new workers are trained.
- **Post-storm dynamics:** High post-storm demand creates regional pricing spikes in coastal metros
- **Waterproofing costs:** 2026 baseline is $4–12 per square foot, depending on method and roof complexity

## Weather & Atlantic Hurricane Outlook

### 2026 Atlantic Hurricane Season Forecast: Below-Average Activity Expected
Colorado State University forecasts:
- **13 named storms** (vs. long-term average ~19)
- **6 hurricanes** (vs. ~10 average)
- **2 major hurricanes** (vs. ~3 average)
- **Activity level:** ~75% of historical seasonal average

**El Niño arrival key factor:** Climate Prediction Center gives 61% probability El Niño emerges May–July 2026; El Niño typically suppresses Atlantic hurricane intensity and frequency.

**Florida-specific outlook:**
- 74% chance a named storm passes within 50 miles of Florida (vs. 86% historical average)
- 15% probability for major hurricanes making U.S. East Coast landfall, including Florida peninsula (vs. 21% average)

## Summary

This week's briefing highlights a strengthening market environment for public adjusters and service contractors:

1. **Insurance landscape improving:** Rate relief flowing through South Florida (11–14% decreases), litigation down, but fraud remains a credibility test for the profession.
2. **Real estate momentum strong:** Miami-Dade accelerating; Broward stable but selective; all markets hindered by FHA financing bottleneck.
3. **Service demand robust:** Roofing and water mitigation demand high; labor shortage sustaining price pressure.
4. **Weather outlook moderate:** Below-average hurricane season expected (El Niño suppressing activity); lower insured loss risk creates opportunity for relationship-building vs. reactive claims surge.
