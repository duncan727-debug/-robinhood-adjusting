Mandatory Flood Insurance Now Layered Onto Every Citizens Wind Policy Over $400K — Universal Phase Hits Jan 1, 2027

Effective January 1, 2026, every Citizens Property Insurance policy on a home insured above $400,000 for wind must also carry a flood policy as a condition of keeping that wind coverage. The requirement steps up again on January 1, 2027, when all Citizens personal-residential wind policies — regardless of home value, regardless of FEMA flood zone — must carry flood. This is the largest single change to South Florida's flood insurance footprint since FEMA's Risk Rating 2.0 rollout.

The mechanism: the flood policy can be NFIP or a private flood writer with an OIR-approved binding form. If a Citizens policyholder lets the flood policy lapse, the wind policy non-renews at the next anniversary.

What it means for homeowners: If you're on a Citizens wind policy above $400K and your renewal hits between now and Jan 1, you should already have a flood policy bound. If you don't, your renewal declarations may carry a conditional-coverage notice. For the broader Citizens book, the universal Jan 1, 2027 deadline means the entire 395,000-policy population needs a flood policy in force inside the next 19 months. The cheapest moves: bind early (NFIP rates don't move on you between elevation certificate, building characteristics, and date of binding) and check whether a private flood writer is meaningfully cheaper for elevated, post-FIRM homes.

What it means for service providers: Restoration contractors and water-mitigation firms should expect a measurable increase in insured flood claims starting in the first storm cycle that follows. Prior to the mandate, a large share of inland Palm Beach County homes carried wind without flood — losses outside the Special Flood Hazard Area went uncovered. Beginning June 1, 2026, more of those losses will be insurable events with carriers paying mitigation invoices instead of homeowners paying out of pocket.

What it means for real estate professionals: This is now a disclosure-conversation item on every Citizens-insured listing. Buyers asking "what's my insurance cost?" need a flood line on the estimate, not just wind. Listings priced on wind-only insurance comps will mis-state monthly carrying cost — and on financed transactions, the lender will require the flood policy regardless.

Citizens Files for 2.6% Statewide Personal-Lines Rate Cut Effective June 2026 — 60% of Customers See Avg 11.5% Premium Reduction

Citizens Property Insurance Corporation's Board of Governors has voted to file an average personal-lines rate cut of 2.6% statewide, with the new rates targeted to take effect in June 2026. The blended number understates what most customers will see: roughly 60% of policyholders are slated for an average premium reduction of 11.5% if OIR approves the filing as submitted. The remaining 40% are skewed to higher-risk coastal census blocks where the actuarial book still demands an increase.

This is the first time since 2018 that Citizens — historically the lever the Legislature pulled when private-market rates ran hot — has filed a negative statewide change. It joins the State Farm 10%, Florida Peninsula 8.4%, and Patriot Select 11.3% decreases that crossed OIR's desk last week. Combined with the 73 filings for rate decreases and 94 for 0% change that OIR had already logged by late November 2025, the cumulative weight of the market is now pointing down.

What it means for homeowners: If you are currently insured with Citizens, your June or later renewal is more likely than not to come back lower, not flat, not higher. Confirm the new rate is reflected on your declarations page; the takeout rule still applies, which means a 20%-or-narrower private offer will move you off Citizens involuntarily. Right now is the best opportunity in three years to shop privately and also benchmark against Citizens.

What it means for real estate professionals: Citizens has been the carrier of last resort and the boogeyman of buyer-financing math for half a decade. A negative Citizens filing is a market signal worth quoting to sidelined buyers. The headline number to memorize: 2.6% average, 11.5% for the majority.

Citizens Depopulation Cleared 546,000 Policies in 2025 — Active Count Now ~395,000, Lowest Since 2012

The Citizens Depopulation Program transferred more than 546,000 policies to approved private takeout carriers during calendar 2025, the largest single-year depopulation in the program's history. Citizens' active policy count peaked at 1.42 million in October 2023 and has fallen ~73% to roughly 395,000 by early 2026 — the lowest the company has carried since 2012. The depopulation engine, when paired with the new private carriers entering the market (20 since reform, including Builder Reciprocal, Frontline Reciprocal, and Wingsail Insurance announced last week), is now structurally lowering Citizens' exposure ahead of the 2026 hurricane season.

What it means for homeowners: If you received a Citizens takeout offer in 2025 and accepted, you're now in a private book — review whether the new carrier offers a wind mitigation re-inspection credit you didn't have on your old Citizens policy. Most takeout carriers will give credit for an up-to-date OIR-1802 inspection. If you declined a takeout that came within 20% of your Citizens premium, you are now ineligible for Citizens by statute regardless. Read takeout letters carefully.

What it means for service providers: The mitigation-inspection refresh cycle around takeouts is a near-term revenue line for the small subset of contractors who hold the wind mitigation inspector certification. Roofers with a wind-mit inspector on staff have a cross-sell that lowers the homeowner's premium and gets them in the door for the next roof conversation.

REAL ESTATE & COMMERCIAL ACTIVITY

MIAMI REALTORS® and RWorld Complete Merger — Now 93,000 Members, World's Largest Local Realtor Association

The MIAMI Association of REALTORS® and Broward, Palm Beaches & St. Lucie Realtors® (the former "RWorld") closed their historic merger earlier this month, combining their respective MLSs — MIAMI MLS and BeachesMLS — into a single platform. The combined organization counts approximately 93,000 members, making it the largest local Realtor association in the world. Pending NAR approval, the new entity will be branded Miami and South Florida REALTORS®.

What it means for real estate professionals: The practical effect for agents working any of the five South Florida counties is one MLS subscription, one set of forms, and one association political voice across the entire footprint from Stuart to the Keys. Cross-county listing access is now native rather than reciprocity-based — Broward agents listing Palm Beach properties (and vice versa) no longer juggle MLS-of-origin issues at closing. For PA/insurance referral partners, the network effect is significant: a single email blast or co-marketing campaign now lands in 93,000 inboxes instead of two segmented lists.

What it means for homeowners and providers: Indirectly. Listing data quality and inventory transparency improve when two MLSs become one — the long-tail effect is more accurate comps and faster price discovery in border-zip neighborhoods (e.g., Boca/Coral Springs, Jupiter/Tequesta).

Wellington-Adjacent 200-Acre Data Center Proposal — Sited ~1,000 Ft From Arden

A developer is moving forward on what would be western Palm Beach County's first large-scale data center, a 200-acre AI/cloud facility sited roughly 1,000–1,200 feet from the Arden community and about five miles from the Village of Wellington. The project is in early planning-and-zoning review.

What it means for real estate professionals: Data centers are mixed news at the parcel-pricing level. Adjacent ag and equestrian land typically picks up speculative buying interest on the rumor, then absorbs a noise/light-pollution discount once operations begin. The 1,000-foot setback from Arden is the practical issue — homeowners within line-of-sight will likely organize, and HOA-driven opposition is the variable to track at the next BCC zoning hearing.

What it means for service providers: A 200-acre data center build is a multi-year mechanical, electrical, fire-suppression, and security-systems project. The MEP scopes alone will support several mid-size contractor crews for 24+ months. Trades that should be tracking the entitlement timeline now: site work, fire protection, low-voltage, switchgear, and chiller/HVAC.

What it means for homeowners: Expect a public-comment window. Western-PBC homeowners — especially Arden, Loxahatchee Groves, and surrounding equestrian parcels — should plan to attend or submit written comment if proximity is a concern. Property values within the 1-mile ring will be sensitive to the BCC vote.

Rainfall: 0.5–1.0 inches widespread; localized totals 2–4 inches where training cells set up. That's enough to surface drainage issues — call-out volume for water-intrusion claims typically spikes 24–48 hours after a 3″-in-1-hour event.

Coastal hazard: Frequent rip currents the entire east coast of Florida; East Coast wave heights 4–5 feet on Memorial Day.

What it means for homeowners: Standard pre-season check applies — keep gutters and primary drains clear, check sump batteries, secure outdoor furniture before afternoon storms. What it means for service providers: A 2–4″ localized event in a holiday week is the classic surge-call setup. Mitigation, plumbing, and roofers running 24-hour lines should expect the Tuesday-morning catch-up wave. Same-day response wins the next three months of work, not just this job.

CONTRACTORS & SERVICE INDUSTRIES

Permanent Sales Tax Exemption on Disaster-Prep Supplies Has Replaced the Annual Tax Holiday — Reminder for Service Providers

Since August 1, 2025, Florida has carried a permanent year-round sales tax exemption on a defined list of disaster-preparedness supplies — batteries, portable generators, smoke and CO alarms, fire extinguishers, and related items. This replaced the prior structure of episodic disaster-prep tax holidays.

What it means for service providers: If you sell or include these items in a customer-facing invoice (e.g., a roofer installing soffit smoke alarms, a generator-installation electrician), the sales tax exemption applies year-round, not just during a posted holiday window. Confirm your POS/invoicing system is set up to exempt the qualifying SKUs by default; this is a recurring audit issue for contractors who only updated their tax settings for the old May/June holiday window.

What it means for homeowners: No need to wait for a "tax holiday" weekend to buy a generator or a smoke detector. The exemption is on every day of the year now.
