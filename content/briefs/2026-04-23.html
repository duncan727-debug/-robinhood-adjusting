Regulatory & Policy Shifts

New Wind-Loss Mitigation Form (OIR-B1-1802) — Effective April 1

Form OIR-B1-1802 was updated via Rule 69O-170.0155 and reflects findings from the 2024 Residential Wind-Loss Mitigation Study. Eligible fixtures and construction techniques for premium discounts have been revised, and submission instructions to insurers have changed.

Impact: Wind-mit inspection reports drive premium discounts, and discrepancies between old-form and new-form inspections are becoming dispute areas on renewals and post-loss settlements. Expect client questions about shifted credits at renewal.

SB 808 — Roof Inspector Pool Expansion (Effective July 1, 2026)

Senate Bill 808 expands who can perform roof inspections to include home inspectors, building code inspectors, general/building/residential contractors, professional engineers, and professional architects — in addition to insurer-recognized inspectors. For roofs 15+ years old, insurers must now differentiate low-slope (≤ 2" pitch) from steep-slope (> 2" pitch) in coverage offers.

Impact: More eligible inspector categories = higher variance in inspection quality. Inspection-quality disputes will be a growing surface for claims friction.

Senate Bill 266 — PA Contract Protections (Effective July 1, 2026)

SB 266 strengthens consumer protections for PA contracts: contract disclosures must appear in minimum 18-point font; enhanced cancellation rights are mandated for vulnerable adults.

Impact: All PAs must review and update contract templates before July 1. Non-compliant contracts invite regulatory scrutiny.

Loss Environment & Claims Landscape

2026 Hurricane Season Forecast: Below-Normal But Not Benign

Colorado State University's April forecast calls for 13 named storms, 6 hurricanes, and 2 major hurricanes — slightly below the 30-year average. El Niño conditions are expected to elevate wind shear across the Tropical Atlantic.

However: CSU puts odds of a major hurricane landfall on the U.S. coast at 32% (Caribbean: 35%). Season runs June 1 to November 30.

Impact: "Below-normal" ≠ benign. This is the best two months to push pre-loss documentation and contract signings before the market goes reactive on June 1.

Water Damage & Mold — Capacity Strained, Pricing Rising

More than 3× normal rainfall since April 1 across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach has pushed remediation firms to full capacity through summer.

Pricing benchmarks:

  • Standard mold jobs: $2,500–$6,000
  • Older single-family / small condo projects: $20,000+ after insulation, cabinetry, subfloor removal
Claim friction hotspot: Claims that straddle policy categories — covered water event vs. excluded long-term seepage — are leaving homeowners with partial reimbursements or steep deductibles. This is prime PA territory.

Ecosystem Spotlight

Mold & Water Remediation Network: Build Your Referral Bridge

Firms booked solid through summer represent a bottleneck and opportunity. Key regional players include Mold Only, R.E. Mold, Pure Maintenance, and FP Property Restoration.

Strategic angle: Water/mold claims that straddle coverage boundaries are exactly where a PA + remediator partnership adds value. Homeowners stuck between coverage and exclusion benefit from coordinated advocacy. This builds referral relationships and ecosystem trust.
