## Regulatory & Policy

### Florida Legislature Pauses Major Insurance Overhaul; Market Stabilization Reduces Policy Urgency

The Florida Legislature is signaling a pause on sweeping property and homeowners insurance reforms for the 2026 session. Senate President Ben Albritton stated that **no major changes to property insurance law are expected** this year, citing landmark legislation passed in 2022–2023 that has stabilized the market and brought rate relief to consumers.

**Ongoing narrow measures:**
- **House Bill 815** — "Roof age and condition" protections; prohibits insurers from refusing coverage solely due to roof age without inspection; allows homeowner-initiated roof inspections before carrier-mandated replacements
- **Senate Bill 1452** — Expands eligibility for My Safe Florida Home program (wind mitigation grants); would extend three-story-or-less detached homes eligibility for roof replacements and mitigation improvements

**Implication for PAs:** The legislative pause signals confidence in the market trajectory. Wind mitigation and coverage optimization (topics addressed in SB 1452 and HB 815) are now being driven by market demand, not legislative mandate. PAs should position mitigation consulting and claims-readiness as value-add services aligned with carrier underwriting standards, rather than regulatory catch-up efforts.

**Timeline:** Legislative deadlines approaching; bills with narrow scope expected to advance.

**Carrier-specific actions:**

- **GEICO:** 700,000+ Florida customers receiving rate relief (effective April 2026)

**Cumulative impact:** Families receiving multiple rate notifications (Citizens homeowner, auto insurance, USAA) in May are **most receptive to PA optimization conversations.** Positioning: "Your premiums are dropping because the market is stabilizing. Let's audit your coverage to ensure you're protected for what insurance actually covers."

**Connection to property insurance:** Auto and property rate relief reflect the same underlying trend—litigation down 35% since 2021, fraud prosecutions active, and reinsurance costs normalizing. Market confidence is real.

## Contractor & Service Sector

### Labor Shortage Remains Industry #1 Challenge; 90% of Roofers Report Staffing as Biggest Constraint

Despite market optimism and strong bookings, Florida roofing and construction contractors continue to face acute labor shortages. **90% of roofers report finding skilled labor as their #1 business constraint.**

**Current mitigation strategies being deployed:**
- **Structured career paths** replacing "sink or swim" training; retention focus prioritized over recruitment
- **AI-driven tools** for workforce planning, fatigue risk detection, and overbooked crew alerts
- **Apprenticeship expansion** and wage competitiveness to attract younger workers
- **Immigration policy advocacy** (Dignity Act support) to stabilize seasonal and specialty labor pools

**Market signal:** Labor constraints are not easing despite rate relief and favorable weather forecasts. Contractors booking mitigation work for June–July are locking in May installation schedules now to secure crews before hurricane-season demand surge.

**For PAs:** Partnerships with pre-vetted, fully-booked contractors create referral value. Homeowners needing documented mitigation work (for coverage optimization or claims defense) benefit from PA connections to contractors who can deliver on schedule.
