Boca Raton Says Yes to Hotel-to-Workforce Conversion, Slows Down on City Land

Two May 12 Boca Raton City Council moves that change what you say at the next closing in either direction:

1. Adaptive reuse is open for business. Resolution 45-2026 approved the conversion of the 183-room Holiday Inn at 701 NW 53rd Street into a Commercial Industrial Multifamily Development (CIMD) — 125 rental units, including affordable and workforce-priced product. For investor-buyers asking what kind of hotel and retail parcels are re-entitlement candidates this cycle, the answer just got clearer: privately-owned hospitality with adequate parking and a workforce-housing component is the path of least resistance.

2. Anything that touches municipal land has a new political gate. The Council advanced the Save Boca ordinance and a related charter amendment — a charter-level rule that any sale or repurposing of city-owned land has to go to voters first. The question heads to the next available election. For your transaction practice: city-owned-land deals are not dead, but the timeline now includes an electoral cycle. Tell investor-clients that today, not on the way to closing.

Separately, the Council reviewed background on freestanding emergency facilities at its May 26 workshop — no vote, but it is the opening framework for stand-alone ER regulation, which has knock-on disclosure implications for adjacent residential.

The Insurance Conversation at Every Showing Just Got Three New Sentences

The disclosure-to-buyer conversation through summer 2026 has three additions that did not exist 30 days ago. Drop them in.

Sentence one — the renewal letter the buyer will receive in 12 months has new rules. Effective July 1, HB 815 prohibits carriers from non-renewing a policy solely because the roof is over a given age. If the home's roof is 12-18 years old in good condition, that is no longer an automatic renewal-kill.

Sentence two — Citizens premiums are coming down, not up, this cycle. The pending 2.6% statewide personal-lines rate cut is effective with June 2026 renewals. About 60% of Citizens customers see a meaningful decrease. If the listing is Citizens-insured, that is a fact you can use.

Sentence three — there is up to $10,000 available to harden this home after closing. My Safe Florida Home is still funded. Eligibility is income-and-value-tested (≤120% AMI, homesteaded, built before 1/1/2008, insured ≤$700K), but for buyers who fit, a same-year retrofit creates a measurably lower renewal next April.

Pair the three together at any first showing for a Citizens-insured pre-2008 SFH under ~$700K of coverage. That is your premium-relief listing narrative for the summer.
