Roofers: HB 715 Quietly Expanded Your Scope of Work

The CILB scope memo flowing from HB 715 (signed by Gov. DeSantis on May 19, 2025) revised the statutory definition of "roofing contractor" to include the evaluation and enhancement of roof-to-wall connections on structures with wood roof decking.

What that means in plain English: roof-to-wall strap retrofits — the work that drives wind-mit credits on the new OIR-B1-1802 form — is now squarely inside your roofing scope. You no longer need to coordinate a structural sub or punt that line item to a GC. If you were already including it informally, you are now on solid statutory ground. If you weren't, this is the cross-sell.

Pipeline play: Pair a re-roof estimate with a roof-to-wall strap retrofit quote and a wind-mit re-inspection. Three line items, one truck-roll, one credit-improving outcome the homeowner can show the carrier. If they hit the new wind-mit form right, their renewal arrives lower — and that is the call-back that wins next year's referral.

Your License Renews August 31 — Start the CE Now

Certified state licenses (roofing, building, residential, mechanical, plumbing, etc.) expire on August 31 of every even year. That is August 31, 2026 — 13 weeks from today. Required hours for the biennial renewal:

Hours Topic
1 Workplace safety
1 Workers' compensation
1 Business practices
11 Trade-specific or general construction
**14** **Total**

Approved providers (Gold Coast, 1 Exam Prep, AGC, FRSA, FRCBA, etc.) all run online courses you can knock out across two weekends. If you have a journeyman or a foreman who holds a separate cert, this applies to them too. Don't let one of your people get into September and discover they cannot pull a permit.

My Safe Florida Home Is Still Writing Checks — Get on the Approved Vendor List

The program's $352M from last August has carryover into this cycle, and a proposed FY 2026-27 budget request north of $600 million is on the table. Grants are up to $10,000 per home for impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, roof upgrades, and wind-rated garage doors.

Two operator-level facts that matter to you:

1. The grant pays the contractor directly. The homeowner does not float the cash and you do not chase invoices. The state's vendor-payment portal is the cleanest receivables channel currently available to a residential trade in this state.

2. Vendor approval has a wait. If you are not already on the list, start the application — credentials, certs, insurance, references — this week. Crews not on the list cannot book this work, period.

Eligibility for the homeowner side: at or below 120% of county median income, homesteaded, built before Jan 1, 2008, insured for $700K or less. That is your target customer profile when you canvass — older Wellington / Royal Palm / Greenacres single-family on tile or shingle, primary residence, working couple. Door-knock script writes itself.

Materials Are Permanently Sales-Tax-Exempt Now — Stop Hoarding for the Holiday

The temporary tax holidays are gone. Florida's 2025 tax-relief package made these permanently exempt from state sales tax, year-round:

  • Batteries, portable generators, smoke detectors, fire extinguishers
  • Waterproof tarps
  • Ground anchor systems and tie-down kits
  • Insect repellent, sunscreen, life jackets, portable gas/diesel cans

If you have been timing material orders to a two-week window in May or June, just stop. Your distributor invoices already reflect the permanent exemption.

A handful of items are not on the permanent list (lanterns, flashlights, candles, portable power banks, non-electric coolers, ice packs, pet supplies) — minor for a trade outfit, but worth knowing if you bundle homeowner storm kits as a thank-you.

Source: Florida Department of Revenue — Disaster Preparedness
