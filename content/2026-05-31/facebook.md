# FB Post — Sunday, May 31, 2026 (T-1 day to hurricane season)

Hurricane season opens **Tuesday**. Four things actually changed for South Florida homeowners this week — most of which are *good* news.

1) **Citizens just filed its first negative rate change since 2018.** -2.6% statewide on average; about 60% of policyholders are slated for around an 11.5% premium decrease at renewal. State Farm filed -10%, Florida Peninsula -8.4%, Patriot Select -11.3%. If your renewal letter lands flat or up, that's now the outlier — worth a written question back to the carrier.

2) **HB 815 takes effect July 1.** Carriers can no longer non-renew you solely because your roof is under 15 years old. If your roof is 15+, you have the right to a professional inspection before a forced replacement. Low-slope roofs can be coated, not replaced, if certified to have 5+ years of life left.

3) **The wind-mitigation form changed April 1.** If your last 1802 inspection was before 2023, a fresh inspection under the new form is the cheapest path to capturing the full credit set — usually $800–$1,500/yr back on a $4,500 premium.

4) **Citizens flood mandate is now live above $400K wind coverage.** Bind early — NFIP has a 30-day waiting period, so a flood policy bound today doesn't cover the first 2-3 weeks of season.
