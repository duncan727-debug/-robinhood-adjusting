Eight Service-Provider Opportunities This Week

1 PA Enforcement Wave — Clean, Licensed PAs Have a Differentiation Moment

The trend: Three enforcement stories hit within weeks: Miramar PA (Chaparro) arrested for allegedly diverting $600K in Hurricane Ian settlements; six people charged in a $1.7M damage claims scheme; CBS News whistleblower investigation into a Florida insurer diverting reserves to investors. DFS is visibly active. SB 266 (effective July 1) tightens PA contract disclosure rules — 18-point font mandates, enhanced cancellation rights for vulnerable adults.

Why it matters: Any service provider who refers a homeowner to a PA now carries reputational risk on that referral. Roofers and attorneys especially have been burned by pairing with sketchy PAs. A new, licensed, clean-record PA has a narrative advantage right now that will fade in 60–90 days.

Categories benefiting: All — but the sharpest reception will be from roofers, water mitigation firms, P&C plaintiff's attorneys, and GCs who have referred to PAs before.

Outreach angle:

"You probably saw the Miramar PA arrest last week. The news just reminded every contractor and attorney in Broward why WHO you refer matters. I'm a new PA, my DFS license is clean, and I'm building a referral bench from scratch. If you've ever had to un-recommend a PA to a client, let's have a 15-minute call."

2 April 7 Flood-Event Claims Entering the Statutory Pipeline NOW

The trend: A Flood Watch hit Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach on April 7 — stalled frontal system, tropical moisture, localized flooding. That was 15 days ago. Under Florida law: insurers must acknowledge claims within 14 days, begin investigation within 10 days of proof of loss, and pay or deny within 90 days. Homeowners who filed in the first week are hitting the acknowledgment/investigation phase this week.

Why it matters: This is the exact window where slow-walked claims, lowball estimates, and improper denials start surfacing — and where a PA can still enter the process cleanly before the 90-day clock matters.

Categories benefiting: Water mitigation (primary), mold remediation, plumbers, GCs, P&C attorneys.

Outreach angle:

"If your crew took dry-out calls in the first or second week of April, some of those homeowners are getting acknowledgment letters right now — and some are getting the runaround. I handle the paperwork side so you don't lose the repair job to a lowball scope."

Time-sensitive. This angle expires roughly 60 days out. Use it now.

3 St. Lucie Rate Shock — Concentrated PA Demand in Port St. Lucie / Fort Pierce

The trend: Citizens has proposed a ~6% rate increase for St. Lucie — the only county in Florida with a proposed increase for 2026 (Miami-Dade −13.9%, Broward −14.1%). The driver is aging housing stock and older roofs.

Why it matters: Homeowners already stretched thin on premiums will fight denials harder. Friction = PA opportunity. Contractors working St. Lucie will see more claim-dependent jobs than their peers in Miami-Dade or Broward.

Categories benefiting: Roofers (massive), GCs, plumbers, water mitigation, P&C attorneys.

Outreach angle:

"St. Lucie's the only county in Florida getting a Citizens rate hike next year. Your customers are about to get squeezed — and the ones with 15+ year old roofs will get squeezed twice. If a claim gets denied on age alone, that's usually a winnable fight. I can take it off your desk."

Geographic priority: Port St. Lucie, Fort Pierce, older coastal subdivisions.

4 HVHZ Roofing Compliance Tightening — Every Inspection Is a Paper Trail

The trend: 2026 Miami-Dade/Broward HVHZ rules now require Secondary Water Resistance applied directly to sheathing and mandatory self-adhering underlayment on all re-roofs. Parallel insurance rules: roofs under 15 years cannot be denied coverage for age alone; roofs over 15 years need inspection, and if 5+ years of remaining life is certified, age-only denial is prohibited.

Why it matters: Roofers are already doing the inspections — their reports are the exact documentation a PA needs to reverse an age-based denial. Most roofers don't realize how valuable their inspection paperwork is downstream.

Categories benefiting: Roofers (primary), GCs doing re-roofs.

Outreach angle:

"Every >15-year roof inspection you do is a document I can use to overturn an insurer's age denial. Most roofers don't know that. I'm trying to build a bench of inspectors whose reports I actually trust. Can I send you a one-pager on what I need to see in the report?"

5 Wind-Driven Rain → Flood Mischaracterization (Coral Springs, Inland Broward)

The trend: Carriers in Coral Springs and inland Broward are actively mischaracterizing wind-driven rain damage as flooding to redirect claims to NFIP (lower sublimits). This is one of the most contested disputes in South Florida right now.

Why it matters: When a claim gets coded flood instead of wind, the homeowner loses sublimit headroom and the contractor loses scope. Both sides are aligned in wanting the wind coding.

Categories benefiting: Water mitigation, roofers, GCs, P&C attorneys.

Outreach angle:

"If you're in Coral Springs or Weston and the carrier coded your last water-damage job as flood instead of wind-driven rain, the homeowner's sublimit just got cut in half — and your scope probably got cut too. That's a fight I pick all day. Got a recent file you want me to look at?"

Lead with a specific file review. Low-commitment first ask.

6 In-Migration Wave — New Transplants Don't Know Florida's Claims Mechanics

The trend: Out-of-state driver's license exchanges in Miami-Dade / Broward / Palm Beach: 19,918 in Q1 2026, up 24% YoY. Top origin states: New York, California, New Jersey. $1M+ closed sales up 22% YoY.

Why it matters: New homeowners from non-hurricane states don't know: windstorm isn't automatically bundled, mold sublimits are often $10K, A/C condensate claims are covered, HO-6 vs master-policy splits. Any service provider who sees these customers first is a natural referral source.

Categories benefiting: A/C contractors (highest first-touch rate), plumbers, roofers, property managers, HOA/condo managers.

Outreach angle:

"You're probably doing install and service calls for people who just bought from New York or Jersey. Half of them don't know Florida policies don't auto-bundle windstorm. I'll give your office a two-page cheat sheet they can hand to new customers — it makes your techs look like experts and gets me a referral when one of those customers eventually has a claim."

Co-branded education materials = soft first move, long tail.

7 Drought + A/C Failures + The 24–48-Hour Mold Window

The trend: Extreme drought across interior/western South Florida, moderate-to-severe across the eastern corridor. Drought accelerates roof and structural deterioration. A/C systems run harder; A/C-failure-caused water intrusion is a recurring covered-loss pattern, but carriers are contesting A/C-caused mold claims more aggressively. Florida law: same firm cannot do both mold assessment and remediation.

Why it matters: A/C techs are the first responders to a covered loss that gets mis-documented as maintenance — a $500 service call that should have been a $15K claim. Water mitigation and mold remediation firms can't cross-bill, so they need a PA-connected partner network.

Categories benefiting: A/C contractors (primary), water mitigation, mold remediation.

Outreach angle:

"Your techs walk into covered losses every week and code them as maintenance. I'd like to spend 20 minutes with your dispatch team on how to spot a claim-eligible call — no cost to you, and every one we flag correctly is a customer who becomes a reference for both of us."

8 Hurricane Season Prep Window (Opens June 1) — 40 Days to Build the Bench

The trend: Atlantic season opens June 1. Forecasts mixed: CSU below-average (13 named, 6 hurricanes, 2 major), U. of Arizona above-average (20 named, 9 hurricanes). "Quiet seasons still deliver one hurricane to whoever is in its path."

Why it matters: Every roofer, water mitigation firm, and A/C contractor knows June–November is overwhelm season. The PAs they meet before the phones start ringing get the referral; the ones they meet during are a distraction. The next 40 days are the optimal recruiting window.

Categories benefiting: All storm-exposed trades — roofers, water mitigation, mold remediation, A/C, GCs.

Outreach angle:

"Season opens in 40 days. I'd rather have you know me now than during. 20 minutes, no pitch — just so when the first named storm lands and a customer asks you 'who should I call,' I'm a name you can give without hesitation."
