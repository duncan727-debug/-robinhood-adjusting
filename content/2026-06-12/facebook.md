# Facebook Draft — June 12, 2026

A Palm Beach Gardens bonding agent was just arrested for allegedly collecting over $90,000 from a roofing company and a construction firm for surety bonds — and never sending a dime to the surety carrier. Those contractors thought they were bonded. They weren't. (Source: Insurance Journal / Florida DFS)

Here's the part that keeps me up at night as someone who works claims for a living: "I paid for it" and "coverage exists" are two completely different things. If one of those bonded jobs had gone sideways while the contractor was unknowingly unbonded, the entire exposure lands on the contractor — not the agent who pocketed the premium. And her license still showed valid this week. The tell was buried in the carrier appointments.

The 15-minute fix for any contractor reading this: pull every active bond you carry and call the surety company directly — not your agent — to confirm the bond is in force and the premium was actually received. Then run your agent through the DFS license search (licenseesearch.fldfs.com) and check their active carrier appointments, not just the license status. Going forward, ask the carrier itself for confirmation on any new bond.

Same logic applies to your insurance certificates, by the way. Verification is boring right up until the day it saves your business.

When's the last time you verified a bond or COI directly with the carrier instead of taking the paperwork at face value? Honest answers welcome. 👇
