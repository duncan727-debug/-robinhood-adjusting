# Wall Street South Is Now a Backlog: 1.6M Sq Ft of Office Construction in WPB–Boca and What It Means for Service Providers

For three years, "Wall Street South" has been a story. Hedge funds announcing satellite offices. Private equity firms taking out short-term leases on Worth Avenue. Family offices renting out floors in downtown West Palm Beach while they figured out whether the move was permanent.

This week, the story turned into a number: **the combined West Palm Beach–Boca Raton metro now ranks fifth in the United States for office space currently under construction**, with **nearly 1.6 million square feet** in the active development pipeline.

That's not speculation. That's contracted construction — buildings with financing in place, GCs selected, and shells going up over the next 24 months.

If you run a service-trade business in Palm Beach County, this is the single most important commercial development of the year for your firm. Here's why, and what to do about it.

## What Changed and Why It Matters

The driver behind the pipeline is now well-documented: the post-2020 migration of financial-sector firms to Florida, accelerated by the no-state-income-tax advantage and a wave of high-earner talent that followed. What was anecdotal in 2021 — "I heard Citadel is opening an office here" — is now a measurable absorption story backed by class-A office construction at a scale that puts WPB–Boca alongside markets like Austin and Nashville.

For real estate brokers, the headline is leasing velocity. For service providers, the headline is something different: **a multi-year revenue band of trade work that hasn't been bid out yet.**

A 1.6 million square foot construction pipeline doesn't just need steel and concrete. It needs:

- Roofing and waterproofing
- Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP)
- Life safety and fire protection
- Glazing, finishes, and millwork
- Site work, landscaping, and exterior hardscape
- Tenant improvement (TI) build-outs at every fit-out cycle

The shell-and-core work is largely awarded by the time a project is publicly under construction. But the **tenant build-out window** — where every new financial-services tenant builds their own custom space — is where the recurring revenue lives. And those vendor relationships are being built right now.

## The 24-Month Window Is Not Evenly Distributed

Here's the part most subcontractors miss. A construction pipeline this size doesn't open evenly. It opens in stages:

1. **Pre-construction and shell (now to ~12 months out):** Awards already made. If you're not on these jobs, you're not getting on these jobs.
2. **Topping off and exterior close-in (~6–18 months out):** GCs begin building out their TI subcontractor benches. This is your window.
3. **Tenant fit-out (~12–36 months out):** Where the real volume of HVAC, electrical, finishes, life-safety, and specialty trade work happens — and where pre-qualified subs eat first.
4. **Building turnover and recurring service (~24 months out and beyond):** Maintenance, service contracts, and warranty work go to firms with prior project relationships.

If your shop isn't on the approved-vendor list before stage 3 starts, you're competing against firms that already have project history, badge access, and a relationship with the on-site superintendent. That's a hard fight to win on price alone.

## What to Do This Quarter

If you operate a service-trade business in Palm Beach County and you want a piece of this pipeline, the work is straightforward but it has to happen now.

### 1. Identify the top three projects in your service radius

The pipeline is concentrated in downtown West Palm Beach, the Rosemary Square corridor, and the Boca Raton I-95 office submarket. Public permit records and commercial real estate trackers list the active developments. Pick the three closest to your shop with the largest gross square footage and start there.

### 2. Find the GC, not the developer

Developers commission buildings; general contractors run the trades. The names you want are on the construction signage, the permit history, and the city's active construction inspection records. Once you have the GC, find the project executive and the subcontractor pre-qualification contact.

### 3. Get pre-qualified — formally

This is not a "send an introduction email" step. Most large commercial GCs run formal pre-qualification packages: insurance certificates, bonding capacity, OSHA logs, project history, financial statements, safety program documentation. The package takes a week to assemble the first time. After that, you re-use it.

If your firm has never been formally pre-qualified by a large commercial GC, that's the gating activity. Until that package is filed, you're not in the running for the build-out work.

### 4. Walk the site once shells are visible

Once a building is topping off, there is value in being physically present — meeting the superintendent, walking the trailer, putting a face to the firm name. GCs award TI work to subs they recognize. Anonymous bid packages lose to relationships.

### 5. Watch pre-leasing percentages

Pre-leasing is the leading indicator of when tenant fit-out demand hits. Projects with 60%+ pre-leasing at shell completion will start TI work fast. Projects under 30% pre-leasing may stall before fit-out — and that's a signal to focus your bench-building energy elsewhere.

## A Note for Real Estate Professionals

If you broker commercial space in PBC, the class-A office absorption story is durable for at least the next 24 months. The construction pipeline is contracted demand, not speculation. Commercial brokers should be tracking pre-leasing percentages on the top three projects — that's the leading indicator for whether the pipeline holds or wobbles.

If you broker residential, the relevance is indirect but real: the financial-services jobs landing in those towers are the buyers driving the high end of the Palm Beach and Boca residential market. Knowing which firms are taking which floors is a buyer-pipeline signal worth tracking.

## The Bigger Picture

The reason the Wall Street South pipeline matters beyond construction is that it's a **demand anchor for every adjacent service business** in Palm Beach County. New office tenants need:

- Property management and janitorial services
- Security and access control
- IT and telecom infrastructure
- Catering, food service, and corporate hospitality
- Legal, accounting, and professional services
- Insurance — both commercial lines for the buildings and personal lines for the executives relocating

Every one of those categories has a Palm Beach County operator who should be positioning for the next 24 months. The construction trades are first in line because their work is most concentrated in the build phase, but they are not the only category that benefits.

## Bottom Line

A 1.6 million square foot pipeline doesn't show up in a market every year. When it does, the firms that move on it early — formally, professionally, with the paperwork to back the introduction — are the ones that capture the recurring revenue band on the back end.

The subs already on these projects' approved-vendor lists are set up for 2027 and 2028. The subs that wait six months will be bidding into a closed bench.
