# The South Florida Contractor's Pipeline Just Split in Two: SIRS Deadlines and the July 1 Roof Rule

**Meta description:** Florida's SIRS deadline and the July 1 HB 815 roof certification pathway are reshaping the 2026–2027 contractor pipeline in PBC, Broward, and Miami-Dade. Here's what owner-operators should be doing in the next 60 days.

If you run a roofing crew, restoration GC, concrete specialty, or trades operation in South Florida, two regulatory clocks are doing more to reshape your next 12 months of revenue than anything you'll see on the news this week.

The first is **December 31, 2026** — the SIRS (Structural Integrity Reserve Study) outer-pairing deadline that's pulling deferred condo work forward. The second is **July 1, 2026** — when HB 815's certified-inspector pathway changes the residential roof replacement decision for tens of thousands of South Florida homeowners.

Both are job-category signals. Owner-operators who position now will own the next two quarters of pipeline. Those who don't will be bidding into the leftover work at compressed margins.

## SIRS Is Already Reshaping Coastal Condo Spend

The Structural Integrity Reserve Study requirements that flowed out of Florida's post-Surfside building safety reforms have been on the books for a while. What's changed in 2026 is the **enforcement clock**. Older mid-rise buildings — particularly anything built before 1996 — across coastal Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach County have been kicking scope decisions down the road. They can't anymore.

Industry analyses of post-Surfside SIRS outcomes are showing special assessments landing in the **$134,000 to $400,000 per unit** range. That's not a small repair budget. That's a multi-trade reconstruction program, and the dollars are flowing to a predictable set of categories:

- **Concrete restoration.** Spalling repair, post-tension cable replacement, balcony reconstruction. This is the single largest scope category on most SIRS-driven projects.
- **Waterproofing and coatings.** Above-grade and below-grade systems specified by the engineer of record. Specs usually require NACE Level II inspection or equivalent — if you don't have that credential on staff, build the subcontractor relationship now.
- **Window and door replacement.** The envelope is already open. In Miami-Dade and Broward, the HVHZ 30% rule frequently drags the entire opening package into current-code compliance.
- **Restoration GCs.** Boards want one number, one schedule, one accountable contractor. Multi-trade coordination is the bottleneck and the highest-margin position.

### How to Position into SIRS Work in the Next 60 Days

Three concrete moves:

**1. Build a building list, not a contact list.** Pull condo-association mailing addresses from the PBC, Broward, and Miami-Dade clerks (each county publishes association registries). Segment by year built. Start with everything pre-1996, coastal first.

**2. Time outreach to board meeting cycles.** Most condo boards meet monthly, often the second Tuesday or Wednesday. Their meeting schedules are usually in the association docs. Show up — or call — the week before. That's when the engineer's report is on the agenda and the board is actively looking for help interpreting it.

**3. Lead with the engineer's report, not your services.** Every restoration GC in South Florida is pitching condo boards right now. What boards actually need is help reading a 60-page engineer-of-record letter and translating it into a phased scope of work they can defend to owners. Be the firm that does that, and your bid is the reference bid against which everything else gets compared.

## The Residential Roof Pipeline Splits on July 1

While SIRS is reshaping commercial multifamily, HB 815 is rewriting the residential roof story.

For years, older asphalt-shingle roofs in the 15-to-25-year band have been the forced-replacement category on insurance renewal. Carriers used a hard age cap on the roof and the homeowner either replaced or lost coverage. Starting July 1, 2026, a **licensed inspector can certify those roofs for extension** based on condition, not age.

The practical effect on residential roofing pipeline is a clean bifurcation:

- **Inspection-and-repair becomes a product.** A meaningful share of homeowners who were resigned to a full replacement are going to opt for inspection plus targeted repair. That's a new $350–$550 product line for any roofer who positions for it.
- **Re-roof market shifts toward upgrade.** Homeowners who do replace are increasingly choosing standing-seam metal or tile — partly because the OIR-B1-1802 wind mitigation form (in effect since April 1, 2026) credits stronger roof systems more generously.

### What to Do Before July 1

**Add a certified-inspection product to your menu.** Price it cleanly — $350–$550 is the typical band. Market it side-by-side with your replacement quotes. The inspection is the homeowner's "second opinion" before they accept a carrier non-renewal letter, and it's the cheapest way to stay in front of customers who would otherwise have left your funnel.

**Update your material-escalator clauses.** Roofing materials remain 15–25% higher than 2024 baselines. Section 232 tariffs on steel and aluminum (50% rate, covering 400-plus derivative categories including drip edge, flashing, and prefab panels) are not letting up. Aluminum mill shapes are up 33% year-over-year; steel mill products are up 20.7%. If you wrote contracts in early 2026 that lock fixed material pricing through August, update your template language before your next signing.

**Find a wind-mit inspector partner.** The OIR-B1-1802 premium-relief number is the homeowner's primary motivation to call a roofer. If you can't deliver the form alongside the work, you're leaving the close on the table.

## Don't Miss the August 31 CE Deadline

Florida construction-trades license renewals require continuing-education completion by **midnight EST on August 31, 2026**. Roofing, HVAC, plumbing, and general-contractor licenses are all covered. The hour requirements and topic mix depend on license type.

This is the kind of thing every owner-operator says they'll handle in August. Block 4 hours next week. A lapsed license means you're uninsured on the next job — a single liability claim during the lapse lands on your personal balance sheet.
