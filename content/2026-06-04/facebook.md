# Facebook Post — June 4, 2026

If you do concrete, envelope, waterproofing, or roofing work in South Florida, the next 210 days are going to define your 2027 book — and most of the buildings driving it have already started writing the checks.

The SIRS (Structural Integrity Reserve Study) deadline is December 31, 2026. Older coastal mid-rises in PBC, Broward, and Miami-Dade are landing special assessments in the $134K–$400K-per-unit range, and the bulk of that spend is concrete, post-tension cable, balcony, waterproofing, and envelope. Boards aren't kicking these decisions down the road anymore — they can't. The buildings I'd be calling first are the ones built before 1996.

Here's the part most contractors miss: condo boards are drowning in unsolicited GC pitches. What they actually need is help reading the 60-page engineer-of-record letter that just landed on their table. Lead with the report, not your services, and time the call the week before their next board meeting (most meet the second Tuesday or Wednesday).
