**211 days. That's the clock running on every roofer, window installer, and GC in South Florida right now.**

The Florida Building Code 9th Edition takes effect December 31, 2026. Permits pulled before that date are locked into 8th Edition rules through completion. Permits pulled after fall under the new code — updated wind-load tables, sealed-roof-deck refinements, and revised energy provisions.

For Miami-Dade and Broward contractors, the HVHZ angle is bigger than people are saying out loud. A 9th Edition roof replacement on an older HVHZ home can pull windows, doors, and attachments into current-code compliance under the 30% rule. That changes a $25K roof into a $60K envelope job — or kills the bid entirely if the homeowner wasn't ready for it.

Two things I'm telling every service provider I talk to this week:
1. If you've got a discretionary project (roofs, additions, large remodels), there's a real pull-forward window between now and late December.
2. Material orders placed before Dec 31 that ship after may end up out of spec. Ask your distributor for a written code-effective-date plan now, not in November when everyone is asking.

What's your team's plan for the transition — front-load the pipeline before December, or wait and reprice everything in Q1? Curious which way folks are leaning.
