# Facebook Post

The public adjuster profession took a hit this week—and it's a reminder of something every service provider needs to know.

A Florida public adjuster was arrested for stealing over $700K in Hurricane Ian claim funds from homeowners and elderly clients. It's one of the worst-case scenarios in a profession built entirely on trust. When someone handles your clients' claims, vets your partnerships, or coordinates recovery work, you're making a bet that they're honest.

Here's what matters for you as a contractor, plumber, roofer, or property manager: **how you vet the professionals you work with directly impacts your reputation and your paycheck.** A fraudulent adjuster creates a tainted project, damages client relationships, and can turn a profitable claim recovery into a legal mess.

The good news? Florida's insurance market is in the strongest position it's been in years. Litigation is down 35% since 2023, insurers are cutting rates, and new carriers are flooding the market—which means more claims, more recovery work, and more opportunity. But that opportunity only pays off if you're partnering with adjusters and service providers you can actually trust.
