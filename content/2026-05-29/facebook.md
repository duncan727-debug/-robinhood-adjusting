# Facebook Draft — Friday, May 29, 2026

Roofers in Palm Beach County — there's a quiet update in HB 715 that just expanded your scope of work, and most crews I've talked to this week don't know about it yet.

As of last May, the statutory definition of "roofing contractor" now includes **evaluating and enhancing roof-to-wall connections on wood-decked roofs**. In plain English: those strap retrofits that drive wind-mit credits on the new OIR-B1-1802 form? You can pull them inside your scope. No structural sub, no punting the line item to a GC. One truck-roll, three line items: re-roof estimate + roof-to-wall straps + wind-mit re-inspection. The homeowner walks into renewal season with a credit-improving form, the carrier drops their premium, and guess who they call the next time anything happens up there.

The bigger pipeline play sitting underneath all this is My Safe Florida Home — still writing checks, $10K per home for impact windows, doors, roof upgrades, and shutters, and **the state pays the contractor directly through the vendor portal**. No floating cash, no chasing invoices. But the vendor approval list has a wait, and hurricane season opens Monday — if your crew isn't on it yet, this week is the week to start the application.

Which one of these are you already doing — and which one is news? Drop a comment, happy to share what I'm seeing across the county.
