# Blog Draft — Friday, May 29, 2026

**Title:** HB 715 Just Expanded Your Roofing Scope — Here's the Cross-Sell Most South Florida Crews Are Missing

**Meta description:** Florida HB 715 brought roof-to-wall strap retrofits into the statutory roofing-contractor scope. Here's how Palm Beach County crews can turn it into a three-line-item cross-sell — and pair it with My Safe Florida Home before hurricane season opens.

---

## A quiet statute change with a loud pipeline effect

If you run a roofing crew in Palm Beach, Broward, or Martin County and you haven't read the CILB scope memo flowing from **HB 715**, this is the post to read before Monday — because hurricane season opens June 1, your phones will start ringing differently, and there's a line item you can now legally pull onto every estimate that you couldn't a year ago.

Gov. DeSantis signed HB 715 on **May 19, 2025**, and the bill revised the statutory definition of "roofing contractor" to include the **evaluation and enhancement of roof-to-wall connections on structures with wood roof decking**.

In plain English: roof-to-wall strap retrofits — the very work that drives the wind-mitigation credits on the brand-new OIR-B1-1802 form — are now squarely inside your roofing license. You don't need a structural sub. You don't need to coordinate a GC. You don't need to punt the line item or eat the margin.

If you were already including strap work informally, congratulations — you're now on solid statutory ground. If you weren't, this is the cross-sell of the season.

## What the cross-sell actually looks like

Here's the play, in the order I'd run it on a Friday quote:

### Line item 1 — The re-roof estimate

This is what got you in the door. Insurance call, hail event, age-of-roof denial, whatever the trigger was. Standard quote.

### Line item 2 — Roof-to-wall strap retrofit

While the deck is exposed, this is the moment. The labor differential is small compared to going back in 18 months as a stand-alone job. Truss-to-top-plate connectors, hurricane clips, the works — to the spec the new OIR-B1-1802 form rewards.

### Line item 3 — Wind-mitigation re-inspection

This is the line item the homeowner doesn't know to ask for. When you complete the re-roof and the strap work, schedule a licensed inspector to file an updated wind-mit form with the carrier. **That form is what triggers the premium credit at renewal.** Without it, the work you just did doesn't show up on the insurance side.

One truck-roll. Three line items. One credit-improving outcome the homeowner can hand to their carrier. And — here's the part most operators miss — **that's the call-back that wins next year's referral.** When the renewal comes back lower because of the form you helped them file, you're the person they tell their neighbor about.

## Why this matters more in 2026 than it did in 2025

Two things are stacking on top of HB 715 that make this play even sharper this season:

**1. The OIR-B1-1802 form was updated.** The new form rewards correctly-installed roof-to-wall connections more granularly than the old version. A homeowner with single-wraps gets more credit than nails, and a homeowner with double-wraps gets more credit than single. If your shop knows the form, you can quote the upgrade with the credit attached.

**2. Citizens depopulation is still moving.** **480,000 policies remain** on the Citizens book, with seven private takeouts approved for 2026. Every accepted takeout means a new agent of record, a new carrier, and a new underwriting review. Homeowners with a fresh wind-mit form in hand survive that transition with their rate intact. Homeowners without one don't.

## Don't sleep on My Safe Florida Home

The other piece of the pipeline I'd put in front of your sales lead this week: **My Safe Florida Home is still writing checks**, and a proposed FY 2026-27 budget request north of **$600 million** is on the table for the next cycle.

The grant program pays up to **$10,000 per home** for impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, **roof upgrades**, and wind-rated garage doors. Two facts that matter to a trade operator:

- **The grant pays the contractor directly.** The state's vendor-payment portal is currently the cleanest receivables channel available to a residential trade in Florida. The homeowner doesn't float the cash. You don't chase the invoice.
- **Vendor approval has a wait.** Credentials, certs, insurance, references — start the application this week. Crews not on the approved vendor list cannot book this work, period.

Your target customer for canvassing: at or below **120% of county median income**, **homesteaded**, home **built before Jan 1, 2008**, insured for **$700K or less**. That maps cleanly to older Wellington, Royal Palm Beach, and Greenacres single-family — primary residence, working couple, tile or shingle roof. Door-knock script writes itself.

## The CE deadline nobody is talking about

While you're optimizing the cross-sell, don't lose your license over it.

Certified state licenses (roofing, building, residential, mechanical, plumbing) expire on **August 31 of every even year**. That is **August 31, 2026 — thirteen weeks from today**. The biennial CE requirement:

- 1 hour workplace safety
- 1 hour workers' compensation
- 1 hour business practices
- 11 hours trade-specific or general construction
- **14 hours total**

Approved providers — Gold Coast, 1 Exam Prep, AGC, FRSA, FRCBA — all run online courses you can finish across two weekends. If you have a foreman or journeyman who holds a separate cert, this applies to them too. Don't be the shop that gets to September and discovers a key person can't pull a permit.
