# 5 Storm-Prep Vendors PBC Homeowners Met at the 2026 Wellington Hurricane Expo

**By Robin Hood Adjusting · Wellington, FL · 12 days to hurricane season**

The Village of Wellington's annual Hurricane & Severe Weather Expo brought thousands of Palm Beach County homeowners face-to-face with the local businesses that handle storm prep, hardening, and recovery. We pulled five exhibitors worth knowing about before June 1 — not because we're paid to mention them (we're not — no referral fees, no sponsorships), but because PBC homeowners ask us about exactly these categories every week.

---

## 1. Dura Guard Roofing

**What they do:** Residential roofing and storm-damage replacements across PBC.
**Why pre-season matters:** Roofs over 12 years old are the single biggest claim-denial trigger in Florida. Getting an inspection now — before a storm — flips the conversation from "is this storm-related" to "we documented this in May."
**Contact:** Brandi Milbert · brandi@duraguardroof.com

## 2. Solace Roofing

**What they do:** Roofing installs and storm-damage repair, PBC-focused.
**Why pre-season matters:** Roofers are already booking crews through July. Pre-season jobs get scheduled; June 2 emergency calls land in a queue.
**Contact:** events@solaceroofing.com

## 3. Florida Window and Door

**What they do:** Impact windows and hurricane-rated doors.
**Why pre-season matters:** Impact-rated openings don't just protect the structure — they unlock wind-mitigation insurance discounts (often 20-45% off the wind portion of your premium). The inspection paperwork to claim those discounts has to be done *before* a storm, not after.
**Contact:** Lisa Wray · lisaw@floridawindowanddoor.com

## 4. Max Guard Hurricane Windows

**What they do:** Hurricane-rated window manufacture and installation.
**Why pre-season matters:** Same wind-mitigation logic as above — and capacity is the constraint. Anyone calling in late May for a June install is going to hear about a backlog.
**Contact:** Ni Xue · ni@maxguardhurricane.com

## 5. Florida HomeCheck Solutions

**What they do:** Home inspections, wind mitigation inspections, 4-point reports.
**Why pre-season matters:** A current wind-mitigation inspection is the document your carrier needs to honor wind-mitigation discounts on your policy. If yours is more than 5 years old, it may not reflect your current openings or roof — meaning you're paying full freight for protection your home already has.
**Contact:** Marshall Miller · durnik00@yahoo.com
