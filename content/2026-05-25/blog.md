# Wall Street South Just Put Palm Beach #5 in the Country for Office Construction

If you run a trade business in Palm Beach County — mechanical, electrical, plumbing, finishes, life safety, glazing, fire protection, low-voltage, demolition, site work — the number you need to know this Memorial Day is **1.6 million square feet**.

That's the active office construction pipeline in the combined West Palm Beach–Boca Raton metro right now. It puts our market at **#5 in the entire United States** for office space under construction. Not #5 in the Southeast. #5 in the country, ahead of most of the legacy financial hubs.

And unlike the 2021–2022 speculative cycle, this one has signed leases and capital partners behind it.

## What's actually driving it

The headline driver is the "Wall Street South" relocation wave that's been building for five years now. Hedge funds, private equity firms, family offices, and major financial institutions are opening satellite headquarters or moving substantial operations from New York, Chicago, and Boston to South Florida.

Two structural reasons it isn't slowing down:

1. **No state income tax.** A senior partner relocating from a 6.85%-tax state to Florida picks up six figures of after-tax income per year, every year, for the rest of their working life. That math compounds. For firm-wide moves it compounds across hundreds of professionals.
2. **Talent has already moved.** The 2020–2022 personal-relocation wave seeded the labor pool. Firms can now staff a Palm Beach office without paying full New York relocation packages, because the analysts and associates already live here.

The buildings going up today are catching up to demand that's been pent up for three years.

## Why this matters more for subs than for the GCs

The general contractors running these projects already won the work. The interesting opportunity in the next 12–24 months is on the **subcontractor and specialty trade** side.

Here's the sequence:

- **Months 0–12: shell construction.** Site work, foundations, structural steel/concrete, exterior envelope. Most of these scopes are already awarded.
- **Months 12–24: tenant build-out.** This is the long tail — MEP rough-in and finish, interior framing, drywall, ceilings, flooring, millwork, glazing, fire/life safety, A/V and low-voltage, security, commissioning, punch-list trades.

The build-out phase is where the work fragments across dozens of trades, on schedules driven by tenant move-in dates. That's where firms that aren't already locked into the project can still get on.

But here's the constraint: **GCs do not add new subs onto a hot project mid-stream unless they have to.** Approved-vendor lists are built early — usually during the shell phase — and they're stable.

If you want build-out work on these towers in 2027, you need to be on the list in 2026.

## What to do this quarter

Three concrete moves:

### 1. Identify the projects

The 1.6M sq ft figure is spread across a manageable number of towers. Start with the three or four largest active developments in West Palm Beach proper and the Boca Raton CBD. Public permitting records, the city's planning and zoning agendas, and the local commercial brokerages all publish enough information to identify the GC of record on each.

### 2. Get on the qualification list

GC pre-qualification packages have become more rigorous since 2022 — bonding capacity, EMR, financials, OSHA history, references on comparable work. If your pre-qual packet is the same one you submitted three years ago, refresh it before you send. Most GCs running class-A office work want to see at least one comparable project of similar size and finish level.

If you don't have a comparable project on your résumé, this is the moment to find a partnership or joint-venture path with a sub that does. Subs at this tier are willing to bring up smaller firms specifically because the labor demand is going to outrun their own bench.

### 3. Build the relationship before the bid

The trades that get called for build-out work on these projects are almost always trades the project executive or senior super already knows. Cold pre-qual is table stakes. Warm relationships are how you actually get the call sheet.

Memorial Day weekend is a slow news week — but the week after a holiday is when project teams clear backlog, return calls, and take outreach meetings. Tuesday and Wednesday next week are unusually good days to make first contact.

## The insurance and claims angle (briefly)

Build-out projects produce claim activity. Water intrusion through a partial envelope, storm damage to a tenant's stored FF&E, contractor-to-contractor scope disputes, owner-against-GC delay claims that ripple down to subs. When something goes sideways mid-build-out, the sub usually isn't the first call to the insurance carrier — but they're often the party whose work is impacted.

A licensed public adjuster represents the policyholder's side of the claim — the building owner, the tenant, or the sub themselves if they're the named insured on the damaged property. If you're working on these projects and want to know who in your insurance chain is on whose side, that's worth a 15-minute conversation.

## The broader signal

The 1.6M sq ft pipeline isn't a one-off. Two other items from this week's brief point the same direction:

- **State Farm filed a 10% statewide rate decrease for Florida homeowners** — along with Florida Peninsula (−8.4%) and Patriot Select (−11.3%). When the largest single carrier in the state cuts pricing 10%, it's because their loss experience and reinsurance costs are improving. Stable insurance markets correlate with stable commercial development capital.
- **Florida's 1% emergency insurance surcharge ends Oct 1, 2026, two years ahead of schedule.** The state estimates $650M in consumer savings over the following two years.

These are not coincidences. The reinsurance, regulatory, and capital signals are all moving in the same direction at the same time, and the Wall Street South office pipeline is one of the visible outputs.

If you've been waiting for confirmation that 2027–2028 is going to be a strong commercial build year in Palm Beach County, this is it.
