One-line summary. Money this summer: Publix bagger or country club caddy. Long-term play: send one short email to Bespoke Capital Partners this weekend and try to land a 20-minute coffee chat.

Part 1 — Jobs that will hire you at 15

FL law: 14+ can work, no permit needed since 2022. Summer (school out): up to 8 hrs/day, 40 hrs/week, between 7am and 7pm. Bring a valid ID showing date of birth.

A. Publix — bagger (highest probability yes)

  • Confirmed Publix hires at 14+ for courtesy clerk/bagger roles.
  • Find the closest Wellington store at publix.com store locator. (verify exact address before walking in)
  • Walk in between 2-3pm (between lunch and dinner rushes).
  • Ask for the store manager. Don't apply online first — in-person beats it.

Script:

"Hi, I'm 15 and looking for a bagger position.
 I have my ID. Is there a manager I can talk to?"

B. Country club caddy (highest hourly pay)

  • A loop = roughly $60-$120 for 4-5 hrs (tips-driven, varies by club).
  • Show up Saturday morning at 6:30-7am at the pro shop.
  • Three Wellington-area clubs to try, in order of easiest door: Binks Forest (public), Wanderers Club, Wycliffe. (call ahead to confirm they run a caddie program — programs come and go)
  • Ask for the Caddie Master or Head Golf Pro.

Script:

"Hi, I'm 15, I'm looking for a caddy spot. My dad will sign
 whatever you need. Can I shadow once to learn the bag?"

C. Restaurants — bussing / dish / host

  • Legal at 14+ in FL for non-cooking back-of-house and host roles.
  • Walk in 2:30-4:30pm when the lunch rush is over.
  • Independent restaurants are friendlier than chains. Subway, Tijuana Flats, Chick-fil-A, Panera are reasonable chain starting points.
  • Ask for the manager on duty — name on the schedule by the host stand if you can sneak a look.

D. Self-employed work (no child-labor rules apply when you're the boss)

  • Mowing, dog-walking (Rover/Wag — Dad's account needed since under 18), tutoring elementary math, car detailing.
  • The pressure-washing venture Dad has been planning is the version of this with the most upside.

Skip — these require 18

  • Village of Wellington Parks & Rec summer camp counselor (HS diploma + 18 required)
  • Most lifeguard positions at Wellington pools

Who to ask for

  • Target an Associate or Vice President on LinkedIn. They're 25-30 and remember being you.
  • Do not email founders/partners first — too busy, won't reply.
  • If only founders are visible publicly, message the firm's general info address with the email below — sometimes they forward it down.

Email template (Bespoke version)

SUBJECT: 15-year-old in Wellington — 20 minutes to ask how you ended up in PE?

Hi [first name],

I'm Mathias Littlejohn, a 15-year-old in Wellington. I came across
Bespoke Capital Partners when I was researching private equity firms
close to home, and I'm trying to understand what the work actually
looks like day-to-day before deciding whether to chase it through
high school and college.

I'm not asking for a job — I know I'm years too early for that.
I'd love 15 or 20 minutes of your time, in person at your office
or on a call, to ask a few questions like:

  - What does an associate's actual day look like?
  - What classes, books, or activities did you find most useful
    before college?
  - Does Bespoke ever take on high-schoolers for a shadow day,
    even a single one?

I'll work around whatever schedule you have. I appreciate your
time either way.

Thank you,
Mathias Littlejohn
[phone number]

For a second firm, change line one ("I came across [Firm Name]…") and tailor the third bullet to something from their portfolio. Don't blast all four firms — pick two.

Five questions to bring (write them in a notebook, real pen)

  1. Walk me through yesterday — what did you actually do hour by hour?
  2. What's the part of the job nobody talks about that surprised you?
  3. If you were 15 again, what's the one thing you'd start doing this summer?
  4. How did you decide on your college and major?
  5. Is there one person you think I should meet next?

The single highest-leverage move

Handwritten thank-you note, mailed the next day. Not email. A real card with a stamp. Almost nobody under 30 does this — it lives on their desk for a week and they remember you for years.
