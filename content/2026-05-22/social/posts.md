## FACEBOOK — Post 3 (Pre-storm prep — documentation)

**10 days until hurricane season. The single most valuable thing you can do this weekend: document your home BEFORE a storm hits.**

After a loss, the carrier will ask one question over and over: *"Was this damage already there?"* Pre-loss photos are how you answer that question without an argument.

Here's what to capture this weekend (~30 minutes with your phone):

1. **Every exterior wall** — full elevation, then close-ups of any existing cracks, stains, or wear
2. **Roof from the ground** — all four sides, with any flashing/vent details visible
3. **Inside every room** — wide shot showing ceiling, walls, floor; close-ups of ceilings under any prior leak repairs
4. **Big-ticket items in place** — TVs, appliances, A/C unit, water heater, furniture you'd want replaced at full value
5. **Open every closet, cabinet, drawer** — quick pan-video is fine
6. **Garage and storage areas** — tools, equipment, anything stored

Save the entire library to **cloud backup** (iCloud, Google Photos, Dropbox). The phone in your hand after a hurricane may not be the phone in your hand right now.

This is the single biggest difference I see between claims that pay clean and claims that drag on for months. The work takes 30 minutes. The value if you ever need it is measured in tens of thousands of dollars.
