Quick one for South Florida homeowners renewing this summer. A wind mitigation inspection is the biggest discount most policies can access — often 15 to 45% off the wind premium. But timing matters: get it done 60 to 90 days BEFORE your renewal date, not after. Once your renewal notice arrives, the rate is already locked. The inspection only flows into the next cycle. FB · Post 2 Service provider Suggested slot: early afternoon
RA
Robinhood Adjusting
Wellington, FL · Public
For South Florida roofers and restoration crews working on commercial portfolios — one tip on locking in priority-response season agreements without the back-and-forth on rate. Property managers expect to negotiate price. What they actually need is the SLA. Lead with the response window, not the rate. Template language that tends to work: "Priority response: crew on-site within 12 business hours of activation call. Dry-in within 48 hours. Tarp through standard adjuster cycle. Standard pricing applies. Allocation: portfolio receives queue priority over non-contracted work." That's it. No discount language. The value being sold is the slot, not the price. Two things crews commonly miss: (1) define "activation call" so weekend and holiday timing is unambiguous, (2) cap each portfolio at a specific square footage so you can sign multiple property managers without overcommitting capacity. If you can't service everyone you signed, your SLA reputation is the thing that breaks first.
FB · Post 3 Real estate Suggested slot: late afternoon
RA
Robinhood Adjusting
Wellington, FL · Public
For South Florida agents writing listing agreements this summer — a small tweak that's saved a few clients from post-close grief. When a seller has had a prior insurance claim (even a paid-and-closed one from 5+ years back), it shows up on the buyer's CLUE report at underwriting. Buyer's lender pulls it, buyer's premium quote spikes, deal nearly falls apart at 11pm two nights before close. Happens more than you'd think. The fix: ask the seller upfront for their CLUE report (free from LexisNexis, 7 to 10 day turnaround) and disclose any prior losses in the listing remarks. Buyer's agent prices the higher premium quote in from day one. No surprise underwriting calls at the worst possible moment. Costs nothing, fixes a pattern. Most sellers don't even know CLUE reports exist until something goes sideways with the file.

5-minute habit that saves a claim: Every spring, walk to the curb, point your phone at your roof from the same four spots, and shoot. That's it. That's the whole tip. Two years later when a storm pulls eight shingles and the adjuster says "this looks like wear and tear" — you've got dated photos showing the roof was clean and tight in 2024, in 2025, in 2026. IG · Post 2 Homeowner Suggested slot: late morning

Caption

Four things most South Florida hurricane prep lists skip: 1. Test your water shutoff valve in May. If it's been 3 years since anyone turned it, it's probably seized. You don't want to learn that at 2am during a leak. 2. PDF your declarations page to your phone. Not the whole policy — just the dec page. That's the document your adjuster, your contractor, and your mortgage company all ask for first. 3. Photograph each room with the doors open. Two angles. Then do the garage. Time-stamped, geo-tagged, sitting in your camera roll. That's your contents inventory if anything ever goes sideways. 4. Know where your meter is. Power and water. If a tree pulls the line, the first responder's question is "where's the cutoff" — not "where do we find it." Shutters are the visible 5%. These four are the boring 95%. #HurricaneSeason2026 #SouthFlorida #PalmBeachCounty #HomeownerTips
