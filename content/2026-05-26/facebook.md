**The Tuesday-after-Memorial-Day wave is here.**

If you ran a 24-hour line over the holiday weekend, you already know — localized 2–4" rainfall on Sunday and Monday across South Florida, training cells that sat over the same neighborhoods for hours. That's the classic setup, and the call volume always lands the next morning. Window seals weeping, soffit drips that didn't show up until the homeowner walked back in from the BBQ, slab moisture that took 36 hours to show on a baseboard.

Here's the part most contractors don't think about: starting January 1, 2027, *every* Citizens wind policy in Florida — regardless of home value, regardless of flood zone — must carry a flood policy or the wind coverage non-renews. That's 395,000 South Florida homes coming into mandatory flood coverage in the next 19 months. A huge slice of inland Palm Beach County losses that used to come out of the homeowner's pocket are about to be insured events. That changes who pays your invoice.

For roofers, plumbers, mitigation crews, and restoration contractors — same-day response on this week's surge calls is what wins the next three months of work, not just this job. And the same homeowner who calls you Tuesday is the one who's going to need a flood-aware referral partner six months from now.

Who's your insurance-side relationship when one of those claims gets denied or underpaid? If the answer is "I don't have one yet," let's talk.
