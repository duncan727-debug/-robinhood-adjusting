Lead Story

High-Profile Public Adjuster Fraud Case Underscores Compliance Risk

Florida Chief Financial Officer Blaise Ingoglia announced the arrest of public adjuster Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus on charges related to the misappropriation of over $703,000 in Hurricane Ian claim funds. Chaparro-Araus allegedly forged victims' names, concealed insurance settlement checks, and refused to pay homeowners after being contacted. He has been charged with organized fraud, financial exploitation of an elderly person, diversion and misappropriation of a premium, and failure to report and account for funds.

Notable: This is Chaparro-Araus's second arrest for similar conduct; he was previously prosecuted in July 2024 for misappropriation by the Department of Financial Services. The case highlights the regulatory scrutiny facing practitioners in the space and the importance of transparent fund handling.

Industry Infrastructure

Roofing Contractor Market: High Demand, Regulatory Shift

Expansion Activity: Atlantic Metal Roofing and All Phase Construction USA are both expanding operations across South Florida (Deerfield Beach, Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach) to capture demand for wind-rated roofing systems.

Demand Trends: Wind-rated roofing material requests have climbed 47% across Palm Beach and Martin Counties since early 2024, with metal and tile systems leading.

Regulatory Change: Effective December 31, 2026, the 9th Edition Florida Building Code revises the 25% roof damage rule. Partial roof replacements are now allowed even when damage exceeds 25% — if the remaining undamaged roof was built to 2007 Florida Building Code standards or newer. This change expands contractor opportunities for partial replacements rather than full re-roofs.

Context: AccuWeather forecasts 11–16 named storms along the Atlantic Coast this hurricane season, signaling active contractor demand ahead.
