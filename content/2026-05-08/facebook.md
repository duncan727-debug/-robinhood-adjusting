## Your Weekend Just Got Busier (In a Good Way)

South Florida's looking at extreme heat this weekend—we're talking 90s that feel like 100+ with the humidity. For HVAC contractors, this is your moment. Emergency cooling calls are about to spike, and so are the follow-ups.

What I'm seeing in the field: heat stress failures happen *during* the peak, but they compound *after*. Homeowners and commercial property managers who ignored that aging A/C unit for two years? They're calling you Friday afternoon. But here's the play—while you're there for the emergency, you're also inspecting their attic ventilation, roof condition, and ductwork. One service call becomes three.

The bigger picture? We're 24 days from hurricane season. Property owners are waking up to the fact that heat isn't their only risk. Sustained heat weakens roofing systems, stresses electrical panels, and makes older properties vulnerable when storms hit. That HVAC job you do this weekend? It's your entry point to positioning them for wind mitigation, roof inspections, and damage assessments when the season opens June 1st.

**For other trades:** This is prime time for property managers to schedule preventive maintenance before the demand surge hits. Roofers, electricians, and plumbers—get your inspections booked now. By mid-May, everyone's calling at once.

**How are you staffing this weekend?** Are you positioning emergency calls as inspection opportunities, or letting them stay one-off jobs?
