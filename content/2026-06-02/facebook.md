# FB Post — Tuesday, June 2, 2026 (provider angle)

For the SF roofers, impact-window installers, and garage-door folks reading this:

**HB 815 takes effect July 1**, and it changes your homeowner conversation in two ways most crews haven't caught yet.

▫️ Carriers can't non-renew a policy solely because a roof is under 15 years old. That kills the "your insurance will drop you if you don't replace" pressure script your competition has been leaning on. Use it instead as a *trust* opener with the homeowner.

▫️ For 15+ year roofs, the homeowner has the *right* to an inspection before a forced replacement. Low-slope roofs can be coated rather than replaced if certified to have 5+ years left. If you have elastomeric or silicone coating capacity — that's a newly statute-blessed line item starting July 1.

Pair either play with a fresh **wind-mit inspection on the new 1802 form** (live since April), and the homeowner sees premium savings of $800–$1,500/year on a $4,500 premium. One truck-roll, three line items, a happier homeowner, and a referral source for life.
