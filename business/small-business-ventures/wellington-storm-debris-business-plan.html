Executive Summary

A residential and small-commercial debris-hauling service serving Wellington, Royal Palm Beach, Loxahatchee, and west WPB. The business runs on three stacked revenue streams: (1) on-demand yard-waste pickup ($150–$500/job), (2) optional monthly subscription for routine pickup ($40–$80/mo), and (3) post-storm emergency response (premium pricing during named-storm windows). Built around the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season starting June 1 and the year-round PBC green-debris reality.

Why this fits Duncan + son for summer 2026

  • Seasonal alignment: Hurricane season opens June 1; school's out; father-son labor available exactly when demand spikes.

Market & Demand Drivers

Service Area

  • Wellington: ~64,000 population, ~25K residences
  • Royal Palm Beach: ~40,000 population
  • Loxahatchee / Acreage: ~45,000 population, large lots
  • West WPB (33411 / 33414 / 33470): ~50,000 population
  • Service zone total: ~200,000 residents

Demand Engines (year-round + seasonal)

  • Year-round palm-frond shedding (~6–10/tree/yr)
  • Royal palm and queen palm prevalence on PBC residential lots
  • Average lot size in target zips: 0.25–2.0 acres (larger than FL avg)
  • Average named storms hitting PBC annually: 1–3 (NOAA 2020–2025)
  • Hurricane Season: Jun 1 – Nov 30
  • Existing dedicated competitors: 4–6 (mostly tree-service add-ons, no pure haul-only player at this price tier)
SWA tipping reality: Solid Waste Authority of Palm Beach County accepts vegetative debris at the North County Transfer Station (Jog Rd) and South County (Lantana) at $35.00/ton or $5.25/yd³ minimum (FY 2025–26 schedule). Pickups paid at $200/load with tipping cost of $25–40 produce gross margins above 80% before fuel and labor.
Why landscapers won't absorb this themselves: Most small PBC landscape crews already run full-capacity routes. They charge premium for separate debris pickup or refuse altogether. We are the cheap, fast, no-contract alternative for the homeowner who just wants the pile gone.

Door-to-Door & Direct-Marketing Playbook

Door Hanger Mechanics

  • Print run: 1,000 units, $0.12–0.18 each, full color, 2-sided
  • Front: storm-debris photo + "$150 Small Loads / Same-Day Quotes"
  • Back: QR code → booking page; subscription pricing; phone number
  • Drop cadence: 250/day Saturdays in Tier 1 zips before season opens; refresh post-storm
  • Conversion target: 0.8–1.5% (8–15 jobs per 1,000 drops)

Yard Signs at Active Jobs

  • $3 each, request placement at every job (offer $25 service credit)
  • Sign stays 7 days; pull on next nearby job
  • Doubles visibility for free; closes referrals naturally
  • Phone number large; "Locally Owned, Wellington" tagline

HOA Board Pitch (in person)

  • Hook: "We're a Wellington father-son crew offering single-bill, 24-hr-response yard-waste removal for common areas. Cheaper than your tree-service contract for routine pickup."
  • Tier offered: Monthly common-area pickup (negotiated price); priority post-storm window included.
  • Leave-behind: One-page service sheet + price list + insurance certificate.
  • Close: "Want me to send a free quote on next month's pickup so the board has a number to look at?"

Tree-Service Partner Approach

  • Target: 8–12 local tree services who currently haul their own debris
  • Pitch: "We take the haul off your hands. You stay on tools, finish more jobs, no SWA round trip."
  • Pricing: 30% discount to standard rate (still very profitable for us; saves them 2 hrs/job)
  • Already-existing PBC tree services (sample): Davey Tree, Treeman Co., Bartlett, Tropic Tree

Bottom Line

Why this venture replaces the pellet pitch cleanly

  • Capital at risk is small: $5.5K–$10K to find out, vs. $200K+ for the pellet pitch
  • Cash-positive Month 1 with first job done in Week 3
  • Three stacked revenue streams reduce single-channel risk: on-demand + subscription + storm surge
  • No regulatory drag: No FDEP permits, no zoning fights, no licensing required at this scale
  • Leverages existing assets: Robin Hood newsletter, HubSpot HOA/PM contacts, Duncan's local trust, son's labor through Aug 17
  • Year 1 prove-out is grounded: 432 jobs, $107K revenue, ~$60K owner take. Numbers assume average storm year, not heroes.
  • 3-year cumulative owner net of ~$245K with one operator + one truck expansion — credible compared to the pellet deck's $649K fantasy on infeasible assumptions.
