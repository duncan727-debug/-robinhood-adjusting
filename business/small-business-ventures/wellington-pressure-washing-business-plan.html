Residential Exterior Cleaning — Wellington & West Palm Beach County

Business Plan v1.0 · Prepared 2026-05-17 · Operator-prove → hire-out → restart model

Executive Summary

Wellington Pressure Washing (working name) is a residential exterior-cleaning service focused on driveways, paver patios, pool decks, roofs (soft-wash), and house siding for Wellington, Royal Palm Beach, Loxahatchee, and west WPB. The business runs a high-ticket project model ($200–$800/job) with a recurring "Annual Refresh" subscription overlay, and is designed from day one to be handed off to a $20–22/hr operator once proven.

Why this business fits the operator-holding model

  • High-ticket, daylight-visible work: $200–$800 jobs; results are dramatic and drive word-of-mouth in dense, manicured neighborhoods.
  • Hireable by design: Surface-cleaner work is trainable in 1–2 weeks under a written SOP. No state license required for residential pressure washing in Florida (commercial chemical roof-cleaning has separate rules — see Risks).
  • Cheap to fail: Under $8K committed if using existing truck. Sub-90-day prove window.
  • Independent of Robin Hood Adjusting: No PA-license dependency, no claims-cycle exposure. Visible, tangible service that closes on a single visit.
  • Wellington-specific tailwind: Paver driveways, tile/barrel roofs, pool decks, and equestrian-property concrete are the four highest-margin surfaces — and Wellington has them in abundance.

Market — Wellington & West PBC

Service Area

  • Wellington: ~64,000 population
  • Royal Palm Beach: ~40,000 population
  • Loxahatchee/Acreage: ~45,000 population
  • West WPB (33411 / 33414 / 33470): ~50,000 population
  • Service zone total: ~200,000 residents / ~65,000 households

Addressable Demand

  • SFL homeowners who pressure-wash annually: ~30%
  • Estimated annual-buy households in zone: ~18,000–20,000
  • Avg ticket: $350 (driveway + walkway) to $650 (full house + roof soft-wash)
  • Realistic addressable market: ~$8M–$10M annual spend
  • Current competitors in zone: 12–18 active (mix of solo operators, 2 franchises, 1 mid-size local)
Wellington-specific wedge: The four most lucrative residential pressure-washing surfaces — pavers, barrel-tile roofs, pool decks, and white estate fencing — are dramatically over-represented in Wellington vs. typical FL markets. Mold/algae buildup on these surfaces is highly visible and seasonal (peaks May–Oct), which compresses the annual buying decision into a tight, urgent window.

Hiring & Handoff Plan (Year 2)

Months 1–6 (Year 1)

Duncan + son operate. Build SOPs by surface type (driveway, soft-wash, roof, paver seal). Document every chemical ratio, dwell time, and rinse sequence.

Months 7–12

Hire a part-time helper. Ride along on every job; train as second wand-operator. $18/hr, 20–25 hr/week. Test the trainability of the SOP.

Year 2 Month 1

Promote helper or hire full-time lead operator. Duncan transitions to estimating + customer relations + QC (~6 hrs/week). Operator owns the wand.

Operator Job Spec

  • Title: Lead Technician — Wellington Pressure Washing
  • Pay: $20–24/hr (FL market for skilled outdoor trade work); $45–50K/yr base + $5K performance bonus tied to job-quality reviews and customer retention
  • Requirements: Valid FL driver's license, clean record, can lift 60 lbs, comfortable on ladders + roofs, basic chemical-safety understanding, fluent in English (Spanish preferred), reliable transport to Wellington
  • Training: 2 weeks ride-along with Duncan; written SOP per surface type; chemical-mixing checklist; ladder + roof-safety certification (free, OSHA online)
  • Daily tools: Truck (provided), trailer w/ equipment (provided), uniform, smartphone with Jobber app, GoPro for before/after documentation

Risks & Mitigations

Equipment failure (pump, engine, hose) Medium Maintenance schedule (oil + pump-oil every 50 hr); carry spare belts, fittings, O-rings; warranty service provider identified pre-launch

Decision Gates

Gate 1 — Day 90

Continue? Need 20+ jobs/mo, $7K+ monthly revenue, blended CAC under $50, 8+ five-star reviews. If yes → continue ramp. If no → diagnose or shut down (sunk cost <$12K).

Gate 2 — Day 180

Hire helper? If at 25+ jobs/mo and route is taking >30 hrs/week including drive + estimating, hire part-time helper. Begin operator-trainability test on chemical handling.

Gate 3 — Day 365

Hire FT lead + restart? If at 30+ jobs/mo and SOPs are documented across all surface types, promote helper or hire FT lead. Duncan steps to estimating + QC. Begin scouting Business #3 (yard patrol or storm debris already on the bench).

Bottom Line

Why this venture deserves the green light

  • Capital risk is moderate: ~$10K to find out, vs. $200K+ for the pellet pitch
  • Cash-flow positive from Month 1 at realistic ramp rates — first job covers the marketing spend that produced it
  • Highest visible ROI per dollar of effort: $385 average ticket, 73% gross margin, 2.5–3 hr per job
  • No regulatory drag: No FDEP, no zoning fights, no state license (roof soft-wash may need Limited Commercial Applicator — confirm pre-launch)
  • Leverages existing assets: Robin Hood newsletter, Duncan's HOA / property-manager relationships, the truck you already drive
  • Year 1 prove-out is concrete: 250 jobs, $97K revenue, ~$55K owner take. Numbers don't require heroic assumptions.
  • 3-year cumulative owner net of ~$257K with one operator hire and one truck expansion — high-margin, high-visibility, hireable work that complements the operator-holding-co thesis.
