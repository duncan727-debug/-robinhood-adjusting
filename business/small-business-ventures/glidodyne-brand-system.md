## 1. Brand thesis

**Glidodyne is the engineering house that makes the family pool magical.**

We sit at the intersection of three brands you've probably owned: the design clarity of **Apple**, the engineering pedigree of **Braun**, and the playful precision of **Aerobie**. The category we operate in — underwater pool toys — has been treated for 30 years as a $3 throwaway. We're the brand that engineered it.

Glidodyne's product feels heavier in the hand than the competition, performs noticeably better in the water, and looks like something you'd be happy leaving on the pool deck where guests can see it. That premium signal is the whole product.

## 2. Voice & tone

**Adjectives we want associated with the brand:** precise · engineered · understated · playful · confident · honest.

**Adjectives we explicitly avoid:** epic · extreme · ultimate · "max performance" · "blast" · "next-gen" · cheap · cute · cartoonish.

**The "little rebel" injection** (per Duncan, 2026-05-23): we're not stuffy. The brand voice has a slight smirk — a maker's confidence, not a corporate vacuum. Compare to:
- ✅ Patagonia ad copy: factual, specific, no superlatives, slightly anti-establishment
- ✅ Aesop product descriptions: technical detail elevated as poetry
- ✅ Aerobie's original tagline: *"The world's farthest-thrown object."* Plainspoken claim.
- ❌ Hasbro / Nerf: superlatives, exclamation points, kid-energy
- ❌ Swimways: chrome typography, action-blur photography

## 3. Visual identity

### Primary monogram

See `glidodyne-monogram.svg`. The mark is a geometric **G** with the mouth opening to the right (firing direction). A signal-orange dot sits at the muzzle position — both as a brand accent and as a literal reference to the product. The mark works at favicon size and on a pool-deck product label.

**Construction:**
- Outer ring: 32px stroke at 600×600 reference scale, perfect geometric circle
- Mouth opening: rightward, full-width cut, 64px tall
- Inner bar: 30px tall, extends to the inside of the ring's right edge
- Accent dot: 12px radius, at the natural firing point

### Wordmark

System-stack sans-serif (SF Pro on Apple devices, Helvetica Neue / Inter elsewhere), **medium weight** (500), letter-spacing **+2px at 64pt**. Set in title case (Glidodyne), never all-caps in body copy.

### Color palette (v0.1 — open to refinement)

| Use | Pantone / hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Primary ink | Black `#1d1d1f` | Used for monogram + wordmark on warm-white surfaces |
| Surface — warm white | `#f5f3ef` | Background everywhere; matches matte ABS product body |
| Accent — signal orange | `#ff7a30` (PMS 165C territory) | Single-accent rule: never more than one orange element per surface |
| Body — charcoal alt | `#3a3a3c` | For dark-body product variant + premium packaging |
| Body — sea-foam alt | `#cfe5dd` | Reserved for a possible "summer collection" variant — defer |

**Single-accent rule:** anywhere the brand appears, the orange shows up exactly **once**. On the product: muzzle ring or pressure dot, not both. On the box: a single thin orange band. In an ad: the product photo or the call-to-action color, never both.

### Typography hierarchy

| Level | Font | Weight | Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wordmark | System sans | 500 | 64pt @ 600 logo scale |
| Headlines | System sans | 600 | 32-48pt |
| Body | System sans | 400 | 16-18pt |
| Captions / specs | System sans monospaced (SF Mono / JetBrains Mono) | 400 | 13pt |

The mono use for specs is the "engineering house" cue — every spec sheet, every parts diagram, every measurement uses monospace. Body copy stays warm.

### Photography style

- **Hero shots:** product alone, on a single sheet of warm-white paper, soft top-light, slight cast shadow. Like an Aesop product page.
- **In-use shots:** kid or dad, mid-action, underwater half-frame, no lifestyle clutter, no people looking at camera, no cheering. Quiet competence.
- **Avoid:** stock-photo families, exclamation-point copy on the image, sun-flare lens effects, blur trails behind the projectile.

### What we explicitly will NOT do

- No chrome anywhere on the product or in branding.
- No character mascot.
- No decals applied to the product. Brand goes on via **laser etching only** (single G, monogram-size, low-contrast).
- No "Extreme," "Pro," "Max," "Ultra," "X" prefixes or suffixes on product names. Variant naming uses neutral identifiers: Glidodyne **One**, Glidodyne **Two**, Glidodyne **Mini**.
- No fake-tactical visual language. We're not a Nerf-shaped product.

## 4. Product naming convention

| Layer | Name format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Brand | Glidodyne | Glidodyne |
| Product line | Single word, generic | The Launcher |
| Generation | Numeral | Glidodyne One, Two, Mini |
| Accessory | Single noun | Refill pack, Stand, Travel case |
| Special edition | Year or place | Glidodyne / Wellington edition |

Never: "Glidodyne Pro Plus 2.0 Extreme Edition." If we ever feel pulled toward language like that, we've lost the thread.
