Wellington Yard Patrol

Pet Waste Subscription Service — Wellington & West Palm Beach County

Business Plan v1.0 · Prepared 2026-05-16 · Operator-prove → hire-out → restart model

Executive Summary

Wellington Yard Patrol (working name) is a weekly subscription pet-waste removal service serving Wellington, Royal Palm Beach, Loxahatchee, and west WPB. The business runs on a fixed weekly route, charging $20-30 per visit on auto-pay, and is designed from day one to be handed off to a $20/hr operator once proven.

Why this business fits the operator-holding model

  • Recurring revenue: Subscription auto-pay = predictable cash flow, low churn (~5%/month industry average).
  • Hireable by design: The work is routine, trainable in a week, and runs on a written SOP. No license required (FL does not require a pet-waste-removal license).
  • Cheap to fail: Under $5K committed if using existing truck. Sub-90-day prove window.
  • Independent of Robin Hood Adjusting: No PA-license dependency, no claims-cycle exposure, no Duncan-the-person required after Year 1.
  • Wellington-specific tailwind: Dog-dense, large-yard, high-income, 4,000+ permanent horse-property residents who outsource yard work as default.

Market — Wellington & West PBC

Service Area

  • Wellington: ~64,000 population
  • Royal Palm Beach: ~40,000 population
  • Loxahatchee/Acreage: ~45,000 population
  • West WPB (33411 / 33414 / 33470): ~50,000 population
  • Service zone total: ~200,000 residents

Addressable Demand

  • FL household dog-ownership rate: ~45%
  • Estimated dog-owning households in service zone: ~25,000-30,000
  • National pet-waste subscription penetration: ~1-2% of dog households
  • Realistic addressable market: 250-600 paying customers
  • Current competitors in zone: 3 active (DoodyCalls franchise, Poop911 affiliate, 1-2 independents)
Wellington-specific wedge: Equestrian-community residents already pay $200-1000+/month for landscape and stable services. The "pay someone to handle my yard" buyer is the default mindset here, unlike most FL markets. Multi-dog households in equestrian neighborhoods are common.

Hiring & Handoff Plan (Year 2)

Months 1-6 (Year 1)

Duncan + son operate. Build SOPs, customer playbook, route software. Document every edge case.

Months 7-12

Hire a part-time helper to ride along, learn the route. $18/hr, 15-20 hr/week. Test the trainability of the system.

Year 2 Month 1

Promote helper or hire full-time operator. Duncan transitions to 5 hrs/week oversight. Helper becomes route owner.

Operator Job Spec

  • Title: Route Operator — Wellington Yard Patrol
  • Pay: $18-22/hr (FL market for outdoor route work); $40-45K/yr base + $5K performance bonus tied to customer retention
  • Requirements: Valid FL driver's license, clean record, can lift 40 lbs, comfortable around dogs, fluent in English (Spanish preferred), reliable transport to Wellington
  • Training: 1 week ride-along with Duncan + son; written SOP for every customer property; route software training
  • Daily tools: Truck (provided), tool kit, smartphone with Sweep&Go app, branded uniform shirt

Decision Gates

Gate 1 — Day 90

Continue? Need 60+ customers, $5K+ MRR, churn under 5%/mo. If yes → continue ramp. If no → diagnose or shut down (sunk cost <$8K).

Gate 2 — Day 180

Hire helper? If at 100+ customers and route is taking >30 hrs/week, hire part-time helper. Begin operator-trainability test.

Gate 3 — Day 365

Hire FT operator + restart? If at 150+ customers and SOPs are documented, promote helper or hire FT operator. Duncan steps to oversight. Begin scouting Business #2.

Bottom Line

Why this venture deserves the green light

  • Capital risk is small: <$8K to find out, vs. $200K+ for the pellet pitch
  • Cash-flow positive by Month 3 at realistic ramp rates
  • Recurring revenue = predictable handoff — the whole point of the operator-holding model
  • No regulatory drag: No FDEP, no zoning fights, no licensing
  • Leverages existing assets: Robin Hood newsletter, Duncan's HOA/PM relationships, the truck you already drive
  • Year 1 prove-out is concrete: 150 customers, $90K-100K revenue, ~$75K owner take. Numbers don't require heroic assumptions.
  • 3-year cumulative owner net of ~$348K with one operator hire and one route expansion — far more credible than the pellet deck's $649K fantasy.
