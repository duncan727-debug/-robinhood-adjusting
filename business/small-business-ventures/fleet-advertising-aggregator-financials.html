What changed from v2: Every revenue and cost driver is now anchored to researched industry benchmarks rather than internal estimates. Business Assumptions

Revenue Model

  • Inventory mix (Y1 blended): 40% panel, 30% vinyl, 20% partial wrap, 10% full wrap
  • Advertiser rate (blended): $500/truck/mo
  • Fleet payout: 60% of advertiser revenue ($300/truck/mo — inside the published $300–$500 industry range)
  • Our net per truck/mo: $115 (after wrap amort, install, processing, ops)
  • Effective take rate: 23% blended
  • Avg fleet size: 30 trucks (mid-market commercial)
  • Min advertiser term: 3 mo panel / 6 mo partial / 12 mo full

Operational Assumptions

  • Customer Acquisition Cost: $3,000 per fleet (within $1.5K–$3.5K B2B benchmark)
  • Sales Cycle: 60–90 days (industry-validated B2B norm)
  • Annual fleet churn: 15%
  • Campaign fill rate: 70% (truck-level utilization)
  • Phase 0 fleets (Mo 1-4): 2 (Wellington/PBC pilot)
  • Phase 1 fleets EOY 1: 6 (SFL expansion)
  • Y1 trucks EOY: 245
Where the 60/40 split came from: Published truck-side advertising programs (Wrapify, fleet-broker outfits) advertise fleet payouts of $300–$500/truck/month. We anchor to the conservative end ($300) and price advertisers at $500 — fleet keeps the majority, our take rate is 23%. The 23% sits inside the 20–30% gross-commission band typical for OOH media reps; the difference is that we also operate the inventory (wrap amort, install, advertiser servicing) — that's what produces a defensible margin instead of pure-broker thinness.

Why This Business Scales (and Service Ventures Don't)

The operating leverage thesis

  • Service ventures (yard patrol, pressure washing, debris): Each new customer adds linear cost. Operator labor is the bottleneck. Geographic radius caps TAM.
  • This business: Adding the 50th fleet costs less than adding the 5th (sales playbook + onboarding SOPs already exist). Software handles measurement. Geographic expansion is repeatable.
  • Margin structure: Y1 net margin 36%, Y5 net margin 67%. Operating leverage compounds.
  • Exit multiple: Service businesses sell at 1.5–3× EBITDA. Ad-tech / OOH-platform businesses sell at 5–10× EBITDA. At Y5's $1.91M owner net, that's a $9.5M–$19M exit value vs. $3M–$6M for a comparable service-business owner net.
Honest caveat: The exit multiple assumes a buyer values the platform + advertiser + fleet relationships, not just the cash flow. That valuation premium requires consistent measurement, multi-market presence, and recurring contracts — all of which the v3 plan builds toward but none of which is guaranteed.

Summary & Investment Thesis

Why this venture earns the green light for Phase 0

  • Phase 0 is cheap: $10K cap, 4 months, Day 120 kill-or-continue gate
  • Unfair distribution: Robin Hood newsletter + provider directory + HOA/chamber relationships pre-warm both sides of the marketplace
  • Real market gap, not imaginary: Carvertise doesn't do fleets; wrap shops aren't media brokers; OOH platforms don't sell trucks at local mid-market scale
  • Scale path is concrete: Phase 1 SFL → Phase 2 statewide → Phase 3 SE US. Each phase has a gate; each gate has hard numbers.
  • Defensible unit economics: 37:1 LTV:CAC at researched CAC ($3,000). Fleet payout 60% (anchored to published $300/truck/mo industry baseline). Take rate 23% — fleet still gets the majority.
  • Exit-friendly: Ad-tech multiples at Y5 produce a $9.5M–$19M outcome vs. $3M–$6M for a comparable service business — the reason this is the venture to focus the scale ambition on, not service ones.
The decision:

Authorize $10K for Phase 0 and commit ~10 hrs/week through Day 120. If the gate hits (2 fleets, 25 trucks, 5 advertisers, $5K MRR), Phase 1 unlocks. If it misses, we shut down for under $10K of sunk cost.
