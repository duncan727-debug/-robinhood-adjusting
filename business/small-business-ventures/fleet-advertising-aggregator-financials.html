Business Assumptions

Revenue Model

  • Commission Structure: 20% of advertiser spend
  • Advertiser Rate: $400-500/truck/month (market rate)
  • Our Revenue/Truck: $80-100/month
  • Fleet Payout: 80% ($320-400/month per truck)
  • Fleet Size (avg): 50 trucks
  • Fleet Monthly Revenue: $4,000-5,000

Operational Assumptions

  • Customer Acquisition Cost: $1,500 per fleet
  • Sales Cycles: 6-8 weeks per fleet
  • Churn Rate: 5% annually (low due to revenue-share model)
  • Campaign Conversion: 80% (fleets → live campaigns)
  • Year 1 Ramp: 2 fleets (Jan) → 8 fleets (Dec)
  • Year 1 Avg Fleet Count: 5 fleets
Key Assumption: Model assumes 20% commission from advertisers (standard for ad tech). This means $80-100 revenue per truck per month with zero inventory, logistics, or production costs—pure margin business.

Summary & Investment Thesis

Why This Business Works

  • Low Capital: $20K investment with positive cash flow by Month 3. ROI of 18,400% over 5 years.
  • Exceptional Unit Economics: LTV:CAC of 108:1. Every fleet pays for itself in 2-3 weeks.
  • Proven Market: $3.8B global vehicle wrap market, 9.5% CAGR. Advertisers pay $400-500/truck/month. Demand proven.
  • Zero Inventory Risk: Pure commission model (20% of advertiser spend). No production, logistics, or inventory costs.
  • Scalable Model: Add fleets = add revenue at $4,500/month per fleet with minimal cost increase. Margins improve as we scale.
  • Relationship Defensibility: Fleet owners want a dedicated account rep and revenue-sharing partner, not a self-serve marketplace. Creates switching costs.
Key Risks & Mitigations:
  • Fleet Churn: Model assumes 5% annual churn. Mitigate by: revenue-share keeps them aligned, long-term contracts, excellent customer service.
  • Advertiser Demand: Proven market ($3.8B), but need to source quality advertisers. Mitigate by: partner with agencies, brands, local businesses early.
  • Execution/Sales: Need salesperson who can close fleet owners. Mitigate by: hire experienced B2B sales person; referral incentives; proof-of-concept pilots.
  • Competition: Existing players (TRUCK ADS, Crosswalk) could copy model. Mitigate by: lock in fleets early; build brand; focus on service excellence.
Why Now?

South Florida logistics market is booming (Amazon expansion, port growth, last-mile delivery). Fleet operators are undermonetizing truck assets. Advertisers are seeking cheap OOH inventory. Market is ripe for aggregator who can connect these two sides efficiently.
