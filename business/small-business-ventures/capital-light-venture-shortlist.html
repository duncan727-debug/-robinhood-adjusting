Capital-light venture shortlist — father-son partnership

Brief: 12 sub-$15K-startup ventures suited to a Wellington / Palm Beach County father-son operation, leaning on the storm/insurance/property/local-services thesis already validated by the pressure-wash + storm-debris + fleet-ad decks.

Constraints applied: - Startup capital ≤ $15K (most ≤ $5K) - Father-son labor model (no employees Y1) - Wellington/PBC market, leveraging the 100-in-100 PA network where useful - Cash-flow positive within ~90 days - Each idea has a clear first customer (not "build it and they will come")

Format: rank → name → one-line concept → why it works here → biggest risk → first 30-day move.

1. Hurricane-prep concierge (residential)

Concept: Pre-storm checklist service — shutters up, patio furniture in, generator gassed, photo inventory for insurance, emergency contact card. $150/visit, recurring annual contract for $300/yr "always-ready" tier.

Why here: PBC has ~250K single-family homes, 60% absentee/snowbird-adjacent. Existing PA client base = warm intro pool. Aligns with Robinhood positioning.

Biggest risk: Sells once a year unless bundled with off-season maintenance.

30-day move: Sell 10 annual contracts to existing PA contacts; target snowbird HOAs in Wellington/Boca/Jupiter.

Startup: $1.5K (tools, magnetic signs, insurance rider). Y1 rev target: $35-50K.

2. Post-claim documentation service

Concept: Mobile property-damage photo + video documentation for homeowners filing claims. Drone exterior, 4K interior, timestamped + GPS-stamped, delivered as a court-ready PDF + USB. $250-500 per home.

Why here: Every PA client needs this. Carriers increasingly demand it. Duncan can refer; son operates. Lives inside the existing PA workflow — almost zero acquisition cost.

Biggest risk: Regulatory line — must NOT be construed as adjusting. Pure documentation, no opinions.

30-day move: Add to Robinhood website as a service; route every new PA engagement through it.

Startup: $3-4K (drone + Part 107 license + decent camera). Y1 rev: $40-70K.

3. Generator & shutter pre-season service

Concept: Annual generator load-test + hurricane shutter inspection. $125/visit, May/early-June window. Light electrical + handyman work; pull permits where required.

Why here: ~40% of Wellington homes have generators or shutters; almost nobody services them between storms. Highly seasonal but very predictable.

Biggest risk: Liability if a generator fails when called upon. Mitigated with a "performed inspection only — owner responsible for repair" disclaimer.

30-day move: Door-hangers in 5 Wellington subdivisions; HOA newsletter ad in 3 communities.

Startup: $2K (load bank rental + multimeter + ladders). Y1 rev: $25-45K.

4. Estate & downsize photo cataloging

Concept: Photograph and catalog a household's contents for insurance, estate planning, or pre-move. Searchable PDF + cloud copy. $400-1,200 per home depending on size.

Why here: Aging-out PBC demographic, lots of $1M+ snowbird homes. PA network knows which homes recently had claims (= owners who just learned why they needed this).

Biggest risk: Slow per-home throughput — must charge accordingly.

30-day move: Partner with 2 elder-law attorneys + 1 estate-planning CPA for referrals.

Startup: $1K (camera + barcode printer + tablet). Y1 rev: $30-50K.

5. HOA compliance walks ("violation finder")

Concept: Monthly community walk for HOAs — photograph deferred-maintenance / code violations, log GPS+photo report, deliver to HOA board. Replaces volunteer board members doing it themselves. $400-800/month per community.

Why here: Wellington alone has 80+ HOAs. Volunteer burnout on boards is universal. One contract = one recurring revenue stream.

Biggest risk: Awkward neighbor dynamics — must position as "service to the board," not "the snitch."

30-day move: Identify 5 HOA presidents via PA network; pitch one written proposal.

Startup: $500 (app subscription + template designs). Y1 rev: $35-60K with 6-10 HOAs.

6. Junk-haul + donation routing

Concept: Single-truck junk removal — but explicit donation routing (Habitat ReStore, Goodwill, local shelters) instead of straight-to-dump. Charge same as 1-800-JUNK ($150-450/load) and market the diversion rate.

Why here: Saturated market, but the "we donate first" angle is genuinely uncommon and HOA-newsletter-friendly. Wellington skews values-conscious.

Biggest risk: Disposal-fee margin compression if dump prices rise.

30-day move: Buy/lease a used dump-bed truck on Marketplace; offer 5 free hauls in exchange for referrals.

Startup: $8-12K (used truck is the big line). Y1 rev: $60-100K.

7. Storm-shutter installation/removal-on-demand service

Concept: Two visits per named storm: install before, remove after. $125 install + $75 removal per home, annual prepay $250. Snowbird-tier $400/yr (we hold a key).

Why here: Aging owners can't safely climb ladders anymore. The "I'll get to it" trap kills people every storm. Highest-trust offering on this list.

Biggest risk: Volume crunch during a 48-hour pre-storm window — must cap clients per season.

30-day move: Pitch 3 Wellington HOAs as preferred provider for elderly residents.

Startup: $2K (ladders, hardware kit, signs). Y1 rev: $30-45K.

8. Newsletter ad-sales side hustle

Concept: Sell ad slots in Duncan's daily brief to local service businesses (roofers, plumbers, HVAC, attorneys). $250-500/month per slot, 3-5 slots. Father-son does outreach; Duncan provides the inventory.

Why here: The audience is already there. The newsletter has a clear PA/insurance/local-property reader profile = exact buyer for these service categories. Zero startup cost.

Biggest risk: Pre-revenue audience size — need ≥500 subscribers before ad value is honest. Park until then.

30-day move: Build a 1-pager media kit; start outreach when sub count crosses 500.

Startup: $0. Y1 rev: $15-40K once gated open.

9. Pressure-wash + soft-wash subscription (recurring twist on prior plan)

Concept: Same pressure-wash skill set already planned, but priced as $89/month "always-clean driveway+walk" subscription instead of one-off jobs. Drives recurring revenue + route density.

Why here: Already capitalized in prior plan. Subscription model is the missing piece that turns the plan from $97K Y1 to $150K+ Y2 with same labor.

Biggest risk: Churn if monthly visits feel wasteful in dry season — offer "skip month" option.

30-day move: Convert 20 of the planned first-50 customers to subscription.

Startup: Already in prior plan (~$10K). Incremental Y1 rev: $15-25K.

10. Pool-cage screen-repair specialist

Concept: Single-trade focus on screen repair (not pools, not full enclosures) — just rip/tear/storm-damage screen replacement. $4-6/sq ft. Highly fragmented competition.

Why here: Every storm shreds screens. Every PA claim includes screen line items. Father-son can do 2-3 cages/day.

Biggest risk: Heat — only feasible Oct-May. Stack with storm-prep work in summer.

30-day move: Lock the supplier (screen roll + spline tools); post in 3 community FB groups (when FB unblocks).

Startup: $1.5K (rolls of screen, spline, roller tools, ladders). Y1 rev: $35-55K.

11. Insurance-document scanning / shoebox service

Concept: Pickup → scan → index → cloud-delivery of homeowners' insurance/property/medical files. Flat fee $200-400 per shoebox. Annual maintenance tier $150/yr to re-scan new docs.

Why here: Hurricane wipes out paper. PA network has the exact "I should organize this before next storm" anxiety. Father-son can scan in evenings.

Biggest risk: Trust — must position as a known-name service (Robinhood's reputation helps).

30-day move: Buy a Fujitsu ScanSnap; pilot with 5 PA clients free in exchange for testimonials.

Startup: $1K (scanner + cloud storage subscription). Y1 rev: $20-35K.

12. "Property concierge" subscription bundle

Concept: Wrap ideas 1, 3, 7, 11 into a single $79/month homeowner concierge subscription: hurricane prep, generator check, shutter service, doc scanning — one provider, one bill. Targets snowbird absentee owners who can't fly down to handle these themselves.

Why here: This is the bundle play once individual services have customers. Snowbirds will pay for the "I don't have to think about it" tier. PBC has ~70K seasonal homes.

Biggest risk: Hard to staff during peak season; needs the underlying services profitable first.

30-day move: Park as Y2 bundle play; build the components 1/3/7/11 first.

Startup: $0 incremental. Y2 rev target: $100-200K with 100 subscribers.

Suggested filter

If picking three to deck-up next:

  1. #2 (Post-claim documentation) — lowest acquisition cost, lives inside existing PA workflow, regulatory line clean as long as it stays documentation-only.
  2. #5 (HOA compliance walks) — pure recurring revenue, distinct from anything else on Duncan's plate, ≤$500 startup.
  3. #7 (Storm-shutter on-demand) — highest-trust positioning, snowbird buyer profile maps onto known PBC demographics, fits seasonally with #2 and #5.

These three together: ~$3K total startup, $100-160K Y1 revenue with father-son labor only, zero overlap with each other on labor calendar, and all three cross-pollinate with the PA referral flow.
