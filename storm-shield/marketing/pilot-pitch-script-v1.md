a free pilot for one of their communities — not a buy-now. This protects
credibility while the signup flow + Concierge fulfillment are still being built.

24 firms in `prospects/palm-beach-pm-firms.csv`.

## KEY MESSAGING GUARDRAILS

1. **Never** describe Storm Shield as a "home inspection" — Florida Ch. 468
   reserves that term. Always "pre-loss documentation."
2. **Never** imply the PM/HOA gets paid or shares revenue. Compensation = zero.
3. **Disclose the affiliate relationship** explicitly: "I'm the founder of
   both Robinhood Adjusting and Storm Shield." First-call talking point.
4. **Don't push the on-site Concierge tier in cold outreach.** Lead with the
   free Guided pilot. Concierge is upsell during the call once they're warm.
5. **Pilot framing protects you legally and ethically** — you're not selling
   storage of a homeowner-file you can't yet store. You're validating demand.

## CALL OPENING (when they show up to the 15-min)

"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the call. Real quick on me: I'm a Florida
public adjuster — I represent homeowners after claims. I'm also the
founder of Storm Shield, which is a separate documentation service we
built because we kept seeing the same problem on every claim: homeowners
didn't have pre-loss photos, and that cost them five figures on settlements.

The pilot I'm proposing for [Community Name] is: residents get the
Guided plan free for the first hurricane season. They use it or they
don't — no pressure. If a storm hits and one of them files a claim,
they can use any PA they want, not just me. That's the design.

The reason I'm asking the board first is the only thing that actually
gets adoption is a board-forwarded email. Cold marketing to homeowners
doesn't work. A nudge from you does.

Does that make sense as a structure? What would you need to feel
comfortable taking it to the board?"

## OBJECTION HANDLERS

**"This sounds like a sales funnel for your PA business."**
> Fair concern. That's why we built the affiliate disclosure and the
> 'use any PA you want' clause. The subscription revenue is what makes
> Storm Shield work — not steering claims to my firm.

**"Our community already has a hurricane prep email we send."**
> Perfect — Storm Shield complements that. Your email tells them WHAT
> to do; Storm Shield gives them the tool to actually do it. Free.

**"Why before June 1?"**
> Documentation has to happen BEFORE a loss to be useful. Once a storm
> hits, the chance to do it is gone.

**"What's the catch?"**
> No catch for the board. The catch for us is we need 1–2 communities
> to prove the model works before we scale. That's the trade.
