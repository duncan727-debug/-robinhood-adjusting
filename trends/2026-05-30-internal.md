## Priority Prospect Targets — by CRM Category

**CRM category:** roofers
**Geo focus:** Palm Beach County county-wide; secondary Broward HVHZ.
**Why now:**

Touch-2 escalation: offer the inspection-referral relationship explicitly. Cite the July 1 HB 815 effective date as the calendar pressure.

### 2. Restoration / Water Mitigation
**CRM category:** restoration, water damage
**Geo focus:** Palm Beach County; inland zips especially (Wellington, Royal Palm Beach, Loxahatchee, Greenacres) — universal Citizens flood mandate at $400K+ already live, so post-storm losses outside the SFHA are increasingly insurable events.
**Why now:**
- Flood mandate on Citizens >$400K is live; Jan 1, 2027 extends it to the full Citizens book. More losses will hit *insured* coverage starting with the first storm cycle.
- SB 266 ($5K-per-act vulnerable-adult solicitation fine effective July 1) reshapes how restoration crews can refer PAs — clean policy is to hand a list of three licensed PAs, not bring one to the door. Surface this as a compliance education touch.
- Pre-season urgency window closing this weekend; restoration crews need priority-customer commercial agreements signed.

**Outreach angle (claim-lead wedge):**
> "With the Citizens flood mandate phasing in this year, more inland water losses are going to be on-the-policy events instead of out-of-pocket. When a homeowner calls me post-loss and needs a mitigation crew on site fast, I want a short list I can hand them. Want to be on it — and want to talk through the SB 266 referral rules before July 1?"
