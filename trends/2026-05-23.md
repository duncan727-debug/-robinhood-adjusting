## Headline Trend of the Week — "Below-Normal Forecast, Above-Normal Stakes"

NOAA's May 21 outlook formally called the 2026 Atlantic season **below-normal** (55% probability; 8–14 named storms, 3–6 hurricanes, 1–3 majors). It is the lowest named-storm range issued since 2014–2015, driven by a developing — possibly "super" — El Niño.

**Why it matters:**
- **Property owners:** "Below-normal" is a probabilistic *season* call, not a *landfall* call. 1992 was officially a below-normal season — and produced Hurricane Andrew. Per-property preparation, documentation, and insurance posture should be unchanged.
- **Service providers:** A quieter forecast historically pulls forward margin pressure as homeowners delay non-emergency work. The June–July off-season inspection-and-maintenance window is the operational opportunity — sold *before* the first named storm sets the tone for the rest of the season.
- **PA industry:** Pre-storm consumer education ("what a legitimate post-storm contract looks like," "what photos to take this weekend") is high-trust, low-friction outreach in a quieter forecast cycle.

**Beneficiary categories:** Roofers, wind-mitigation inspectors, HVAC, general inspectors, water-mitigation, mold remediation.

## Trend 3 — Compliance Pressure on Condos is Now Stacking

Four separate condo obligations are compounding inside the same 12-month window:

1. **Milestone inspection deadline — December 31, 2026** for 25–30+ year buildings 3+ stories. Missing it → Construction Board of Adjustment → potential unsafe-building determination → vacate order.
2. **SIRS reserve mandate live since January 1, 2026** — eight components (roof, load-bearing walls, fire protection, plumbing, electrical, waterproofing, windows/exterior doors, any deferred item >$10K) no longer waivable.
3. **HB 1021 document disclosure** — mandatory for 25+ unit associations as of January 1, 2026.
4. **Fannie/Freddie 15% reserve floor — effective January 4, 2027** — gates conforming-mortgage eligibility for unit resales.

**Why it matters:**
- **Unit owners:** Silence from an association about milestone or SIRS status is itself a flag. Request written timelines this month, not Q4.
- **Real estate professionals:** Milestone inspection status is now the new gating document for showings on 3+ story condos. Listing-side disclosure timing materially affects time-on-market.
- **Service providers:** A multi-year revenue pipeline for roofing, plumbing, electrical, fire-protection, waterproofing, window/door, and structural specialists — driven by *compliance*, not weather.

**Beneficiary categories:** Roofers, plumbers, electricians, fire-protection contractors, waterproofing specialists, window/door installers, structural/concrete-restoration engineers, condo SIRS consultants.

## Trend 4 — Roofing Code is Quietly Resetting

Three roofing-side updates appeared this week, all pointing in the same direction — *prescriptive* compliance is giving way to *performance-based* compliance, and documentation is everything.

- **Miami-Dade RAS 118, 119, 120, 127** updated to remove prescriptive methods in favor of performance-based standards with expanded uplift-resistance tables.
- **FBC 2026 9th Edition** HVHZ roofing approvals reset; self-adhering underlayment mandatory in Miami-Dade/Broward; many pre-2026-approved products carry documentation friction at carrier renewal.
- **Storm-period contract rule** — emergency-declaration roof contracts must now carry consumer-protection disclosures in 14-point bold font; "roofing contractor" definition formally expanded to include roof-to-wall connection inspection on wood-deck structures.
- **OIR-B1-1802 wind-mitigation form** revised effective April 1, 2026 — inspections from before then should be re-verified.

**Why it matters:**
- **Property owners:** A "passed inspection 5+ years ago" wind-mitigation credential is increasingly a paper credential, not a current compliance statement. If the last inspection pre-dates 2023, request a fresh one before renewal — the discount may be larger *and* the paperwork will hold up at claim time.
- **Service providers:** Estimating templates and re-roof scope language need to update *this quarter*. Roof systems that passed prior cycles may no longer comply under performance-based reads, especially older tile underlayments.
- **PA industry:** Roof-to-wall connection scope is now formally inside the roofer's lane — a documentation trail PAs should expect to encounter at every claim review.

**Beneficiary categories:** Roofers (especially HVHZ-licensed), wind-mitigation inspectors, general contractors with NOA documentation discipline.

## Trend 5 — Pre-Season Window Closing Fast

| Date | Inflection |
|---|---|
| **June 1, 2026** | Hurricane season opens; Citizens June rate order effective |
| **July 1, 2026** | HB 815 (roof-age non-renewal bar) + SB 266 (60-day PA written-estimate rule) effective |
| **Dec 31, 2026** | Condo milestone inspection deadline |
| **Jan 4, 2027** | Fannie/Freddie 15% condo reserve floor effective |

The NFIP bind-before-season window is **already closed** — flood policies bound now carry a 30-day waiting period and will not provide coverage for the first 2–3 weeks of the season.

**Why it matters:**
- **Property owners:** The single highest-leverage maintenance window of the year closes May 31. Inspection-and-mitigation work scheduled in June competes with the rest of the state.
- **Service providers:** Capacity at roofers, wind-mitigation inspectors, and mold/water-mitigation crews tightens fast in late May. Booked-out clients in June represent committed revenue that protects against post-storm-rush-only revenue cycles.

**Beneficiary categories:** Roofers, wind-mitigation inspectors, mold remediation, water mitigation, general inspectors.
