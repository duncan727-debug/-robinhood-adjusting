2 · The 7 Patterns That Repeat

1. Boring beats sexy at the top

The 5 highest-revenue businesses outside of food: porta-potties, junk, wipes, handyman, mechanic. Categories competition is allergic to. DIRECT FIT

2. Recurring B2B contracts > one-shot B2C

Bay Area Sanitation, Patsnap, Awardco, Heart Bandits all cluster in the $3M-$200M tier because they sell to customers who renew. Pure DTC plateaus lower.

3. Dual-stream the waste

Junk Teens charges to haul + resells what they haul. Bite by Bite buys junk for $0 and sells refinished for $400. The waste IS the product.

4. Formalize the informal

Repair.sg, Crown's Corner, Rowan, Cobbler Bushwick. Take a category that runs on word-of-mouth + handshakes, add brand + UX + trust signals.

5. Single-SKU launches beat catalogs

Sleepy Hat, Citymouse, Stall Mates — one product, one demo, one TikTok wedge. Catalog comes after PMF, not before.

6. Side-hustle while-employed is the new default

Stall Mates founder still teaches HS. Bay Area Kids Rentals: 8 hr/week. Legit Kits: 1 day/wk. The 2024-26 cohort treats "still working day job" as a strategy, not a stopgap.

7. Acquired skill > credential

~1/3 of founders had zero prior industry experience. Desiree Hill (ex-nurse) Googled how to fix cars. The McKinneys (junk) learned on YouTube.

Bonus: capital efficiency

Of 40, only 3 raised meaningful outside capital. Bootstrap is the genre. The median start was under $10K. FITS YOUR PROFILE

4 · The Answer: Equestrian Waste Management

Wellington is the #1 equestrian destination in the US during winter. The 9,000-acre Equestrian Preserve has hundreds of private horse properties + Palm Beach International Equestrian Center hosting 12 weeks of WEF (12,000+ horses) + International Polo Club. Each horse produces ~50 lbs/day of manure. Combined: ~50,000 tons/yr that must be hauled by a licensed operator.

Why this beats everything else

  • Regulatory moat. Wellington Code requires all livestock waste to be removed by an approved hauler, containerized and covered. Wellington Manure Regs & BMP. Customers can't legally DIY past a tiny threshold.
  • Fragmented, contested incumbents. Five existing manure haulers are publicly feuding with PBC municipalities right now over routing favoritism — meaning the market has demand, the brands are weak, and the government is unhappy with the current providers. WFLX, Jan 2026.
  • Wellington gov is courting entrants. The Village sent Agricultural Blending Company a business plan to dispose of/reuse manure and reduce illegal dumping. Translation: the local government will help you launch.
  • Recurring, sticky, weekly contracts. Bay Area Sanitation's exact model. Barn owners need pickup 1-3x/week, every week, forever.
  • Dual revenue stream. Junk Teens' trick: don't just charge to haul — process and resell as composted "Wellington Stable Blend" to PBC landscapers, nurseries, and HOAs (the buyer market you already touch via your B2B referral ecosystem).
  • AI-proof, recession-proof. Horses don't stop producing manure when ChatGPT releases v6 or a recession hits.

The concept (one-liner)

"Wellington Stable Co." — premium equestrian waste service for the Preserve. Sealed dumpsters at the barn, scheduled weekly pickup, composted on-site at an Ag-zoned partner parcel, resold as branded soil amendment to landscapers and nurseries. Tagline: "The barn-to-garden loop."

5 · The Two Backup Plays (if equestrian is a "no")

B+ Premium Storm-Debris Hauling

Strongest pitch: pre-sold seasonal subscription — "Hurricane Ready Subscription" billed Apr-Nov, includes pre-storm prep + post-storm priority haul. Recurringizes a seasonal category.

B Wellington Event Sanitation

Direct Bay Area Sanitation clone. WEF, polo, equestrian shows, Wellington Amphitheater concerts, Chamber events — all need porta-potty + handwash + waste. ~$60K to start (1 truck, 50 units). But: more competitors and lower regulatory moat than manure.

Combo play: bundle event sanitation with equestrian manure under one brand. Same trucks, same labor pool, same compost endpoint.
