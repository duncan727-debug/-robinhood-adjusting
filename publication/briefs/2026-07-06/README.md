## Lead

South Florida starts the week with a market split worth watching: luxury demand is still firm while the broader market remains price-sensitive. That is the business story today. Insurance friction, roof age, mitigation records, flood exposure, and permit history can still decide whether a strong-looking deal actually closes.

## Top Stories

### Miami luxury prices are still running hot while non-luxury softens

Redfin's May luxury-market report says U.S. luxury home prices rose 4.7% year over year, more than triple the 1.5% gain for non-luxury homes. Miami ranked second among the 50 largest metros for luxury price growth, with luxury prices up 14.2% year over year while non-luxury prices fell 0.7%. Redfin also reported Florida strength in luxury pending sales: West Palm Beach was up 18.5% and Miami was up 14.6%.

**Implication:** high-end sellers and agents have a stronger story than the rest of the market, but insurance and inspection friction still matter. For waterfront, older, or heavily renovated properties, clean roof, wind mitigation, flood, permit, and prior-claim documentation can protect deal momentum.

Source: [Redfin luxury housing market report](https://www.redfin.com/news/luxury-housing-market-may-2026/) via [MIAMI Realtors July 6 roundup](https://www.miamirealtors.com/2026/07/06/monday-july-6-2026-south-florida-beyond-the-headlines-news-to-share/)

### New York and Miami CRE ties keep deepening

Bisnow's South Florida report describes more commercial real estate professionals splitting time between New York and Miami, with Miami's tax position, Latin America connectivity, office demand, and high-density development pipeline continuing to pull capital and operators south. The report notes that corporate migration has pushed Brickell office rents close to Manhattan levels, and that Miami-Dade added more than 100,000 residents from 2020 to 2025.

**Implication:** real estate and service-provider opportunity is no longer just seasonal or residential. More capital, denser projects, and out-of-market operators mean more demand for risk documentation, maintenance records, waterproofing, roof condition, HVAC resilience, and claim-ready vendor relationships.

Source: [Bisnow South Florida CRE migration report](https://www.bisnow.com/south-florida/news/commercial-real-estate/new-york-miami-migration-story-evolving-what-it-looks-like-to-live-it-135266)

### Loss environment: quiet tropics make this a documentation week

The National Hurricane Center's July 6 Atlantic outlook reported that tropical cyclone formation was not expected during the next seven days for the North Atlantic, Caribbean Sea, and Gulf of America. That is not headline drama; it is operational breathing room.

**Implication:** use the quiet week to clean up files. Homeowners should take dated property photos and videos now; agents should update insurance and mitigation files for active listings; contractors should document pre-existing conditions before starting exterior, roof, drainage, or water-intrusion work.

Source: [NHC Atlantic Tropical Weather Outlook archive](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/mobile/text/refresh/MIATWOAT%2Bhtml/061136_MIATWOAT.html)

### Insurance transparency rule is the policy item to keep on the radar

Florida's HB 767 analysis says residential property insurers seeking a rate change after October 1, 2026 must prepare a plain-language rate transparency report. The report must break down factors affecting the rate, including reinsurance, claims costs, and profits, and must be included with offers of coverage and renewals. The analysis also says insurers may not include land value when establishing coverage amount or adjudicating a claim for a dwelling or other structure.

**Implication:** this is not today's renewal fix, but it is a useful talking point for fall renewals. Consumers should be trained to ask for the "why" behind rate changes, and claim files should separate structure value from land value cleanly.

Source: [Florida Senate HB 767 analysis](https://www.flsenate.gov/Session/Bill/2026/767/Analyses/h0767a.IBS.PDF)

## Segment Notes

### Homeowners

Do not treat a quiet Atlantic as a reason to wait. Take photos now: roof from the ground, ceilings, windows, doors, AC condenser, pool equipment, fence, garage door, electrical panel, and every room. Save a copy outside your phone.

### Real Estate Professionals

Luxury demand is strong enough to hide weak documentation until inspection, insurance, or financing exposes it. Make insurance, roof age, permits, mitigation, and flood information part of the listing file early.

### Service Providers

This is a good week for documentation-heavy messaging: pre-work photos, written scopes, moisture readings, roof condition notes, permit screening, and post-work closeout packets.
