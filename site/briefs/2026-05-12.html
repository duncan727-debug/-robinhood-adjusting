INSURANCE & REGULATION

New Public Adjuster Regulation: Vulnerable Adults Can Rescind Contracts (Effective July 1)

Florida Legislature has enacted new consumer protections for vulnerable populations. Effective July 1, 2026, vulnerable adults, adults lacking capacity to provide informed consent, and their legal representatives may now rescind contracts with public adjusters within a specified window.

Why it matters: This adds compliance complexity for PA firms, particularly in multi-generational households and estates. Firms should review contract language to ensure informed-consent documentation is robust and timely notice procedures are in place. This regulation reinforces the industry's trend toward transparency and fiduciary accountability.

Roofing Contractor Scope Expansion: Roof-to-Wall Connections Now Licensed Contractor Work

Clarification on SB 808 (Effective July 1): Roofing contractors in Florida are now explicitly authorized to evaluate and enhance roof-to-wall connections on structures with wood roof decking. This scope expansion opens retrofit and repair opportunities that were previously outside standard roofing work.

Why it matters: This creates new revenue streams for roofing contractors during roof inspections and replacement work. Wind-mitigation consulting now encompasses connection upgrades, not just covering material replacement. Public adjusters should familiarize themselves with roof-to-wall requirements when reviewing roof damage claims involving wood-deck structures.
