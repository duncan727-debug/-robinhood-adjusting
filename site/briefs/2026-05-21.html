INSURANCE & REGULATION

Florida Condo Milestone Inspection Deadline — December 31, 2026, Now ~7 Months Out

[Compounding Compliance Pressure — Newly Surfaced Angle] Florida's milestone inspection law (post-Surfside) requires structural evaluations of condominium and cooperative buildings 3+ stories. For buildings 25–30+ years old, the hard deadline is December 31, 2026. The Structural Integrity Reserve Study (SIRS) can be completed simultaneously with the milestone inspection.

The eight SIRS-mandatory reserve components (no longer waivable as of January 1, 2026):

1. Roof

2. Load-bearing walls and primary structural members

3. Fire protection

4. Plumbing

5. Electrical

6. Waterproofing

7. Windows and exterior doors

8. Any other item with a deferred-maintenance or replacement cost exceeding $10,000

Consequence of missing the deadline: Referral to the Construction Board of Adjustment and Appeals → potential unsafe building determination → vacate order forcing residents out.

Stacked condo-owner pressure (May 2026 snapshot):

  • 2024 reserve-funding mandate (live; full funding required as of Jan 1, 2026)
  • HB 1021 document-disclosure requirement (live; 25+ unit threshold)
  • Milestone inspection deadline (Dec 31, 2026)
  • Fannie/Freddie 15% reserve floor (Jan 4, 2027) — covered May 20

Practical angle for PAs and advisors: This is the inflection point in 2026 where missed deadlines stop being theoretical and start producing vacate orders, financing freezes, and unit-resale obstruction. Unit owners whose associations are quiet about milestone or SIRS status should request written timelines this month, not Q4.

Storm-Chaser Contractor Window Opens at June 1 — New 14-Point Bold Font Rule on Emergency Roofing Contracts

[Newly Surfaced Operational Item] Florida's updated roofing-contractor rules tighten storm-period contract requirements. When a contractor executes a roof replacement or repair contract during a declared state of emergency, the contract must include consumer-protection disclosures in at least 14-point bold font. The definition of "roofing contractor" has also been revised to specifically include evaluation and enhancement of roof-to-wall connections for structures with wood roof decking.

Why this matters this week:

  • The state-of-emergency contract rule kicks in the moment a tropical system triggers a declaration. Contractor practices observed June 1+ should be expected to comply.
  • Roof-to-wall connection inspections are now formally within the roofing-contractor scope of practice — that maps directly onto the OIR-B1-1802 wind mitigation form (revised effective April 1, 2026). Roofers and PAs reviewing inspection paperwork should verify the inspector is qualified to evaluate roof-to-wall connections on wood-deck structures.
  • License verification stays the single best fraud screen: myfloridalicense.com is the free state database.

PA/advisory angle: Build a one-page client handout: "What a legitimate post-storm roofing contract looks like — and three red flags." Distribute via newsletter Friday or Monday post-NOAA-outlook. Pre-storm consumer education is high-trust, low-friction outreach.

CONTRACTORS & SERVICE INDUSTRIES

Hurricane Roof Prep Window — 11 Days to Season Start

[Practical Operating Window] The single highest-leverage maintenance window for South Florida property owners closes May 31. Roofing contractors with capacity should be in inspection-heavy mode through end-of-month.

The four roof prep items most likely to surface in post-storm claim friction:

1. Loose or corroded flashing — the most common single source of post-storm leak entry, even when shingles look intact.

2. Roof-to-wall connection documentation — now formally within the roofing-contractor scope; matters for OIR-B1-1802 wind mitigation credit.

3. Self-adhering underlayment in HVHZ counties (Miami-Dade, Broward) — mandatory under FBC 2026.

4. Recent re-roof product approvals — anything installed under pre-FBC-2026 approval may carry documentation friction at carrier renewal.

Operational tip — pre-storm photography: Property owners should take dated, geo-tagged photos of every elevation of the roof, all flashings, and the roof-to-wall transition zones before June 1. If a storm hits, those photos become the baseline that proves new damage, not pre-existing wear. This is a 30-minute homeowner exercise that materially strengthens claim construction.
