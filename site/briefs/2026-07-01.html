South Florida Property & Insurance Industry Brief

July 1, 2026 | Palm Beach, Miami-Dade, Broward, Martin, St. Lucie

Today's signal: July 1 is a real reset point. Citizens rate cuts begin for new policyholders and flow through at renewal, the Atlantic is quiet, HB 803 changes small-project permitting, and Live Local continues to turn older office inventory into multifamily pipeline.

TOP STORIES

Citizens July 1 rate cuts take effect

Citizens' approved 2026 rates take effect today for new policyholders and apply to existing policyholders at renewal: average 8.8% down for homeowners multiperil, average 5.5% down for homeowners wind-only, and at least 2% down for all Citizens personal-lines policyholders. Citizens says its count is down to 336,000 policies from a 2023 peak of 1.41 million.

Implication: homeowners should check renewal timing, real estate professionals should update stale insurance estimates, and service providers should use renewal season to push documentation and mitigation conversations.

Source: Citizens Property Insurance

NHC: no Atlantic tropical cyclones today

The National Hurricane Center's July 1 update shows no tropical cyclones in the Atlantic and no active Atlantic disturbances on the outlook.

Implication: this is a clean pre-loss documentation window. The message for homeowners is not fear; it is preparation while the map is quiet.

Source: National Hurricane Center

HB 803 changes building permit rules

HB 803 takes effect today. It requires local governments, except in flood hazard areas, to exempt certain single-family residential work valued at $7,500 or less from building permitting, excluding electrical, plumbing, structural, mechanical, and gas work. It also addresses temporary residential hurricane/flood walls, private providers, permit applications, and HOA review prerequisites.

Implication: useful for small non-structural work, but dangerous if oversold. Contractors need a written scope screen before promising permit relief.

Source: Florida Senate HB 803 summary

Live Local update plus Aventura project

HB 1389, Florida's latest Live Local update, takes effect today. The Faith Group also proposed a 376-unit Live Local Act rental project near Aventura, replacing an older office/medical building. The proposal fits a broader South Florida pattern: aging suburban offices are increasingly redevelopment candidates.

Implication: real estate pros should re-underwrite older office sites; trades should track conversion pipelines for future MEP, roofing, fire, waterproofing, low-voltage, and maintenance work.

Sources: Florida Senate HB 1389 summary · The Real Deal
