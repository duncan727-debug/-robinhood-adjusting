A Palm Beach Gardens bond agent allegedly pocketed contractors' surety premiums — verify your bonds today

This one is directly about your money. A licensed property-casualty agent in Palm Beach Gardens — Checree Bryant, 51, of Innovative Bonding Services — was arrested and charged with embezzlement, fraud, and larceny. Per the Florida Department of Financial Services affidavit, she collected more than $90,000 from a roofing company and a construction firm for three contractor surety bonds and never sent the premiums to the surety (Old Republic Surety Co.). The contractors thought they were bonded. They weren't.

Investigators believe she "participated in a systematic, ongoing course of conduct with intent to defraud," and may have targeted contractors over several months. Hearings are set for July 5 and July 30.

The 15-minute fix for your shop:

1. Pull every active bond you carry and call the surety company directly — not the agent — to confirm the bond is in force and premium was received.

2. Run your agent through the DFS license search (licenseesearch.fldfs.com) and check their active carrier appointments, not just the license. Bryant's license was still showing valid this week — the inactive Old Republic appointment was the tell.

3. Going forward, ask for the surety's confirmation or rider directly from the carrier on any new bond.

If a bonded job had failed while one of those contractors was unknowingly unbonded, the exposure lands on the contractor — that's the whole point of doing this check now.

Source: Insurance Journal

Roof-age reform is dead — the replacement market stays hot

HB 815 died in committee March 13 and is confirmed dead for 2026, per Florida Senate records. That's the bill that would have stopped carriers from refusing to write or renew a policy solely because of roof age, and given homeowners an inspector-certified path to keep an older roof.

Pipeline read for roofers and GCs: the status quo holds. Carriers can keep using roof age as an underwriting trigger, which means the "my insurer says replace it or lose coverage" call keeps coming all renewal season. Two practical angles:

  • Wind-mitigation and roof-condition inspections remain the gatekeeper documents. If you're a roofer not offering inspection reports (or partnered with someone who does), you're handing the first customer touch to a competitor.
  • The PA-contract bill (SB 266) died the same day, but it had cleared its first committee 10-0 — expect both bills back in the 2027 session. Nothing changes July 1.

Source: HB 815, Florida Senate · SB 266

First disturbance of the season is on the map — your pre-storm window is open

The National Hurricane Center is tracking the season's first Atlantic-basin system: a Bay of Campeche low with a 10% formation chance and no Florida threat — it likely dies over Mexico this weekend, though NHC notes it could re-emerge over the northwestern Gulf midweek.

The signal for your business isn't this system — it's the calendar. Once the first named storm forms, two things happen fast: material lead times stretch, and homeowners start calling about prep work all at once. Mid-June is the window to:

  • Lock supplier pricing on fasteners, underlayment, tarps, and shutters before the first run on materials.
  • Push pre-storm inspection/documentation packages to your maintenance customers — dated roof and exterior condition reports protect them at claim time and keep your crews busy in the shoulder weeks.
  • Confirm your own COIs, bonds (see above), and license CE status — the statewide construction-trades CE deadline is August 31.

Source: NHC Atlantic outlook

Brickell keeps building — 300 more units on the Citadel campus

Two Miami-Dade signals worth filing (The Real Deal, June 11):

  • Ken Griffin expanded the planned Citadel Brickell supertall to add roughly 300 apartments plus parking, alongside more office space. That's another large post-CO maintenance, HVAC, and plumbing contract pipeline forming for late decade.
  • JDS Development brought on Jeff Soffer as a partner on the Mercedes-Benz Miami condo project while arranging a $1 billion loan; a project affiliate has been in foreclosure litigation with its lender. Recapitalizations like this often re-bid subcontractor packages — worth watching if you work commercial.

Sources: TRD / Griffin · TRD / JDS-Soffer
