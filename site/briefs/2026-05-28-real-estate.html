CORAL GABLES TROPHY TAPE IS THE COMP SET FOR THE SEASON

Two Coral Gables waterfront closings this week reset the comp set for $20M+ Miami-Dade product:

  • 200 Casuarina Concourse — ~10,200-sq-ft waterfront mansion closed at $25.4 million. The 2020 trade was $12.3M — a 106% appreciation in five to six years.
  • 665 Leucadendra Drive — 7,100 sq ft inside Gables Estates closed at $24.3 million, working out to ~$3,200 per square foot.

The takeaway for listing presentations on Coral Gables waterfront product this summer: the $3,000–$3,500/sf band is now the working floor for Gables Estates, not the ceiling. For buyer-side conversations in Miami-Dade trophy, the cleanest comp framework remains bay-front Gables versus Pinecrest dry-lot — the two markets have decoupled materially in the last 18 months.

On the commercial side, El-Ad National Properties picked up a 25-unit condominium complex at 3265 Virginia Street, Miami for $45.5 million — $1.82M per unit. Institutional pricing on a 25-door cap stack is the signal that well-inspected (post-Surfside milestone-compliant) mid-rise condo product is trading on income, not on discount-to-replacement. Worth tracking for your buyers looking at condo-conversion or value-add positioning.

MORTGAGE RATES: THE 6.5% WORLD IS THE BASE CASE FOR THE NEXT 30 DAYS

Freddie Mac held the 30-year fixed at 6.51% — highest since August 2025. Daily trackers show the average 30-year purchase rate at 6.704% by May 22. The 15-year fixed is 5.85%.

The driver has shifted. Through Q1 the move was Fed expectations and CPI prints. The latest leg is the Middle East oil-risk premium working through into headline inflation, which is pulling 10-year Treasury yields up, which drags mortgage rates with them. The Federal Reserve cannot directly counteract supply-shock inflation. Plan on 6.5%–6.75% for at least the next 30 days.

Two postures that work this week:

  • Buyer-side: Lead with buydown structures and seller-paid concession strategies, not rate predictions. Florida wholesale lenders are quoting more aggressive 2-1 buydown programs to keep deals moving. An effective 4.5%-in-year-one rate buys time for the longer-rate cycle to turn.
  • Seller-side: Recut the time-on-market math at $500K–$900K inventory. A 25-bp move in rates moves the qualifying-buyer pool meaningfully at the conforming-loan-limit boundary. Listings priced six weeks ago against a 6.20% expectation are no longer priced to today's qualified buyer.

Sources: Freddie Mac PMMS · Florida Realtors — Mortgage Rates Climb to Nine-Month High

SB 266 — A DISCLOSURE NOTE FOR STORM-DAMAGED LISTINGS AND ESTATE SALES

Senate Bill 266 (2026) takes effect July 1 and rewrites the rescission rights around public-adjuster contracts for protected populations:

  • Vulnerable adults, adults who lack capacity to consent, and their legal representatives can rescind a PA contract at any time, without penalty. The 30-day post-loss / 10-day post-signing rescission window remains the floor for everyone else.
  • The Department of Financial Services can fine PAs up to $5,000 per act for soliciting vulnerable adults.

Two places this matters for your practice:

  • Storm-damaged inventory listings: If the seller's family has signed a PA contract on an open claim, July 1 may change the timeline for claim resolution before closing. Worth a 10-minute call with the seller's family to confirm posture.
  • Estate sales and guardianship transactions: Confirm the status of any open insurance claim and any public-adjuster contract attached to it before listing. A rescinded PA contract mid-listing can shift the claim's settlement timeline by 60–120 days, with knock-on effects on the listing's marketing posture.

Source: Florida Senate — SB 266 (2026)

INSURANCE-BINDING REMINDER FOR JUNE AND JULY CLOSINGS

The standard pattern: most Florida carriers stop binding new wind policies 24–72 hours before a named storm enters the Florida cone. For closings scheduled June and July, the safe practice is to bind insurance on day one of contract, not on day-of-funding. The NHC outlook is clean today — zero disturbances, zero formation expected in 7 days — but the cone-watch posture changes weekly from June 1 forward.

For buyers asking "what will my insurance cost?", the 20 new property-and-casualty carriers approved post-reform — including Builder Reciprocal, Frontline Reciprocal, and Wingsail — give you a wider quoting shelf than at any point in the last four years. Maintain a current short list of two or three independent agents who actively quote the new entrants.
