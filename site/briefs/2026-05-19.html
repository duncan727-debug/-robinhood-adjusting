Citizens' Next Rate Cycle: 8.8% Multiperil + 5.5% Wind-Only Cuts Lock In for July 1

[New Data — Second Wave of Reductions] Most coverage to date has focused on the spring 2026 Citizens cuts now hitting renewal letters. The next leg is now confirmed: Citizens' 2026 multiperil filing was approved at an average 8.8% reduction effective July 1, 2026, with wind-only policies down 5.5%. Roughly three out of five Citizens personal lines policyholders are receiving an average $359 reduction (about 11.5%) in the new cycle.

Why this matters separately from the spring cuts: Spring 2026 renewals already in mailboxes reflect the earlier 8.7% statewide cycle. The July 1 filing is a second, distinct round — meaning Citizens policyholders renewing July onward will see compounded relief. Coastal South Florida counties (Broward, Miami-Dade, Palm Beach, Monroe) were already approved for the largest cuts in that earlier round (11.3%–14.1%), so the July 1 layer stacks on top of the deepest reductions in the state.

Operational angle PAs and agents should use this week — the "20% rule": Under Citizens' depopulation mechanics, if every private take-out offer is 20% or more above the Citizens renewal estimate, the policyholder may stay with Citizens. If any private offer is less than 20% higher (or comes in lower), the policyholder must accept private placement. With Citizens cutting another 8.8% in July, the 20% comparison bar moves down — more policyholders will become eligible to stay with Citizens at renewal than at any point in the last three years. This reverses the depopulation pressure for many South Florida homes and is worth surfacing in any consult triggered by a depopulation letter.

Florida's Hurricane Prep Tax Holiday Is Gone — But the Savings Are Now Permanent Year-Round

[New Policy Reality — Communicate to Clients] The two annual disaster-preparedness sales tax holidays Florida property owners have relied on for the last decade will not return in 2026. Legislation signed in 2025 made the underlying exemptions permanent, so commonly-stocked emergency supplies are tax-free every day of the year, not just during the two windows.

Items now permanently exempt from Florida sales tax (effective August 1, 2025):

  • Portable generators (any wattage)
  • Batteries, fire extinguishers, smoke and CO detectors
  • Tarps, ground anchors, tie-down kits
  • Portable gas/diesel cans, life jackets
  • Insect repellent and sunscreen

Why this matters: Property owners and contractor customers who waited for the May–June holiday to stock up are likely unaware the format changed. The savings are still real — they're just delayed-purchase-friendly now. Service providers should update any client-facing prep checklists, blog posts, and email collateral that reference "tax holiday dates" — those references are now stale and signal out-of-date material.

Advisory angle for PAs: Use this as a no-cost client check-in. A "your hurricane prep checklist for 2026 — what changed" message lands in the inbox as helpful rather than promotional, and the format change is a credible reason to reach out.

NFIP Risk Rating 2.0: 18% Annual Cap Means Many Florida Homes Still Have Years of Increases Ahead

[Operational Detail Worth Surfacing] Even with the headline relief story dominating Florida property coverage, NFIP flood premiums are still climbing under FEMA's Risk Rating 2.0 — and many South Florida policies have years of increases left before reaching their full-risk rate.

The mechanics:

  • Primary residences: capped at 18% annual increase
  • Non-primary residences / business properties: capped at 25% annual increase
  • Increases continue every renewal until the policy hits full risk-based rate — could take 5+ more years for historically underpriced coastal homes

HB 1021: Condo Document-Disclosure Now Mandatory for 25+ Unit Associations (Effective Jan 1, 2026)

[Quietly Material — South Florida-Heavy Impact] HB 1021 dropped the document-disclosure threshold for condo associations from 150+ units down to 25+ units. The change took effect January 1, 2026, but compliance has been uneven. Associations must now provide owners access to governing documents, financials, structural integrity reserve studies, and meeting minutes via website or app.

Why South Florida is the epicenter:

  • Special assessments of $10,000–$100,000+ per unit have become common since the Surfside collapse triggered the 2024 reserve-funding mandate.
  • Special assessment volume is forecast to peak in 2026–2028 as backlogged structural integrity reserve studies clear.
  • South Florida's condo stock skews older, coastal, and structurally complex — exactly the inventory hitting the heaviest assessments.

PA opportunity: Condo unit owners hit with special assessments are simultaneously dealing with wind, water, mold, and structural damage claims under their unit-owner HO-6 policies. The intersection between association master policy coverage and unit-owner policy coverage is the single most under-served advisory space in South Florida right now. Reposition condo unit-owner consults as a policy-stack review rather than a claim-handling pitch.
