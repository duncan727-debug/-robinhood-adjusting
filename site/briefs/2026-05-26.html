TOP STORIES

30-Year Mortgage Rate Climbs to 6.51% — Highest in Nine Months, Up 15 bps in One Week

Freddie Mac's Primary Mortgage Market Survey released last week put the 30-year fixed at 6.51%, up from 6.36% the prior week — a 15 basis-point weekly move and the highest level since August 28, 2025 (6.56%). The 15-year fixed climbed to 5.85% from 5.71%. The driver is Fed-minutes-driven rate-cut deferral; markets had been pricing in earlier 2026 cuts that the latest minutes pushed back.

What it means for homeowners: If you are sitting on a 6.0%–6.3% pre-approval letter that is more than 30 days old, re-lock today rather than tomorrow. On a $500,000 mortgage, the move from 6.36% to 6.51% adds roughly $48/month ($17,300 over the life of the loan). For homeowners considering a cash-out refi to fund roof, impact-window, or HVAC upgrades, the math is now tighter — a HELOC or unsecured improvement loan may pencil better than a full refi.

What it means for real estate professionals: Buyers who were on the fence at 6.36% are now genuinely re-running their DTI. Expect a second wave of price-reduction conversations on listings that have been on market 30+ days, particularly in the $500K–$900K Palm Beach/Broward inventory band where the rate move directly compresses qualifying purchase price.

What it means for service providers: Higher rates push more remodel-in-place decisions versus move-up purchases. Kitchen, bath, impact-window, and roof contractors should expect a measurable summer pickup in homeowner-financed upgrade work — but lean on cash and HELOC channels in the financing conversation, not first-mortgage refis.

Source: Florida Realtors — Mortgage Rates Climb to Nine-Month High | Freddie Mac PMMS

Florida Luxury Market is the Counter-Narrative — Condos $1M+ Up 41% YoY in Q1, $5M–$10M Single-Family Up 31%

While the broader market wrestles with rates, the Florida luxury segment is running hot. Florida Realtors Chief Economist Brad O'Connor's Q1 2026 closed-sales analysis:

Segment Q1 2026 YoY Change
Single-family $1M+ **+14%**
Single-family $5M–$10M (ultra-luxury) **+31%**
Condo/townhouse $1M+ (overall) **+41%**
Condo/townhouse $1.5M–$2M **+61%**
Condo/townhouse $3M–$5M **+69%**

O'Connor's summary: "Luxury seems to largely be doing very well right now." The condo strength is the most surprising line — the segment has been depressed since the post-Surfside milestone-inspection requirements pushed master-policy premiums and assessment expectations up sharply. The Q1 jump suggests buyers are now pricing those costs into offers rather than walking from them.

What it means for real estate professionals: The $1M–$5M condo conversation has materially changed in 90 days. Listings that sat through Q4 2025 with reduced-price relists are clearing. Brokers carrying $1.5M–$3M Brickell, Boca, Singer Island, and Sunny Isles inventory should refresh comps before the next price-reduction memo to the seller.

What it means for homeowners (luxury sellers): If you've been waiting for a market signal to list, this is one. The data is statewide but South Florida historically over-indexes the luxury condo segment — local execution should beat the statewide print.

What it means for service providers: High-end finishing trades (millwork, stone, lighting, AV, smart-home integration) should re-engage their luxury-condo broker relationships. Concierge-grade impact-window retrofit, roof, and waterproofing work is downstream of these closings as new owners begin renovations.

Source: Florida Realtors — Florida Luxury Market Surges to Start 2026

Florida Housing Market at an "Inflection Point" Heading Into Summer — April 2026 Mirrors Two Different Past Forks

April 2026 statewide single-family data: closed sales up 2.5% year-over-year, new pending sales up 8%, eight consecutive months of annual sales gains, and median time on market at 44 days. Florida Realtors Chief Economist Brad O'Connor's flag: this exact statistical profile has been seen twice before — in April 2019 and April 2025 — and the summers that followed went in opposite directions. 2019 stabilized, with time on market holding through the season. 2025 deteriorated, with time on market growing significantly through the summer.

O'Connor's question, quoted directly: "So one question we might ask ourselves is, which path will 2026 take?"

What it means for real estate professionals: The next 90 days of local data — June, July, August — will determine the back-half-of-2026 listing strategy. Weekly track of time-on-market and price-cut percentages in your specific submarket beats the statewide print. If your zip code is trending toward the 2025 pattern (lengthening DOM, more cuts), pull pricing forward on new listings; if 2019 pattern (stable DOM, fewer cuts), hold.

What it means for homeowners: If you are thinking about listing this summer, the next two weekends of showings are diagnostic. Soft traffic and weak feedback in early June are an early-warning signal — adjust price before the July 4 holiday rather than after.

Boca Raton Sets New Single-Family Record at $75M — 2500 East Maya Drive in Royal Palm Yacht & Country Club

The Dingle brothers (SRD Building) closed the sale of their spec mansion at 2500 East Maya Drive in Royal Palm Yacht & Country Club for $75 million (listed at $80M). The 18,300-sqft, 8-bed, 10-bath / 5-half-bath home sits on 0.8 acres with 292 feet of water frontage. The transaction more than doubles the previous Boca Raton single-family record of $36 million — coincidentally, the same 1.7-acre parcel the Dingles purchased in 2024 and then split into two lots. David Roberts of Royal Palm Properties represented both sides.

What it means for real estate professionals: Two implications. First: spec-build economics in Royal Palm Yacht just got reset. Lot acquisition basis up to ~$15M for a 1-acre water-front parcel is now defensible inside the build pro forma. Second: the comp curve for the Boca ultra-coastal segment has stepped — listings priced under the old record should be revisited for selective upward repositioning.

What it means for service providers: High-end Boca custom-build demand is being validated by exit prices. Mechanical, millwork, audio-visual, and specialty pool/spa subcontractors with Royal Palm portfolios should be in active outreach to the next ring of SRD-tier builders (Onshore Construction, SRD itself, Mark Timothy, Albanese-Popkin).

World Cup Is Pulling Foreign Buyers Into Miami Pre-Sales — Latin America = 86% of Foreign Activity

A Real Deal feature confirms what brokers have been quietly reporting all spring: Miami's seven FIFA World Cup 2026 matches at Hard Rock Stadium are accelerating foreign-buyer activity on pre-construction condo inventory. Developer Domus Brickell Park is packaging World Cup ticket pairs with contract signings as a closing-incentive. Surf Row in Surfside is nearly 50% pre-sold at ~$2,000/sqft.

A late-2025 industry study covering 66 South Florida new-development projects found foreign buyers accounted for more than 50% of new-development condo sales between January 2024 and October 2025. Of that foreign share, Latin Americans (Colombia, Mexico, Argentina, Brazil) were 86%; the European balance came mostly from the U.K., Spain, and Germany. The weakening dollar is the secondary tailwind.

What it means for real estate professionals: If you have not added one Portuguese- or Spanish-speaking referral partner in the last 12 months, that is the gap to close before the World Cup window peaks in June/July. The contract-incentive game (tickets, club access, transfers) is the active sales channel right now — match it on co-broke deals.

What it means for service providers: Pre-construction condo absorption is forward demand for finish-out trades 18–30 months from closing. Crews with developer-amenity portfolios (clubrooms, pool decks, lobby finishes) should be tracking Surf Row, Elle Residences, Perigon, and Cipriani Residences Miami construction schedules — the GC bid windows arrive long before COs.

WEATHER & STORM PREPAREDNESS

Atlantic Basin Stays Quiet — Zero Disturbances on NHC 7-Day Outlook, 6 Days Until Season Opener

The National Hurricane Center's tropical weather outlook this morning shows no active tropical cyclones in the Atlantic basin and zero disturbances on either the 2-day or 7-day forecast. The 2026 Atlantic Hurricane Season begins June 1, 2026 — six days from today.

What it means for homeowners: This is the last week of "do it before the season starts" prep that actually has slack in it. The three tasks that matter most:

1. Photograph and date every room and the exterior of your home; back the photos up to a cloud account that does not live on your local device.

2. Confirm your wind, flood, and (where applicable) HO-6 master policy declarations are accurate and in force — call your agent now, not on the day a cone shows up.

3. Check your hurricane shutters or impact windows mechanically — exercise the panels, test the cranks, verify the bolt sets and tracks are clean.

What it means for service providers: The pre-season inspection / re-roof / shutter-install booking calendar is in its final usable week before homeowner urgency disappears into the first storm cycle. After June 1, the conversation shifts from "let's plan an upgrade" to "can you tarp this thing by Thursday." Push final pre-season campaigns this week.

What it means for real estate professionals: Buyers under contract with June or July closings should be reminded that most carriers stop binding new wind policies 24–72 hours before a named storm enters or is forecast to enter the Florida cone of uncertainty. Bind early. A "we'll handle insurance after we get to inspection" plan is fragile from June 1 forward.

Source: NOAA National Hurricane Center
