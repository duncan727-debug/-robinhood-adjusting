Your July 1 Market Brief — Real Estate Professional Edition

July 1, 2026 | South Florida

Citizens cuts are live — update buyer insurance assumptions

Citizens' 2026 decrease takes effect today for new policyholders and at renewal for existing policies: average 8.8% down on homeowners multiperil and 5.5% down on homeowners wind-only. This does not mean every active buyer suddenly has lower carrying costs today, but it does mean stale insurance estimates may now overstate the monthly number.

Closing-table use: for Citizens-eligible properties, ask the insurance agent to quote against the July 1 rate set and confirm whether the buyer's policy is new or renewal-based. For seller-side older-roof listings, insurance remains a diligence item; the rate cut does not solve roof-age underwriting.

Source: Citizens Property Insurance

Live Local keeps pushing office-to-rentals math

Florida's 2026 Live Local update takes effect today. The Senate summary says the law expands where affordable housing preemptions can apply, including certain county, municipal, school district, and religious-institution properties, and limits local governments from using dimensional controls such as setbacks or stepbacks to constructively restrict project height.

The local example landed today: The Faith Group proposed replacing an older Aventura-area office/medical building with a 376-unit Live Local Act rental project. The Real Deal notes older suburban offices are increasingly being targeted for multifamily redevelopment as tenants favor newer space.

Broker read: aging office inventory near Aventura, Boca, Pembroke Pines, Edgewater, and similar corridors is no longer just an office comp. It may be a housing land play, which affects valuation, assemblage conversations, and neighboring residential supply.

Sources: Florida Senate HB 1389 summary · The Real Deal / Aventura Live Local project

Today's deal tape: luxury still clears, but insurance questions stay in the package

The Real Deal's July 1 top-deals report has a Fort Lauderdale home at 100 Royal Palm Drive selling for $12 million, a Miami home selling for $10.8 million, a Miami Beach home selling for $10.3 million, and a Boca Raton home selling for about $9.5 million.

What it means: high-end single-family liquidity is still there, but buyers in this price band expect clean insurance, roof, flood, wind-mitigation, and pre-closing documentation. A listing package with current roof/wind/flood detail reduces friction and gives the agent better control of the narrative.

Source: The Real Deal / June 30 top deals

This week's checklist

1. Re-quote Citizens-linked insurance estimates using the July 1 rate set.

2. Flag older suburban office assets that may pencil as Live Local conversion sites.

3. For luxury listings, update roof, flood, wind-mitigation, and carrier documentation before buyer diligence.
