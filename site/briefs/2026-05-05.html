Realtor Industry Consolidation: Miami & South Florida Merger Creates World's Largest Local Association (Effective May 11)

The real estate industry in South Florida is undergoing a historic structural shift. The Miami Association of Realtors is merging with Broward, Palm Beaches & St. Lucie Realtors, effective May 11, 2026, to create a unified regional association spanning 93,000 members—the largest local Realtor association in the world. The new entity, Miami and South Florida REALTORS® (pending NAR approval), consolidates the MLS, marketing infrastructure, and member services across Palm Beach, Broward, Miami-Dade, St. Lucie, and surrounding areas into a single operating platform.

Operational impact: This consolidation changes how property listings, market data, and transaction reporting flow through the region. The merger creates a more coordinated market intelligence system and standardizes transaction protocols across all five target counties.

What this means for PAs and contractors: Unified data reporting = faster access to transaction timelines and property ownership records. For PA prospecting, the consolidated MLS simplifies market intelligence gathering. For contractors, centralized deal flow and referral pathways may streamline project sourcing and pipeline visibility.

Timeline: Merger implementation begins May 11, 2026, with full integration expected by year-end.

Regulatory & Compliance

Senate Bill 266 Effective July 1, 2026: PA Contract Disclosures & Vulnerable Adult Protections

Florida strengthens public adjuster consumer protections with Senate Bill 266, taking effect July 1, 2026. Key provisions:

  • Contract disclosure font: All PA engagement contracts must display key terms in minimum 18-point font
  • Vulnerable adult cancellation rights: Adults lacking legal capacity (or their guardians) can rescind PA service agreements at any time (vs. prior 10/30-day windows)
  • Compliance scope: Applies to all PA agreements, including emergency declarations, retainers, and contingency arrangements

Implication for Duncan: This elevates disclosure standards but also clarifies legitimate PAs' competitive advantage. Homeowners hiring unscrupulous or unlicensed adjusters (like the Chaparro-Araus case) will have stronger legal recourse—creating opportunity for properly licensed, transparent PAs to position themselves as compliant alternatives.

Compliance checklist: Review all engagement templates; ensure font sizing and disclosures meet 18-point minimum; document vulnerable-adult capacity assessments if applicable.

Source: Florida Senate Bill 266 (2026) | flsenate.gov

Water Damage Claims & Mitigation

May Humidity & Monsoon Pattern: Water Damage Claims Scrutiny Increasing

Florida water damage claims are facing tighter carrier scrutiny in 2026, despite overall insurance market stabilization. Key dynamics:

Timeline pressure:

  • Mold can begin growing within 24–48 hours on wet materials
  • Florida law: property claims must be noticed within 1 year of loss; supplemental claims within 18 months
  • Insurers must pay/deny within 60 days of notice

Coverage distinction:

  • Standard homeowners policies: cover water damage from leaks, plumbing, rain intrusion
  • Flood damage (standing water, storm surge): NOT covered—requires separate FEMA flood policy

Claim strategy: Faster mitigation = lower insurer processing costs = fewer denials. Documentation with photos before cleanup dramatically reduces claim resistance.

PA positioning: "If a pipe bursts or roof leaks before hurricane season, mitigation speed determines whether the claim closes cleanly or escalates. I help document and expedite that process."

Contractor opportunity: Water mitigation firms booking May–June inspections are positioned ahead of storm season. Referral partnerships with PAs create upstream demand signals.
