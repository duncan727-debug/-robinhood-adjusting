RESTORATION + WATER-MIT: SB 266 CHANGES YOUR REFERRAL POSTURE JULY 1

Senate Bill 266 (2026) takes effect July 1 and carries two provisions that directly affect restoration crews on the ground:

  • Unconditional rescission right for vulnerable adults. Any vulnerable adult, adult who lacks capacity to consent, or their legal representative can cancel a public-adjuster contract at any time, without penalty. The 30-day post-loss / 10-day post-signing window stays the floor for everyone else, but the protected-population window stays open permanently.
  • $5,000 per-act fine for soliciting vulnerable adults. The Department of Financial Services can also suspend, deny, or revoke a PA license.

The fine attaches to the public adjuster — but contractor referral patterns can read as steering. If your water-mit crew routinely brings a single PA to the door of a distressed elderly homeowner, that pattern becomes an enforcement risk for the PA and a reputational risk for you.

The clean policy post-July 1:

  • Hand the homeowner a written list of three licensed PAs and let them choose.
  • Do not bring a single PA to the door, do not call a PA from the customer's living room, do not staple a PA business card to your invoice.
  • For households that look like they meet the vulnerable-adult definition, route the engagement decision through the family/legal representative before any contract is signed.

Restoration is downstream of the homeowner's relationship with their adjuster. Protect that relationship.
