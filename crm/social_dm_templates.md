### If they reply positively
> Appreciated. If you've got 20 min sometime this week or next, I'd love to compare notes on how you're seeing the {category} side of pre-season requests. I'm in Wellington — happy to drive your way or grab a coffee somewhere central. Easiest way to lock something in: {calendly_link}

### If they ask "what's a public adjuster" or seem unclear
> Quick version — when a homeowner has a covered loss (storm, water, fire), I negotiate the claim *for the homeowner*, not the carrier. Carriers send their own adjusters; I represent the homeowner side of that conversation. Where {category} comes in: I usually get called before the homeowner has chosen a crew, so being able to hand them a name I trust matters. Always happy to refer when it fits.

### First touch
> Hi {first_name} — Duncan from Robinhood Adjusting, Wellington. Saw {first_listing_or_brokerage_detail}. Reaching out because the insurance side of South Florida transactions just shifted hard — Citizens filed -2.6%, HB 815 changes the roof conversation July 1, and the flood mandate is reshaping how buyers underwrite carrying cost. I'm a public adjuster (FL Lic. G127132) and I help homeowners on the claim side, but I also do free pre-closing policy reviews for buyers your clients are working with. No fee, no obligation — just a second set of eyes on what they're inheriting. Worth a 20-min coffee?

### If they reply positively
> Appreciated. Easiest way to lock time: {calendly_link} (15-min phone or 30-min in-person — your call). Wellington office or wherever's convenient for you.

### If they ask for more info first
> Sure. The pre-closing review I do: I read the seller's current declarations + last 1802 wind-mit inspection + any open claims history, and tell the buyer what their renewal will likely look like, what credits they're inheriting or losing, and any documentation they should request before closing. Takes about 30 min. Useful for the buyer; useful for you because it surfaces insurance issues before they kill the deal.
