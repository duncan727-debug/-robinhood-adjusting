## Strategic Context

The attorney partnership is a **mature, sophisticated referral lane** — but the market has shifted in 2026:

1. **Litigation collapsed 35%+ since 2021.** HB 837 eliminated one-way attorney fees and abusive AOB. Plaintiff P&C attorneys have fewer cases. They need PAs who can resolve claims without litigation — and refer back the ones that do need a lawyer.
2. **HB 459 (2026): New administrative dispute resolution.** A formal pre-litigation path now exists. PAs and attorneys are natural co-pilots on this — PA handles the claim negotiation, attorney steps in only if it escalates.
3. **Palm Beach civil filings surging.** Despite the state-level litigation drop, Palm Beach specifically saw a spike in April 2026. Insurance and property dispute lawsuits dominate. Attorneys who are filing these cases have clients who needed a PA first.
4. **Fraud differentiation:** The PA fraud case ($600K+ diverted, Miramar) makes licensed, clean-record PAs more valuable to attorneys who need to vouch for their referral partners.
5. **Litigation down ≠ disputes down.** Claims disputes are still being filed — they're just going through administrative channels first. PAs who are embedded at that stage direct the escalations.

**The pitch:** "I resolve the claim before it needs you. If I can't, I send it your way. And you can send me the clients who need PA work before they need a lawsuit."

## Key Regulatory Context for Attorney Conversations

- **HB 837 (2023):** Eliminated one-way attorney fees; eliminated AOB for property insurance. Reduced litigation volume but concentrated it in stronger cases.
- **HB 459 (2026):** Formal administrative dispute resolution before litigation. Creates a PA lane before attorney involvement.
- **PA fee caps:** 10% during declared state of emergency; 20% thereafter. Attorneys should know PAs are regulated and fee-capped.
- **PA license:** Licensed by the Florida DFS. Duncan's clean record is a differentiator — cite the Chaparro fraud case as context for why credentialing matters.
