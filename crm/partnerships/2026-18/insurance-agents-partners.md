## Strategic Context

Insurance agents/brokers are a **non-obvious but high-yield** partnership category. The relationship is counterintuitive — aren't agents aligned with carriers, not claimants? But:

1. **Independent agents want client retention.** When a client has a bad claims experience, they blame the agent who sold the policy. A PA who rescues the claim saves the agent's relationship.
2. **Depopulated policy owners need guidance.** ~1.3M Florida policies moved from Citizens to private carriers since 2023. Agents who managed those transfers have clients who are confused about their new coverage — and about to file their first claim with a carrier they don't know.
3. **Rate environment is favorable right now.** With Broward/Miami-Dade/Palm Beach all seeing 11–14% rate drops, agents are in renewal conversations with clients. That's an opening to introduce a PA referral network.
4. **HB 459 creates structured dispute paths.** New administrative dispute resolution before litigation means agents have a clear moment to say "you need a PA now, before this escalates."

**The pitch:** "I help your clients get better claim outcomes. That protects your book of business. When a client calls you frustrated about a denied or low-ball claim, you want to have a name to give them."
