## LICENSING & COMPLIANCE BREAKDOWN

### Path A: Insurance Producer License (Lowest Friction)
**What it is:** You sell insurance for underwriters, earn commission (15-25%)
**License needed:** State insurance producer license (Florida first, then nationwide)
**Florida requirements:**
- Complete 60-hour pre-licensing course (~$100-300, online)
- Pass Florida Property & Casualty exam (or Life & Health — check which covers surplus lines)
- Apply via MyProfile portal (Florida OIR)
- $50-100 application fee
- Background check
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks total
- **Cost:** ~$500-800 total
- **Key:** Also get Surplus Lines license (for Lloyd's and non-admitted carriers)

**Limitation:** You represent specific carriers. Doesn't give you full MGA authority.

### Path B: Managing General Agent (MGA) (Higher Authority)
**What it is:** You can bind coverage on behalf of underwriters, set pricing, issue policies
**License needed:** MGA appointment from each carrier + state producer license
**Additional requirements:**
- E&O coverage for the MGA itself (~$10K-25K/year)
- Each carrier must formally appoint you as MGA
- More complex compliance obligations
**Timeline:** 6-12 months + carrier negotiations
**When to pursue:** After you've proven the concept with a producer license

### Path C: Referral/Lead Gen Only (No License Required)
**What it is:** You refer customers to licensed brokers/agents, earn referral fee
**License needed:** None (if you don't quote, bind, or advise on specific products)
**Revenue:** 5-10% referral fee (lower than producer model)
**Risk:** Commoditized, can be cut out easily
**Use case:** Start here to validate demand before investing in licensing

**Recommendation:** Start with Path C for first 60 days (zero barrier, validate demand). Get Florida producer license by Month 2-3. Pursue MGA status by Month 8-12.

## TECH STACK RECOMMENDATION (MVP)

Don't over-build. MVP needs to do exactly 3 things:
1. Present insurance products clearly
2. Collect customer information / qualify them
3. Route them to the underwriter to complete purchase

**Phase 1 MVP (Months 1-6) — $15-25K, no engineers needed:**
- **Landing page:** Webflow ($50/month) — no code required
- **Quote form:** Typeform or JotForm embedded on Webflow
- **CRM:** HubSpot Free or Pipedrive ($15/month)
- **Email:** Google Workspace ($15/month)
- **Policy documents:** DocuSign API ($25/month)
- **Payments:** Stripe if collecting fees directly, or route to carrier portal

**Phase 2 Platform (Months 6-12) — $75-100K engineering:**
- **Frontend:** React/Next.js (hire 1 contractor at $100-150/hour)
- **Backend:** Node.js or Python, hosted on AWS/GCP
- **Underwriter API integrations:** Each carrier has different API (Munich Re, Nexus, etc.)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL
- **Auth:** Auth0 or Clerk
- **Analytics:** Mixpanel or Amplitude

**Phase 3 Scale (Month 12+):**
- Hire CTO or Head of Engineering
- Build comparative quoting engine (like Policygenius for crypto)
- Mobile app (React Native)

## COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

**The problem with existing players:**
- Evertas: broker distribution only, no retail, black-box pricing
- Nexus Mutual: decentralized, complex, not traditional insurance
- Coincover: limited products, theft-only, no smart contract coverage
- Traditional brokers (Aon/Marsh): enterprise-only, minimum $10M holdings

**Your moat (what you build that's hard to copy):**
1. **Distribution relationships** — partnerships with custody platforms and crypto exchanges (sticky, exclusive where possible)
2. **Crypto-native UX** — product designed for crypto holders, not adapted from enterprise forms
3. **Underwriter diversity** — 5+ underwriters on platform creates comparison value
4. **Regulatory clarity** — position as the compliant, transparent option (trust is scarce in crypto)
5. **Educational content** — become the "Investopedia of crypto insurance" for SEO and brand

## RISKS & MITIGATION

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Underwriters won't partner with small operator | Medium | High | Start with Lloyd's syndicates (more flexible) or specialized carriers (Relm, Evertas) |
| Regulatory delays on licensing | Low | Medium | Start referral model immediately; licensing is parallel, not blocking |
| Market education too slow (retail doesn't buy) | Medium | Medium | Focus institutional first (they're already buying) |
| Competitor launches similar marketplace | Low | Medium | Speed + distribution relationships = moat; don't wait to be first |
| Claims experience drives up premiums | Low | High | Commission model means you don't carry risk; underwriter does |
| Customer acquisition cost too high | Medium | Medium | Custody integration = zero CAC for embedded channel |
