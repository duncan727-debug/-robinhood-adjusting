## FLORIDA REGULATORY PATHWAY

### Florida-Specific Insurance Licensing (Fast Track)

**Step 1 — Producer License (6-8 weeks from now)**
- Florida Department of Financial Services — MyProfile portal
- Course: 60-hour Property & Casualty pre-licensing (or 40-hour for Limited Lines)
- Exam: Florida 2-20 P&C exam (most relevant) — $56 exam fee
- License fee: $75
- **Surplus Lines endorsement** (critical for Lloyd's/non-admitted carriers): 30 additional hours + separate exam
- Total cost: ~$800-1,200 all-in

**Step 2 — Surplus Lines Eligibility (Florida-Specific)**  
Florida has a large surplus lines market — Lloyd's syndicates, non-admitted specialty carriers. Get the 4-19 Surplus Lines license immediately after 2-20. This opens Arch, Beazley, Atrium, Canopius (all Lloyd's) without needing admitted carrier relationships first.

**Step 3 — MGA Registration (Month 6-12)**  
Florida does not require separate MGA registration beyond the producer license, unlike some states. This means **Florida is one of the easiest states for MGA-style operations** — a significant structural advantage.

**Key Contacts at Florida OIR:**
- Division of Agents and Agencies: (850) 413-3137
- Surplus Lines Clearinghouse (SLIP): slip.fslso.com
- Florida Surplus Lines Service Office (FSLSO): fslso.com — mandatory filing partner for surplus lines
