## Lead Story

### Auto Insurance Rate Relief Accelerates: USAA Files 7% Decrease for May 2026

The insurance relief story continues to expand beyond property into auto insurance. USAA filed an average **7% rate decrease effective May 2026**, delivering more than **$125 million in estimated annual savings** for USAA's Florida members. This follows a broader wave: **42 companies covering personal auto lines have filed for rate decreases** in the past year—32 within the last six months. The top five auto insurers (78% of Florida's auto market) now average an **8% rate decrease for 2026**, marking the most significant relief in over a decade.

**Context for adjusters:** Broader insurance affordability improvements (property + auto) signal a stabilizing market environment. Public adjusters should emphasize total household insurance optimization when prospecting—clients now have more budget flexibility to address property coverage gaps and mitigation investments.

## Insurance & Claims

### Home Insurance Market Stabilizing: Average $3,815 Annually Statewide

Home insurance is stabilizing at an average of **$3,815 per year** statewide—significantly lower than the crisis-era highs of 2023-2024. The state-backed Citizens Property Insurance filed for an **8.7% decrease effective mid-2026**. New carriers are entering the Florida market and existing carriers are expanding competition, driving better rates for consumers. Combined with legislative reforms (2022-2023), insurance litigation filings have fallen **more than 35% since 2021**, and those savings are directly reaching policyholders.

**Source:** [Greene & Associates Florida Insurance Market Report 2026](https://www.greeneinsurance.com/guides/florida-insurance-market-2026)

The Business Observer's annual **Top 50 Contractors** report (2026) shows cumulative revenue of **$10.38 billion** across electrical, general construction, roofing, HVAC, landscaping, and plumbing firms operating from Polk County south through Collier County. ## Weather & Seasonal Risk

### May Heat Risk & Rain Pattern Shift: Saturday Heat Advisory Possible, Sunday Storm System

**Today (Thursday, May 1):** Sunny skies across South Florida. High of 89° in Miami-Dade/Broward; high of 83° in Palm Beach with northeast winds 5–10 mph. Clear conditions favoring outdoor inspection and property assessment work.

**Saturday (May 2):** **Moderate to locally major heat risk** with highs near 93° and windy conditions. Heat advisory possible for vulnerable populations; contractors should manage outdoor work schedules accordingly.

**Sunday–Monday (May 3–4):** **Frontal passage bringing widespread rainfall.** 60% chance of rain Sunday with a high of 87°. This marks the seasonal shift toward the May–June wet season pattern ahead of Hurricane season opening (June 1).

**Wildfire context:** Florida faces its **most severe drought in 25+ years**, with hundreds of wildfires reported in early 2026. Interior and coastal areas remain at elevated fire risk despite improved overall weather. Property owners in wildland-urban interface zones should consider evacuation readiness and asset protection planning.
