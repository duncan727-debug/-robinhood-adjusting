## Read the Carrier Name on Your Declarations Page This Weekend

The Florida property insurance market has 20 new carriers since 2023 and roughly $850 million of fresh capital. That is genuinely good news for premium. But there is a second story under the headline.

About **20% of Florida property insurers rated by Demotech became insolvent between 2009 and 2022** — even while carrying an "A" rating, according to research from Columbia, Harvard, and the Federal Reserve Board. That is roughly **30 times the failure rate** of insurers rated by the other major agencies. Three U.S. Senators opened a formal probe into Demotech's rating practices in late December 2025 and asked Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac why they continue to treat Demotech ratings as proof of carrier financial strength.

This affects you in one very practical way. If your mortgage is a conforming Fannie / Freddie loan — which most are — and your carrier is Demotech-rated, a downgrade event can mean the policy no longer satisfies your lender's hazard-insurance requirement at renewal. You would have to switch carriers on short notice during the worst possible window.

**What to do this week, 15 minutes:**
1. Pull your most recent declarations page from your insurance email or carrier portal.
2. Look up the carrier name on the **Demotech site** (or your agent can pull it).
3. If the rating is anything other than "A" Exceptional, "A" or higher A.M. Best, ask your agent for **a quote from at least one carrier with a different rating-agency profile** — many of the 20 new entrants carry alternative ratings.

This is not panic. It is the equivalent of checking the brakes on a car you've driven for two years without thinking about it.

## If You Own a Condo Built Before 1996, Read This Twice

Florida's milestone-inspection law (originally driven by the Surfside collapse) requires every condominium and cooperative building **three or more stories** tall to undergo a milestone structural inspection at age 30 — or age 25 in coastal jurisdictions that have determined local circumstances warrant it. Paired with the milestone inspection is a **Structural Integrity Reserve Study (SIRS)**, which calculates the dollar figure a building actually needs in reserves to maintain its structural systems over the next ten years.

The hard date you want on your calendar: **December 31, 2026** is the outer deadline for completing a SIRS when paired with a milestone inspection. That is **210 days away**.

The financial part is what gets buried in the board meeting minutes. Per industry analyses tracking SIRS outcomes since the law took effect, **special assessments are landing in the $134,000 to $400,000 per unit range** for buildings that deferred concrete and envelope work. That is not a worst-case scenario — that is the median for older Hollywood, Sunny Isles, and Aventura buildings whose engineers have signed off on the SIRS in the last 18 months.

**What to do this week:**
1. Read your condo association's last two board meeting packets. Look for the words "milestone," "SIRS," "structural integrity reserve study," or "special assessment."
2. If you don't find them, email your board president and ask for the building's milestone-inspection status and SIRS-completion timeline.
3. If you bought your unit in the last 24 months and were given no SIRS disclosure, talk to your closing attorney. Florida's disclosure requirements have tightened materially.

## If You've Ever Hired a Public Adjuster — Or Have an Elderly Parent Who Might

**Senate Bill 266 (Public Adjuster Contracts)** carries a stated effective date of **July 1, 2026** — under a month away. The bill rewrites portions of Florida Statute §626.854 (the core public-adjuster contracting law) and adds three protections that matter at the kitchen table:

1. **Vulnerable-adult open-ended rescission.** If the contract was signed by a "vulnerable adult" as defined under Florida law (this includes many seniors with diminished capacity), or by someone who lacks capacity to consent, the contract may be rescinded **at any time**, without penalty.
2. **18-point font disclosures.** Every PA contract disclosure must now be in **minimum 18-point font** — roughly twice the size of normal contract body text. The intent is to make the fee structure impossible to overlook.
3. **New disciplinary ground.** Soliciting or taking advantage of a vulnerable adult is now an explicit basis for the Department of Financial Services to discipline a PA license or apprentice license.

Existing protections also remain: a standard **10-day right of rescission** from contract execution, and a **30-day window after the date of loss** (or 10 days after execution, whichever is longer) for claims arising from a declared state of emergency.

**What to do this week:**
1. If a parent or elderly neighbor has signed a PA contract in the past 12-24 months — particularly one signed door-to-door after a roof leak or storm event — the family now has clearer ground to revisit the contract.
2. If you yourself are evaluating a PA after a recent claim, do not sign until July 1 unless you've read the contract twice and confirmed the fee disclosure is in 18-point font.
3. Florida regulates PA fees at **10–20%** generally and **10% during declared emergencies**. Anything above those caps is illegal regardless of contract language.

The bill's signing status by Governor DeSantis was not yet in the public record at the time of this brief; the July 1 effective date applies if signed.

## What's Coming

- **Hurricane peak.** Quiet through next week per NHC. Statistical peak is still mid-September, so the planning window through August is real.
- **HB 815 takes effect July 1.** The certified-inspector path that keeps older asphalt-shingle roofs eligible for renewal kicks in the same day SB 266 does. We covered the roof-age side Monday — short version is that a roof condition certified by a licensed inspector now governs renewal eligibility, not just the calendar age of the roof.
- **Building Code 9th Edition starts December 31.** If you have a discretionary roof or large renovation in the pipeline, talk to your contractor about whether to pull the permit before year-end. The 8th Edition rules generally lock in if the permit is pulled before the effective date.
