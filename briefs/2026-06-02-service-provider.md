## CILB Renewal Deadline: 90 Days, 14 Hours

Your certified-license renewal closes **August 31, 2026**. Even-year licenses are up. That includes general, building, residential, roofing, mechanical/HVAC, plumbing, and most other certified trades.

**The requirements:**
- **14 board-approved CE hours** every two years
- **1 hour each** of workplace safety, workers' comp, and business practices (mandatory)
- **11 hours** of trade-specific or general construction
- Renewal fee: **$209** for HVAC and most certified trades. **$105** individual / **$155** qualified business for certified plumbing.

If you haven't started CE for this cycle, you have roughly 13 weeks. Don't wait until August — the CE providers get jammed and the DBPR portal slows down. A non-renewed license at midnight 8/31 takes your permits, your bonding, and your right to advertise as a licensed contractor down with it.

**Roofers specifically:** Catch a CE module on the **roof-to-wall connection evaluation** scope added under HB 715. That work is now squarely inside the roofing license — you can bid and self-perform what used to bounce to a GC. It also flows directly into the new wind-mit form's credit structure, which means the inspection and retrofit are billable in series.

## The Cost Side: Section 232 Tariffs Are Eating Margin

Material costs are up **15-25% year-over-year** on roofing. Specifically:
- **Aluminum mill shapes up 33% YoY**
- **Steel mill products up 20.7% YoY**
- **Section 232 tariffs at 50%** on steel and aluminum, now covering **400+ derivative product categories**: metal flashing, drip edge, prefab panels

**What to do this week:**
1. Reprice your standard contract. The bid template you've been using since last fall is leaving 10-15% of margin on the table.
2. Add a **material-escalator clause** to residential agreements — common language is "price firm unless steel or aluminum index moves more than X% between signing and material order." Talk to your attorney about specific thresholds.
3. If you're a roofer, the price gap between asphalt-shingle and standing-seam metal has narrowed. Metal is still more expensive upfront, but the **140+ mph wind rating** plus the wind-mit credit shortens payback. Adjust your in-home presentation accordingly.

## HB 815 Roof-Age Loophole Closes July 1 — Inspection Letter Is a Billable Product

**House Bill 815** takes effect **July 1, 2026** (29 days out). Quick recap of the operational change:

- Insurers cannot refuse to issue or renew on roof age alone if the roof is **under 15 years old**.
- For roofs 15+ years, an **authorized inspector** can certify either: 5+ years of useful life (steep-slope) OR ability to be restored with a coating system that adds 5 years (low-slope). With that letter, the carrier cannot deny on age.

**Why this is your opportunity:** That **inspection-and-useful-life letter** is now a discrete, billable service tied to a carrier-acceptance decision. Set internal pricing and turnaround standards now. A reasonable structure: **$250-$500** for the inspection and letter, 5-7 business day turnaround. Position it as a separate product from a repair quote — it's diagnostic work, not a sales call.

If the inspection identifies repair scope, that becomes a separate proposal. Two-step engagement, two invoices, cleaner client journey.

## Condo Work Is About to Surge — Milestone + SIRS Deadline December 31, 2026

For condo buildings **3+ stories** that hit the **30-year age threshold in 2026** (25 years in coastal areas — most of Miami-Dade, Broward, and PBC east of I-95), the **milestone inspection AND Structural Integrity Reserve Study** are both due **December 31, 2026**.

The SIRS forces reserve funding for eight components: **roof, load-bearing walls, primary structural members, fire protection, plumbing, electrical, waterproofing, windows and exterior doors**. Reserve waivers for these are no longer permitted (since budgets adopted after December 31, 2024).

**The pipeline math:** Thousands of South Florida buildings are entering this cycle. Engineers are running the studies; the studies will identify scope; the scope drives RFPs to roofing, waterproofing, plumbing, electrical, and structural trades. The work window is **2027-2030**, but the **bid window is right now and through Q4 2026**.

If you're a:
- **Roofer:** Make sure your CILB renewal is clean by 8/31, then start working condo board contacts. Many boards prefer a contractor who can produce a full pre-construction file (license, insurance, references, OSHA records) on first ask. Build the file once, send it many times.
- **Waterproofer / mason / restoration:** Same playbook. These are the trades where the SIRS findings most often produce surprise assessments.
- **Plumbing / electrical:** Standalone service revenue from milestone-driven repairs is meaningful, especially on cast-iron stack replacements and panel upgrades in 1980s/90s buildings.
