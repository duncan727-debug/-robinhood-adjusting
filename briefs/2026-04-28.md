## LEAD STORY: Hurricane Milton Supplemental Claims Window Closing

The April 9 deadline for Hurricane Milton supplemental claims has now passed, but this event remains the dominant fixture in South Florida's insurance landscape. For public adjusters, this represents both a closing window of supplemental opportunity and ongoing engagement with clients who initially underestimated their losses.

## INSURANCE & CLAIMS

### Wind Mitigation Tops State Policy Agenda

Florida's Insurance Commissioner and Office of Insurance Regulation are pushing hard on wind mitigation investments, with experts at the OIR Summit (April 2026) identifying inadequate mitigation as a systemic vulnerability. Florida statute §627.70132 requires carriers to offer wind mitigation discounts, but adoption remains spotty. This creates opportunity: properties with mitigation investments—roof reinforcement, hurricane clips, impact windows, door reinforcement—become more insurable and may qualify for better terms.

**Source:** [Florida Needs More Wind Mitigation | Insurance Journal, April 20, 2026](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southeast/2026/04/20/866484.htm)

### New Claims Dispute Resolution Process Takes Effect

House Bill 459 (effective 2026) establishes a formal administrative dispute resolution process for property insurance claims before escalation to litigation. This reduces friction for insureds and adjusters, providing a structured path when insurers and claimants disagree on loss value or coverage scope.

**Source:** [Florida New Insurance Laws for 2026 | Boggs Law Group](https://boggslawgroup.com/what-floridas-new-insurance-laws-mean-for-2026/)

### Public Adjuster Licensing & Fraud Alert

A significant fraud case emerged in April 2026: Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus, 46, of All Elements Public Adjusters in **Miramar (Miami-Dade), was arrested and charged with grand theft and misappropriation of funds**. He is accused of diverting more than $600,000 in Hurricane Ian settlements. This underscores the importance of due diligence when selecting a PA firm and reinforces Florida's regulatory framework: fee caps (10% for emergency declarations, 20% thereafter) and 10/30-day cancellation rights for consumers.
