## TOP STORIES

### Rate Relief Accelerates: Citizens Cuts 8.7%, Multiple Carriers Follow Suit
Homeowners insurance in Florida is entering a genuine relief phase. Citizens Property Insurance Corporation has approved an average 8.7% rate cut effective spring 2026, with reductions rolling out at individual policy renewals across the state. The market is moving fast: State Farm filed 10%, USAA 7%, Progressive and GEICO approximately 8% each, and Florida Peninsula 8.2%.

The regulatory environment has shifted decisively. Since OIR reforms took effect, 17 new insurance companies have entered Florida's market, and Citizens has shed 541,000 policies (reducing exposure from 936,000 to 395,000). Auto insurance is seeing even steeper cuts—Progressive alone announced a $1 billion refund to policyholders due to favorable claims experience.

**Why it matters for service providers:** Rate decreases drive renewal activity and new-business opportunities. Property owners evaluating their coverage will call for inspections and damage assessments. Public adjusters should expect increased policy renewal questions and an uptick in claims inquiries as homeowners reassess coverage limits. The market shift from crisis to stabilization also opens relationship-building windows with carriers and brokers.

### Historic Realtor Association Merger: MIAMI + RWorld Create Global Leader
Effective May 11, 2026, the Miami Association of Realtors and Broward, Palm Beaches & St. Lucie Realtors formally merged to create the largest local Realtor association in the world, with 93,000 members. The unified organization will operate under the tentative name "Miami and South Florida REALTORS" pending NAR approval.

This consolidation reflects broad momentum in South Florida's real estate sector: Palm Beach County single-family home sales rose 14.26% YoY in March 2026 (1,255 → 1,434 units), luxury property sales surged 77.3% (properties $5M+), and total dollar volume climbed 29.25% to $2.6 billion.

**Why it matters for service providers:** The merger signals institutional confidence in South Florida's durability. Larger realtor networks create new partnership opportunities for contractors, adjusters, and mitigation specialists. Luxury home sales growth also correlates with high-value insurance claims and sophisticated property recovery work.

## INSURANCE & REGULATION

### OIR 2026 Compliance Requirements Now in Effect
The Florida Office of Insurance Regulation has expanded reporting and transparency mandates stemming from 2025 legislative reforms (Senate Bill 7052). Insurers now submit:
- Quarterly and annual deductions/discount reporting
- Enhanced policy and claims data disclosure
- Policy impact documentation on a frequent, granular basis

Citizens will require five years of prior loss history documentation for new-business commercial risks, taking effect January 1, 2026.

**Sources:** [OIR Rate Approval Archive](https://floir.gov/newsroom) | [Citizens Rule Changes](https://www.citizensfla.com/-/20251106-2026-rate-rule-and-manual-changes)

### Commercial Insurance: Diverging Path
While personal lines see rate cuts, Citizens has filed for a 10.4% average increase on commercial lines effective late 2026. This divergence reflects different risk profiles and loss experience between residential and commercial portfolios.

**Sources:** [Citizens Rate Filings](https://www.citizensfla.com/-/20251210-citizens-recommends-rate-cuts-for-most-policyholders)

## PUBLIC ADJUSTING & CLAIMS INTEGRITY

### Fraud Alert: Public Adjuster Accused of $600K Diversion
A Florida public adjuster stands accused of diverting more than $600,000 in Hurricane Ian insurance payments—the second prosecution allegation against the same individual for similar alleged misappropriation. The case highlights ongoing risks in claims handling and the importance of regulatory oversight.

**Why it matters:** This case underscores the value of transparent, licensed adjusters in the market. Property owners are increasingly aware of fraud risk and may seek established, verifiable credentials when selecting representatives. The incident also creates reputational pressure on the industry to enforce ethical standards.

Backlogs and repeated documentation requests persist in some regions.

## CONTRACTORS & SERVICE INDUSTRIES

### Roofing Industry Adjusting to Market Pressures
Florida's roofing sector faces familiar challenges in 2026: labor shortages, material cost volatility, and lead-quality fluctuations. Contractors are responding with diversification and expansion strategies.

**Key developments:**
- **Atlantic Metal Roofing** launched a dedicated luxury residential/commercial division serving Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, and Naples.
- **Diversified Roofing Solutions** opened a new Delray Beach division, expanding residential and commercial services in Palm Beach County.
- **TopBuild Corp.** announced an acquisition of Johnson Roofing (Texas-based, ~$29M revenue), signaling industry consolidation.

**Building Code Updates:** The 2026 9th Edition Florida Building Code introduces updated wind-resistance standards, improved attic ventilation requirements, and new rooftop solar integration guidance.

**Legal Risk:** The Florida Attorney General filed suit against Florida Roof Specialists (April 10, 2026) for alleged deceptive practices spanning two years.

**Why it matters:** Expansion announcements indicate confidence in South Florida's recovery work pipeline. Updated building codes create compliance consulting opportunities for contractors and adjusters. The AG lawsuit reinforces the importance of transparent, documented work practices.
