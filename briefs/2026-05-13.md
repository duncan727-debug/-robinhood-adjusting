### Public Adjuster Fraud Case: $703K Theft from Hurricane Ian Claims
Florida Chief Financial Officer Blaise Ingoglia announced the arrest of public insurance adjuster **Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus** for misappropriation of over $703,000 in Hurricane Ian insurance settlement funds. Investigators from the Department of Financial Services Criminal Investigations Division documented that Chaparro-Araus:

- Forged victim names on settlement checks
- Concealed insurance payments from 13 homeowners
- Refused to remit funds after being contacted by victims

**Why it matters:** This high-profile case underscores the importance of fiduciary oversight, client trust, and transparent fund handling in the PA industry. It reinforces the regulatory environment shift toward stronger consumer protections (SB 266, vulnerable adult rescission rules effective July 1). For Duncan's practice, this is a cautionary tale and a positioning opportunity: transparent, documented, client-first practices build credibility in an industry facing reputational pressure from fraud cases.

## INSURANCE & REGULATION

### SB 266 Details: Vulnerable Adult Protections Take Effect July 1
Florida Senate Bill 266 (passed and signed) introduces new consumer protections for vulnerable populations and clarifies PA disciplinary standards. Effective July 1, 2026:

- **Vulnerable adults** may rescind PA contracts within a specified window (contract-specific terms)
- **Adults lacking capacity** to provide informed consent and their legal representatives gain rescission rights
- **PA Discipline Standards** clarified for violations including misrepresentation, unauthorized representation, and breach of fiduciary duty

**Compliance Requirements:**
- PA firms must document informed consent with robust language
- Timely notice procedures are critical to avoid rescission liability
- Contract language should reflect consumer-protection posture and fiduciary accountability

**Why it matters:** This adds operational compliance complexity for PA firms, particularly in multi-generational households and estates. Duncan should ensure contract language, disclosure documents, and intake procedures are aligned with the July 1 effective date. The broader trend reinforces that the PA industry is shifting toward transparency and fiduciary accountability (underscored by today's Chaparro-Araus fraud case).
