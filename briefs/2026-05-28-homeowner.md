## YOUR ROOFER'S CALENDAR IS THE STORY THIS WEEK

Once a named storm enters the Florida cone, every roofer, tarping crew, and water-mitigation outfit in the region runs on a triage queue. New customers wait 5 to 14 days. **Your name being on a roofer's existing customer list before June 1 is the single best storm-prep move you can make right now.**

The 90-minute version of this weekend's checklist:

1. **Book a 30-minute pre-season roof check** with a contractor you'd want to be #3 on the call list. If you don't have one, ask two neighbors who they used last August. You're not buying repairs today — you're buying access.
2. **Photograph everything.** Walk the house room by room with your phone in panorama, then walk the exterior. Capture serial numbers on the HVAC outdoor unit, the water heater, and the main electrical panel. Push the album to iCloud, Google Photos, or both — not the phone alone.
3. **Confirm three policies are in force:** wind, flood, and (if applicable) HO-6 condo unit-owner. Look at the declarations page, not just the auto-pay email.
4. **Exercise the shutters or panels** once. If they bind, you find out now — not on a Saturday afternoon as a cone approaches.

The National Hurricane Center's outlook this morning is clean: zero disturbances, zero formation expected through Day 7. That is the window. Use it.

## WELLINGTON IS QUIETLY ABSORBING ITS WESTERN EDGE — HERE'S WHAT IT MEANS IF YOU LIVE THERE

Three Wellington land-use stories that looked separate have resolved into one arc:

- **Arden annexation.** The Village and Palm Beach County are negotiating a boundary agreement that would move roughly **7,000 Arden residents** out of unincorporated PBC and into Wellington. Public discussions are active.
- **Artistry Lakes / Kolter.** The 446-acre Kolter Group parcel was formally annexed in March. **579 single-family homes** are planned — gated, low-density, no commercial.
- **Related Ross at Stribling Way.** Stephen Ross's firm has Village approval for a hotel, restaurants, two commercial pads, and the **405,000-sq-ft Wingrove Academy** private school for 1,750 students, on land entitled for over 600,000 sq ft of mixed use.

The bottom-line for an existing Wellington homeowner: in 24–36 months the tax base widens (which, over time, spreads fixed costs across more rooftops), the commercial amenity set deepens, and construction traffic on Stribling and Forest Hill picks up materially. If you live in **Arden specifically**, the annexation conversation is one to follow at the public-hearing level — permitting, code enforcement, and possibly trash and utility billing all change with the boundary.

## THE 6.5% MORTGAGE-RATE WORLD IS PROBABLY YOUR REFI WORLD FOR A WHILE

Freddie Mac's weekly survey held the **30-year fixed at 6.51%** — the highest since August 2025. The cause of the latest leg up is not the Federal Reserve. It's the **Middle East oil premium** working through into headline inflation, which pushes 10-year Treasury yields up, which drags mortgage rates with them.

If you were waiting for a 6.0% handle to refinance — that wait may run longer than the math on your origination costs supports. Two practical reads:

- **Recut the break-even at 6.51%.** Sometimes the answer is still "refi now" if you came in at 7.5%+ in 2023–2024. The decision should run on numbers, not on a target rate that has stopped being realistic this quarter.
- **Ask your originator about a buydown.** Florida wholesale lenders are quoting more aggressive **2-1 buydown** structures to keep deals moving. That can put you at an effective 4.5% in year one and 5.5% in year two while you wait for the longer-rate cycle to turn.

## IF YOU'RE CARING FOR AN AGING PARENT, READ THIS

A small but consequential Florida law — **Senate Bill 266** — takes effect **July 1**. It gives vulnerable adults, adults who lack capacity to consent, and their legal representatives the **permanent, no-penalty right to cancel a public-adjuster contract at any time**. The normal 30-day-after-loss rescission clock no longer applies to that population — the window stays open indefinitely.

The bill also adds **$5,000-per-act fines** for public adjusters who solicit vulnerable adults.

Two practical actions if you're managing claims or property for an older parent or a disabled family member:

- **Inventory any existing PA contracts** in their household and date them against July 1. Newer contracts get the broader rescission right automatically; pre-July contracts keep the older terms.
- **If a storm loss happens this season,** route the question of whether to engage a public adjuster through the legal representative *before* signing. The protection is strongest when it's used proactively.

Source: [Florida Senate — SB 266 (2026)](https://flsenate.gov/Session/Bill/2026/266)
