## The Big Story: Cheaper Reinsurance Means More Rate Cuts Are Coming

Here's a piece of the insurance machine most homeowners never see. Every June 1, the companies that sell you a homeowners policy go out and buy their own insurance — it's called **reinsurance** — to protect themselves against a really bad hurricane season. That wholesale market just had its annual renewal, and the news is striking: **reinsurance prices for Florida companies came down by 15-20%**, and there was more capacity available than at any point in the last six years.

What does that mean for the letter in your mailbox?

Citizens Property Insurance cut personal-lines rates by **2.6% statewide** effective yesterday — the first decrease since 2015. About **60% of Citizens policyholders are seeing roughly $359 in annual savings**. Heritage Property & Casualty announced a tri-county rate cut of **up to 5%** for Palm Beach, Broward, and Miami-Dade. Those were the first round. With reinsurance now substantially cheaper than it was a year ago, expect more carriers to file rate reductions over the next 90 days — particularly in PBC, Martin, and St. Lucie.

**Action this week:**
1. Pull your current declarations page out of the drawer.
2. When your renewal arrives, don't auto-pay. Ask an independent agent to shop the same coverage limits against the **20 new carriers** that have entered Florida since the 2022-23 reforms — there's $850M of new capital chasing your renewal.
3. If your renewal goes *up*, ask the carrier in writing what's driving it. The market backdrop says it shouldn't.

## Free Money for Home Hardening — Still Available, Multi-Year Now

The state legislature passed the **$115 billion FY 2026-27 budget** over Memorial Day weekend. Tucked inside: roughly **$405 million reappropriated** for the **My Safe Florida Home Program** and the **My Safe Florida Condo Pilot**. The bill heads to Governor DeSantis for signature in June.

Translation: the program that pays for impact windows, doors, shutters, roof upgrades, and wind-rated garage doors is **not running out of money**. Application windows now stretch through the 2026-27 cycle.

**Who qualifies right now:**

- Home is your **homesteaded** primary residence

- Insured for **$700,000 or less**

**What you get:**

The wind-mitigation form your inspector uses (**OIR-B1-1802**) was updated April 1, 2026 to credit window and door protection more generously than before. A funded retrofit through MSFH plus the new form is the cleanest premium-relief move available in our five counties this summer. **Apply at mysafeflhome.com.**

## The 17-Year-Old-Roof Story Just Changed in Your Favor (July 1)

If your roof is between 15 and 25 years old, you've probably had a renewal letter at some point that threatened non-renewal "due to roof age." **House Bill 815 closes that loophole on July 1, 2026** — 29 days from today.

**What changes:**
- Insurers cannot refuse to issue or renew a policy solely because your roof is **less than 15 years old**, regardless of material.
- For roofs 15+ years old, an **authorized inspector** can certify whether your roof has **5+ years of useful life remaining** (steep-slope) or can be **restored with a coating system** that adds 5 years (low-slope). With that letter in hand, the carrier cannot deny on roof-age grounds.

**What to do:** If your renewal lands between now and July 1, you're still under the old rules. Push back, ask for a 30-day extension, and consider switching carriers. If your renewal lands July 1 or later, an inspector's useful-life letter is your new tool. Budget **$200-$500** for the certified inspection — much cheaper than a $25,000 forced re-roof.

## Heads Up: Roof Costs Are Up 15-25% if You Do Need One

Section 232 tariffs at 50% on steel and aluminum — now covering 400+ derivative product categories including metal flashing, drip edge, and prefabricated panels — have pushed material costs hard. Aluminum mill shapes up 33% year-over-year. Steel mill products up 20.7%.

A re-roof quote from last summer is no longer valid. If you're shopping bids:
- Get **three written quotes**, dated within the same 30 days.
- Lock pricing in writing with a **material-escalator cap** (e.g., "price firm unless steel index moves more than 10%").
- Confirm the contractor's CILB license is current — the renewal deadline is **August 31, 2026**. A roofer who hasn't started their continuing-education hours by July is going to be scrambling.

## If You Own a Condo: December 31 Is a Hard Deadline

For residential condo buildings **3 or more stories** that hit the **30-year age threshold in 2026** (25 years in coastal areas — most of Miami-Dade, Broward, and PBC east of I-95), the **milestone inspection and Structural Integrity Reserve Study (SIRS) are both due December 31, 2026**.

If your association hasn't already engaged an engineer, that's a board-meeting agenda item this week. Missing the deadline can mean $500/day fines, unsafe-building determinations, and in the worst case a vacate order. The SIRS will dictate whether a special assessment is coming and how big it is. The reserve waiver for the eight core structural components (roof, load-bearing walls, plumbing, electrical, waterproofing, windows/doors, fire protection, structural members) is no longer permitted — those reserves must be funded.
