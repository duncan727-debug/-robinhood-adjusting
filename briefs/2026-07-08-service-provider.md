# South Florida Trade Professional Brief

**July 8, 2026 | South Florida**

## Today's Signal

The opportunity today is documentation, not storm chasing. Condo assessment pressure, Broward development activity, Citizens access interruptions, and ordinary summer storm calls all point to the same thing: owners need contractors who leave a clean record behind.

## Field Read

The Real Deal reports that Hallandale Beach has a wave of residential, hotel, office, and mixed-use projects in the pipeline, while AvalonBay paid $22 million for a South Miami development site planned for 251 apartments. More density means more roofs, drains, elevators, mechanical systems, parking structures, retail buildouts, association documents, and vendor relationships.

MarketWatch reports that 54% of community associations plan to raise regular assessments because of insurance costs and reserve needs. That means boards and owners will be more sensitive to scopes, estimates, proof of completion, and reserve-related repair planning.

Citizens' July 8 maintenance alert says online policy and claim tools will be unavailable from 6:30 p.m. to 10:00 p.m., while phone claim reporting remains available.

## Field Play

- Take time-stamped before, during, and after photos.
- Include moisture readings, model/serial numbers, product details, and permit notes where relevant.
- Give owners a written scope and closeout note.
- For condo or multifamily work, label unit/common-area boundaries clearly.
- For emergency calls, document what was temporary mitigation versus permanent repair.
