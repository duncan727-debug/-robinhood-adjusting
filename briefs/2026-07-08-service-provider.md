# South Florida Trade Professional Brief

**July 8, 2026 | South Florida**

## Today's Signal

Quiet tropics do not mean quiet field conditions. The National Hurricane Center says no Atlantic tropical development is expected during the next seven days, but Florida Disaster still expects isolated afternoon showers and thunderstorms that can be slow-moving and produce brief heavy downpours.

For roofers, HVAC contractors, plumbers, mitigation firms, restoration crews, electricians, and inspectors, today's work is about documentation quality. A routine storm call can become a claim file later.

## Field Play

- Take time-stamped before, during, and after photos.
- Capture moisture readings where water intrusion is involved.
- Record model and serial numbers for HVAC, electrical, appliances, pumps, and mitigation equipment.
- Put roof, drainage, and opening-protection observations in writing.
- Close each job with a short scope and completion note the homeowner can reuse.

## Why It Matters

Citizens' approved 2026 rate decreases are a helpful market signal, but rate relief does not remove claim friction. If a homeowner has a loss, the carrier still needs evidence. Contractors who deliver clean documentation become more valuable referral partners to owners, agents, and adjusters.
