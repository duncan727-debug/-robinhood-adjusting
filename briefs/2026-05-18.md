### Citizens Policy Count Hits 14-Year Low; 17 New Carriers Now Competing in Florida Market
[New Data Point] Florida's insurance market reset is now measurable in structural terms. Citizens Property Insurance — the state-backed insurer of last resort — has depopulated from a 2023 peak of **~1.5 million policies to ~395,000 in early 2026**, the lowest count since at least 2012. Meanwhile, **17 new private carriers have entered the Florida market** since the 2022 and 2023 tort reforms took effect, and homeowners insurance litigation has fallen **nearly 50%** in the 18 months following reform implementation. Consumers have seen an average **14.5% reduction in costs** versus the pre-reform trajectory.

**What's actually new (vs. our May 14–15 coverage):**
- The May 14 brief covered carrier-by-carrier rate cuts (Citizens 8.7%, USAA -7%, State Farm -10.1%). Today's update is **market structure**, not pricing — Florida went from a near-monopoly Citizens posture to a competitive private market.
- For property owners, this means real **carrier choice at renewal** for the first time in years. The advisory implication: coverage shopping is now legitimate value-add, not a wasted exercise.

**Why it matters for Duncan & PA practice:** The Citizens depopulation cycle means tens of thousands of South Florida homeowners are being **transitioned to private carriers** through Citizens' depopulation program over the next 12 months. Each transition is a coverage-gap moment — different deductibles, wind/hurricane sublimits, named-storm carveouts, and ordinance-or-law coverage. Position pre-season as a **policy comparison and coverage-gap review** for any household receiving a depopulation offer letter.

### Surplus Lines Homeowners Premiums Now Match Admitted Market — Quiet Structural Shift
[New Development] An Insurance Journal report (May 13) confirms that **average surplus lines homeowners premiums in Florida now run roughly even with the admitted market** — a previously unheard-of convergence. Commercial property policy counts in surplus also rose **39% year-over-year vs. April 2025**, while average premiums dropped **39%** over the same window.

**Context:** Florida's 2025 legislation repealed the three-declination rule (agents previously had to document three admitted-market rejections before placing surplus). That gate is gone, surplus capacity is flowing freely, and pricing has compressed.

**Why it matters:** South Florida's high-value, coastal, and older-roof properties have historically been pushed into surplus at significant premium markups. That penalty has now largely evaporated. Public adjusters and agents reviewing a client's policy should not assume a surplus placement is automatically a worse deal — the wrap, sublimits, and named-storm language matter more than the carrier category. **Read every declarations page; don't dismiss surplus placements by reflex.**

## INSURANCE & REGULATION

### HB 815 / SB 808 Watch: Roof Age Can No Longer Be Sole Denial Basis Effective July 1
[Regulatory Clarification — 44 Days Out] Both **HB 815** (House companion) and **SB 808** prohibit insurers from refusing to issue or renew a property policy **solely** because of a roof's age. The condition of the roof — supported by an authorized inspection — must drive the decision. Effective date: **July 1, 2026**.

**Operational details now confirmed:**
- For roofs ≥15 years old, insurers must differentiate **low-slope (≤2" pitch)** from **steep-slope (>2" pitch)** in their coverage offer.
- Expanded pool of inspectors qualifying as "authorized": licensed home inspectors (s. 468.8314), building code inspectors (s. 468.607), GCs/building/residential contractors (s. 489.111), roofing contractors, PEs (s. 471.015), licensed architects (s. 481.213), plus any individual or entity an insurer recognizes.
- Wind mitigation form **OIR-B1-1802 was revised effective April 1, 2026** — the first material rewrite in over a decade. Pre-July inspections using the old form may be partially obsolete for renewal underwriting.

**Why it matters for South Florida specifically:** A large share of the Palm Beach / Broward / Miami-Dade housing stock has roofs in the 15–25 year band where age-based non-renewals have been the dominant pre-2026 friction point. The July 1 shift is **highly favorable to property owners with maintained-but-aged roofs** — and creates an immediate window for re-quoting policies that were previously declined or non-renewed.

**Public adjuster takeaway:** Order an authorized inspection on the updated OIR-B1-1802 form **now** for any client with an aged roof. The combination of a clean inspection + post-July non-discrimination on age = renewal leverage that didn't exist 30 days ago.
