Market Snapshot

Miami-Dade Leads South Florida Transaction Growth

March 2026 data underscores Miami-Dade's outperformance: 2,134 home sales with total dollar volume up 15.59% year-over-year to $2.5 billion. Median sale price: $580K (+1.8% YoY) with pending sales up 13.2% YoY.

Broward followed with $445K median but saw condo weakness — prices falling, inventory growing. The $5M+ ultra-luxury segment in Miami-Dade climbed 27% year-over-year, signaling strong high-net-worth demand and high-value property concentration.

Impact: Real estate velocity correlates with new policy binding and claims potential. Luxury bracket ($5M+) represents higher-value claim segment with concentrated geographic opportunity.

Regulatory & Policy Shifts

Public Adjuster Fraud Arrest Underscores Vetting Importance

Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus, 46, operating All Elements Public Adjusters in Miramar, was arrested on fraud, grand theft, and misappropriation charges after allegedly diverting $600,000+ in Hurricane Ian insurance settlements. His PA license had been previously suspended in 2015 and 2024 — a pattern that went unaddressed until criminal charges.

Impact: High-profile enforcement signals intensified DFS and insurer scrutiny of PA credentials. Expect insurers and claimants to vet professional relationships more closely.

Federal Fraud Actions Signal Active Enforcement Climate

AP of South Florida, LLC (APSF) pleaded guilty to major federal fraud for using street marketers to enroll homeless and vulnerable individuals in ACA plans with falsified income data — generating $141.5 million in unwarranted federal subsidies. Parent company AssuredPartners agreed to pay $135 million in civil settlement.

Additionally, six individuals were charged in February 2026 for a $1.7 million fraudulent damage claims scheme involving Florida government — a reminder of continuous DFS/AG monitoring of inflated claims and bad-faith submissions.

Impact: Enforcement climate is active and expanding. PAs benefit from transparent documentation and defensive claim practices.

Citizens Property Insurance: Depopulation Continues at Scale

Citizens began 2026 with approximately 395,000 policies — down 76% from 2023 peak of 1.41 million. Since 2023, ~1.3 million policies have migrated to private carriers, with 546,000 transferred in 2025 alone. Citizens is no longer the state's largest property insurer.

Loss Environment & Claims Landscape

Drought Intensifying Before Anticipated Late-Month Wet Pattern

Florida faces its most severe drought in 25+ years with hundreds of wildfires in early 2026 in typically fire-resistant areas. While coastal properties aren't directly threatened, interior regions face elevated fire-adjacent claims.

April 22 conditions: Extreme drought across interior and western South Florida; moderate-to-severe drought in eastern corridor; coastal Broward and Miami-Dade classified as Abnormally Dry.

Emerging pattern: Drought-then-rain sequencing historically correlates with surges in plumbing failures, slow-leak claims, and roof deterioration. These claims are often misclassified as "neglect" by carriers, creating dispute opportunity.

Recent Weather: April 10 Severe Event

The April 10 squall line across Palm Beach, Broward, and Miami-Dade brought heavy rain, strong winds, and multiple tornado warnings. Claim cycle from that event is currently active.

Ecosystem Spotlight

Carrier Vetting Essential: New Entrants & Financial Scrutiny

With 16 new carriers approved and Citizens depopulating at scale, PAs must build a "carriers to watch" intelligence sheet before intake:

  • Claim payment patterns (not rate filings) reveal true financial discipline
  • Four of 16 new entities tie to previously-insolvent or restructured insurers — higher denial/delay risk
  • Private reinsurance pricing is falling; legislative debate on Cat Fund's role shapes carrier solvency posture
Impact: Pre-intake carrier vetting protects your firm from binding cases with weak-payers. High denial rates + frustrated clients = negative word-of-mouth and wasted hours.
