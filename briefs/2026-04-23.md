## Regulatory & Policy Shifts

### New Wind-Loss Mitigation Form (OIR-B1-1802) — Effective April 1

[Form OIR-B1-1802 was updated via Rule 69O-170.0155](https://flrules.elaws.us/far/notice/30104258) and reflects findings from the 2024 Residential Wind-Loss Mitigation Study. Eligible fixtures and construction techniques for premium discounts have been revised, and submission instructions to insurers have changed.

**Impact:** Wind-mit inspection reports drive premium discounts, and discrepancies between old-form and new-form inspections are becoming dispute areas on renewals and post-loss settlements. Expect client questions about shifted credits at renewal.

### SB 808 — Roof Inspector Pool Expansion (Effective July 1, 2026)

[Senate Bill 808](https://www.roofingcontractor.com/articles/97786-what-you-should-know-about-floridas-new-property-insurance-law) expands who can perform roof inspections to include home inspectors, building code inspectors, general/building/residential contractors, professional engineers, and professional architects — in addition to insurer-recognized inspectors.

For roofs **15+ years old**, insurers must now differentiate **low-slope (≤ 2" pitch)** from **steep-slope (> 2" pitch)** in coverage offers.

**Impact:** More eligible inspector categories = higher variance in inspection quality. Plan to scrutinize who performed an insurer-accepted inspection and whether their methodology is defensible on slope/age classification. Inspection-quality disputes will be a growing surface for claims friction.

### Senate Bill 266 — PA Contract Protections (Effective July 1, 2026)

[SB 266 strengthens consumer protections for PA contracts:](https://www.flsenate.gov/Session/Bill/2026/266/Analyses/2026s00266.pre.bi.PDF) contract disclosures must appear in **minimum 18-point font**; enhanced cancellation rights are mandated for vulnerable adults.

**Impact:** All PAs must review and update contract templates before July 1. Non-compliant contracts invite regulatory scrutiny.

## Loss Environment & Claims Landscape

### 2026 Hurricane Season Forecast: Below-Normal But Not Benign

[Colorado State University's April forecast calls for 13 named storms, 6 hurricanes, and 2 major hurricanes](https://tropical.colostate.edu/Forecast/2026-04.pdf) — slightly below the 30-year average. El Niño conditions are expected to elevate wind shear across the Tropical Atlantic.

**However:** CSU puts odds of a major hurricane landfall on the U.S. coast at **32%** (Caribbean: 35%). Season runs June 1 to November 30.

**Impact:** "Below-normal" ≠ benign. A single landfalling hurricane — Ian-class or Wilma-class — produces thousands of disputed claims regardless of seasonal total. This is the best two months to push pre-loss documentation (photos, inventories, roof-age records) and contract signings *before* the market goes reactive on June 1.

### Water Damage & Mold — Capacity Strained, Pricing Rising

[More than 3× normal rainfall since April 1](https://www.dailynewswork.com/blog/aprils-relentless-rains-south-florida-homes-face-water/) across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach has pushed remediation firms to **full capacity through summer**. [Pricing benchmarks:](https://moldonly.com/mold-remediation-costs-in-florida-2026/)
- Standard mold jobs: **$2,500–$6,000**
- Older single-family / small condo projects: **$20,000+** after insulation, cabinetry, subfloor removal

**Claim friction hotspot:** Claims that straddle policy categories — covered water event vs. excluded long-term seepage — are leaving homeowners with partial reimbursements or steep deductibles. This is prime PA territory.

### Service Industry Shifts: Roofing & HVAC Consolidation

[Workers' comp rates for roofing (code 5551) dropped 11.04% for 2026,](https://acculynx.com/florida-insurance-crisis/) but minimum premiums increased to **$25,000 per employer**. Result: consolidation pressure on small roofing operations.

[Pearl Street Capital Partners launched Panther Service Group on April 1, 2026](https://www.morningstar.com/news/pr-newswire/20260401fl25329/pearl-street-capital-partners-launches-panther-service-group-a-new-plumbing-hvac-and-electrical-services-platform-focused-in-florida) — a Florida-focused PE roll-up for plumbing, HVAC, and electrical services. Watch which local shops get acquired; post-acquisition integration typically shifts how they handle insurance work.

**Impact:** Fewer independent contractors = claim fulfillment delays and shifting referral networks. Building relationships now with pre-roll-up shops protects your ecosystem.
