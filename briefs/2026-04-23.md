## TOP STORY: CSU Issues April Hurricane Forecast — Slightly Below-Normal 2026 Atlantic Season

Colorado State University's Tropical Meteorology Project released its April extended-range forecast calling for a **slightly below-normal** 2026 Atlantic hurricane season: **13 named storms, 6 hurricanes, 2 major hurricanes**. Tropical Storm Risk (TSR) projects 12/5/1, and The Weather Channel is at 12/6/2. The moderating driver is a forecast of robust El Niño conditions that should elevate wind shear across the Tropical Atlantic and Caribbean.

Landfall probability nonetheless remains non-trivial: CSU puts the odds of a major hurricane landfall somewhere on the U.S. coast at **32%**, and **35%** for the Caribbean. Season officially runs June 1 to November 30.

**What this means for PAs:** "Below-normal" is not "benign." A single landfalling storm — Ian-class or Wilma-class — produces thousands of disputed claims regardless of seasonal total. Clients and prospects should be pushed to document properties *now* (photos, inventories, roof age records, pre-loss condition) before the June 1 bell. This is also the best two months of the year for preventative conversations and contract signings before the market goes reactive.

Sources: [CSU April 2026 Forecast (PDF)](https://tropical.colostate.edu/Forecast/2026-04.pdf) | [CBS News — First Major 2026 Forecast](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/2026-atlantic-hurricane-season-forecast/) | [Popular Science — 2026 Season Outlook](https://www.popsci.com/environment/2026-hurricane-season-prediction/)

## REGULATORY & LEGISLATIVE

### Florida OIR — New Wind-Loss Mitigation Form Takes Effect April 1

Form **OIR-B1-1802** was updated via Rule 69O-170.0155 and became effective **April 1, 2026**. The form reflects findings from the 2024 Residential Wind-Loss Mitigation Study — the fixtures and construction techniques eligible for premium discounts have been revised, and there are new submission instructions for insurers.

**Why it matters for PAs:** Wind-mit inspection reports drive premium discounts, and discrepancies between old-form and new-form inspections are already becoming an area of dispute on renewals and post-loss settlements. Expect to field questions from clients whose 2025 mitigation credits shift at renewal.
Source: [FL Administrative Register — Rule 69O-170.0155](https://flrules.elaws.us/far/notice/30104258)

### SB 808 — Expanded Roof Inspector Pool (Effective July 1, 2026)

Senate Bill 808 takes effect July 1. Key provisions:
- Roof inspections may now be performed by **home inspectors, building code inspectors, general/building/residential contractors, professional engineers, and professional architects** — in addition to any other party recognized by the insurer.
- For **roofs 15+ years old**, insurers must differentiate **low-slope (≤ 2" pitch)** from **steep-slope (> 2" pitch)** in coverage offers.

**PA angle:** More eligible inspector categories = more variance in inspection quality. Plan to scrutinize who performed an insurer-accepted inspection and whether their methodology is defensible on the slope/age classification.
Source: [Roofing Contractor — FL's New Property Insurance Law](https://www.roofingcontractor.com/articles/97786-what-you-should-know-about-floridas-new-property-insurance-law)

### 2026 OIR Insurance Summit — Tallahassee, Late April

Florida OIR is hosting the 2026 Insurance Summit in Tallahassee this month. This is the venue where carriers, reinsurers, and regulators signal direction on rate filings, reinsurance capacity, and legislative priorities. Worth monitoring press coverage for signals on reinsurance pricing into June 1 renewals.
Source: [FL OIR Newsroom](https://floir.gov/newsroom)

### NFIP — September 30 Reauthorization Cliff

The National Flood Insurance Program remains authorized through **September 30, 2026**. Florida holds ~1.7 million of the ~5 million NFIP policies nationwide — roughly **one in three** U.S. flood policies is a Florida policy. A 2025-style lapse would stall closings mid-hurricane season.

No April movement on the NFIP-RE Act (premium caps, low/middle-income affordability provisions, mitigation investment). Monitor into late summer.
Sources: [NAR — NFIP Expiration FAQ](https://www.nar.realtor/flood-insurance/faq-national-flood-insurance-program-expiration) | [NACo — NFIP Reauthorization](https://www.naco.org/resources/reauthorize-national-flood-insurance-program)

## SERVICE INDUSTRIES

### Water Damage & Mold — Remediation Capacity Strained

A persistent weather pattern earlier this month delivered **more than 3x normal rainfall** across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach since April 1. Remediation firms (Mold Only and peers) are booked **full capacity into summer**. Healthcare providers are reporting upticks in respiratory complaints tied to indoor air quality.

**Pricing benchmarks reported for 2026:**
- Standard mold jobs (modest infestation): **$2,500–$6,000**
- Older single-family / small condo projects: **$20,000+** after removal of compromised insulation, cabinetry, subfloor

**Claim friction:** Adjusters are seeing a spike in claims that **straddle policy categories** — is it a covered water event or excluded long-term seepage? Homeowners are increasingly stuck with partial reimbursements or steep deductibles. This is prime PA territory — the category ambiguity is exactly where an experienced PA adds value.

Source: [DailyNewsWork — April Rains & Mold](https://www.dailynewswork.com/blog/aprils-relentless-rains-south-florida-homes-face-water/)

### Roofing — Workers' Comp Rates Drop, Minimum Premium Up

For 2026, workers' comp rates under classification code **5551 (roofing)** dropped **11.04%**, but carriers simultaneously increased minimum premiums to **$25,000 per employer**. Result: smaller roofing operations are squeezed; consolidation pressure on 1–5 employee shops. Expect more one-truck operators closing or merging, which increases the risk of clients holding warranties from defunct contractors — a common post-loss headache.
Source: [AccuLynx — FL Roofing Contractor Outlook](https://acculynx.com/florida-insurance-crisis/)

### HVAC / Plumbing — Panther Service Group Launches

**Pearl Street Capital Partners** launched **Panther Service Group** on April 1, 2026, as a Florida-focused plumbing, HVAC, and electrical services platform. This is PE roll-up activity — expect acquisitions of family-owned South Florida trades companies through 2026. For PA referral networks, watch which local shops get acquired; the initial post-acquisition period typically shifts how they handle insurance work.
