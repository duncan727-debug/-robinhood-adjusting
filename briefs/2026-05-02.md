## Insurance & Claims

### Week-Long Trend: Rate Relief Broadening Across Auto & Property

The past week delivered consistent messaging from major carriers: insurance rates are falling. USAA filed 7% auto decreases; Allstate reported sharp Q1 profit improvements; Citizens Property Insurance is cutting 8.7% across the state. Broward, Miami-Dade, and Palm Beach are all seeing 11–14% property rate reductions. This reflects a stabilizing market environment where insurance litigation is down 35% since 2021, fraud is being prosecuted aggressively, and carriers are rebuilding confidence in the Florida market.

**Implication for PAs:** Declining rates = more stable client relationships and fewer adversarial claims negotiations. Advisors should position rate relief as part of a broader optimization strategy (mitigation, coverage audits, annual policy reviews) rather than a one-time savings event.

### Fraud Vigilance Remains Critical: PA Licensing & Credibility Top Issues

The week's Chaparro-Araus case ($600K diversion) and GEICO's operational errors both underscore a broader theme: **consumers are increasingly vigilant about insurance credibility**. Licensed public adjusters with clean records are gaining market share as homeowners learn about fraud risks. Florida's regulatory framework (10% fee caps for emergency declarations, 20% thereafter, and 10/30-day cancellation rights) protects consumers but also creates a compliance and reputation bar for legitimate PAs.

**Positioning opportunity:** Emphasize licensing, bonding, and transparent fee structures. Reference fraud cases as evidence that proper PA engagement protects homeowners.

## Real Estate & Economic Context

### Wealth Migration Accelerates: Palm Beach Surging, Miami-Dade Concentrating

Week's data confirms a multi-month trend:
- **Palm Beach:** 7th consecutive month of sales growth; total volume +29% YoY to $2.6B; single-family +44% to $1.9B
- **Miami-Dade:** Continued volume momentum (+6.6% YoY); **28% of homes now >$1M (vs. 8% in 2019)**
- **Broward:** Modest growth overall (1.4%); premium properties (+>$1M) +7.4%

The narrative is clear: **high-net-worth buyers are relocating to South Florida faster than local supply can scale.** This creates:
- **For PAs:** Opportunity to target newly relocating affluent buyers (often unfamiliar with Florida hurricane coverage, mitigation requirements, and claims processes)
- **For contractors:** Strong demand signal for mitigation and premium renovation work
- **For property owners:** Rising valuations, but also rising insurance scrutiny on older/undermitigated properties

**Critical constraint:** FHA-approved condos remain bottlenecked (only 21 of 2,397 buildings in Miami-Dade/Broward/Palm Beach). This limits purchase power for mid-income buyers and creates frustration among condo associations—a PA entry point for structural defect and bad-faith claims.

**Sunday–Monday (May 3–4):** Frontal passage bringing 60% rain chance Sunday, highs 87°. This marks the seasonal shift toward the May–June wet season pattern ahead of June 1 hurricane season opening.
