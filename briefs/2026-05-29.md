### My Safe Florida Home Keeps Cutting Grant Checks Into 2026 — $352M Carryover Plus a Proposed $600M+ FY26-27 Refill
Florida's home-hardening grant program — up to **$10,000** for impact windows, doors, shutters, roof upgrades, and wind-rated garage doors — did not run out of money when the legislative session ended. The program reopened on **August 4, 2025** with **$352 million** in funding, and the Department of Financial Services confirmed in early May 2026 that leftover funds continue to flow to new applicants through the current cycle. The proposed FY 2026-27 budget asks for **more than $600 million** in fresh appropriation, though that figure is not yet enacted.

The catch from the 2025 reauthorization is still in effect: applicants must be at or below **120% of county median income**, the home must be **homesteaded** and **built before January 1, 2008**, insured for **$700,000 or less**, and current homeowners insurance documentation is required at application. Low-income applicants (especially age 60+) move first in the queue and qualify for the full **$10,000 no-match** grant. Moderate-income applicants get a **$2-state to $1-homeowner** match.

**Why it matters:** A funded inspection plus a wind-mit retrofit can produce a measurable premium credit on the next renewal cycle, and the wind-mit form that came into force April 1 (OIR-B1-1802) is more credit-friendly to opening protection than the prior version. The combination — fresh grant dollars plus the new form — is the cleanest premium-relief play available to a Wellington / Boca / Pinecrest homeowner this summer.

### Boca Raton Re-Zones a Holiday Inn Into 125 Workforce Units — and Advances "Save Boca" Charter Amendment
On **May 12, 2026**, the Boca Raton City Council approved Resolution **45-2026**, converting the 183-room Holiday Inn at **701 NW 53rd Street** into a Commercial Industrial Multifamily Development (CIMD). The site will deliver **125 rental units, including affordable and workforce housing**. The same meeting advanced the **Save Boca** ordinance — a proposed charter amendment that would put any future sale or repurposing of city-owned land in front of voters before it can close. The charter question is now headed to the next available election cycle.

Separately, Council is scheduled to review background on **freestanding emergency facilities** at a May 26 workshop — no vote, but it is the opening step in the city's regulatory framework for stand-alone ERs, which has implications for medical-tenant commercial leasing and adjacent residential disclosure.

**Why it matters:** Boca's policy direction in May 2026 reads as two signals at once — *yes* to hotel-to-residential adaptive reuse where it adds workforce supply, and *not without a vote* on city-owned land disposition. For investors and brokers, the rule of thumb for the rest of 2026: privately-owned hospitality and retail parcels are the cleanest re-entitlement targets; anything that touches municipal land has a new political gate in front of it.

### Hurricane-Prep Sales Tax: No More Holidays — Most Supplies Are Now Permanently Exempt
The 2025 tax-relief package eliminated Florida's temporary Disaster Preparedness Sales Tax Holidays. Instead, the legislature made most hurricane-prep purchases **permanently sales-tax exempt year-round** — including batteries, fire extinguishers, insect repellent, ground-anchor / tie-down kits, life jackets, portable gas or diesel cans, portable generators, smoke detectors, sunscreen, and waterproof tarps. There is no "shopping window" to plan around this June.

A handful of items that *had* qualified during prior holidays are **not** part of the permanent exemption and are taxable again: reusable ice packs, lanterns, flashlights, candles, portable power banks, non-electric coolers, and pet supplies.

**Why it matters:** Homeowners no longer need to time generator and tarp purchases to a two-week tax window — the savings are baked in any day of the year. Service providers buying materials for jobs (tarps, anchor kits) get the same year-round break. Retail and hardware partners should update any "tax-holiday" signage that is still up from prior years.

## REGULATORY & INDUSTRY

### CILB Roofing Definition Now Includes Roof-to-Wall Connection Evaluation (HB 715, effective 2025)
HB 715, signed by Governor DeSantis on **May 19, 2025**, expanded the statutory definition of "roofing contractor" to include **evaluation and enhancement of roof-to-wall connections** on structures with wood roof decking. Practically, this consolidates work that used to drift between roofing and general-contractor scopes back inside the roofing trade. The CILB scope memo flows through to inspection, retrofit, and Form 1802 wind-mit credit work — a roofer can now bid and execute roof-to-wall strap upgrades without subbing it.

The next CILB certified-license renewal cycle closes **August 31, 2026** (licenses renew every even year). Required CE: **14 hours**, including 1 hour each of workplace safety, workers' comp, and business practices, with 11 hours of trade-specific or general construction. Contractors who have not started their CE for this cycle have 13 weeks.
