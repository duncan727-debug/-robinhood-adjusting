## Florida Will Still Pay Up to $10,000 Toward Your Hurricane Hardening — And There's Still Money Left
If you have ever read a "My Safe Florida Home" headline and assumed the program had run out, the Department of Financial Services confirmed in early May that **leftover funding from last August's $352 million reauthorization is still flowing**. New applications are still being processed for this cycle, and the proposed FY 2026-27 budget asks the legislature for **another $600 million-plus** in the fall.

What is actually on the table:
- Up to **$10,000** toward impact windows, impact doors, hurricane shutters, roof upgrades, or wind-rated garage doors.
- A free inspection from a certified inspector first — that visit is what unlocks the grant.
- **Low-income homeowners** (especially age 60+) move first in the queue and get the full $10,000 with **no match** required.
- **Moderate-income** homeowners get a **$2 from the state for every $1 you put in** match.

The catch from the 2025 reauthorization: this is now an income-and-value-tested program. Your household has to be at or below **120% of the Palm Beach County median**, your home has to be your **homesteaded primary residence**, it has to be **built before January 1, 2008**, and it has to be insured for **$700,000 or less**. You will need to upload current homeowners insurance documentation when you apply.

**Why now and not later:** Florida's new wind-mitigation form (OIR-B1-1802, in effect since April 1) gives more premium credit for opening protection — impact glass, accordion shutters, panel systems — than the form it replaced. A grant-funded retrofit that closes before your next renewal can move two needles on the same project: the structure is safer, and the premium it carries is lower. If you have been on the fence, the combination of "money still available + better form + season opens Monday" is the most lined-up the math has been in years.

**Three things to do this weekend:**
1. Check eligibility and start the application at **mysafeflhome.com** — it takes about 20 minutes.
2. Pull your current declarations page. The portal will ask for it.
3. If you already have a contractor you trust, ask whether they are on the program's approved-vendor list. The grant pays them directly — you do not float the cash.

## You No Longer Have to Wait for a "Tax Holiday" to Buy a Generator
Florida's two-week tax-free hurricane-prep windows are gone. Instead, the 2025 tax-relief package made **most prep items permanently exempt from state sales tax** — not for a window, not for the season, but **all year, every year**. The new permanent list:

- Batteries
- Portable generators
- Waterproof tarps
- Smoke detectors
- Fire extinguishers
- Insect repellent and sunscreen
- Ground anchor systems / tie-down kits
- Life jackets
- Portable gas or diesel cans

A short list of items that **used to** qualify is taxable again year-round: reusable ice packs, lanterns, flashlights, candles, portable power banks, non-electric coolers, and pet supplies. If you keep those in your prep kit, factor them in separately.

**Practical upshot:** stop circling June dates on the calendar. If your generator is ten years old and the carb hasn't been touched since 2019, just go buy the replacement.

## Weather: Bumpy Friday and Saturday Along the I-95 Corridor
The Storm Prediction Center has a **Marginal flash-flood risk** over Southeast Florida today. NWS Miami's outlook: deeper tropical moisture pushing inland from the Gulf side, colliding with the Atlantic sea breeze, focused along the I-95 corridor. Translation: locally heavy rain, **40–50 mph wind gusts** in stronger cells, frequent lightning, and a few hours of feels-like temperatures in the upper 90s.

**If you have been putting it off, do it before Sunday:**
- Walk the perimeter and clear the gutters. Two weeks of antecedent rain means even small blockages back water up into eaves and soffits.
- Check the seal around any skylight you have. Skylights are the leak this kind of system finds first.
- Pull the patio furniture in if a line of storms is forecast for your zip code.

## A Note for Citizens Property Insurance Customers
Two things to watch on your June statement:
1. The **2.6% statewide personal-lines rate cut** filed in late 2025 takes effect with June 2026 renewals. Roughly 60% of customers see a meaningful decrease; the rest see a modest one.
2. If you have been waiting on a private-carrier **takeout offer**, the FL Office of Insurance Regulation updated the 2026 approved-takeout list — American Integrity, Apex Star, Manatee, Mangrove, Slide, Southern Oak, and One Alliance are all active for this cycle. You are under **no obligation** to accept, but the comparison is worth running.
