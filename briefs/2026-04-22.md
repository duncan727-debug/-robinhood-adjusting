## Market Snapshot

### Miami-Dade Leads South Florida Transaction Growth

March 2026 data underscores Miami-Dade's outperformance: **2,134 home sales** with **total dollar volume up 15.59% year-over-year to $2.5 billion**. Median sale price: **$580K** (+1.8% YoY) with pending sales up **13.2%** YoY.

[Broward followed with **$445K median** but saw condo weakness](https://www.redfin.com/county/442/FL/Broward-County/housing-market) — prices falling, inventory growing. The **$5M+ ultra-luxury segment** in Miami-Dade climbed **27% year-over-year**, signaling strong high-net-worth demand and high-value property concentration.

[30-year mortgage rates held near **6.38%** as of late March.](https://www.greeneinsurance.com/guides/florida-insurance-market-2026)

**Impact:** Real estate velocity correlates with new policy binding and claims potential. Luxury bracket ($5M+) represents higher-value claim segment with concentrated geographic opportunity.

## Regulatory & Policy Shifts

### Public Adjuster Fraud Arrest Underscores Vetting Importance

[Francisco Javier Chaparro-Araus, 46, operating All Elements Public Adjusters in Miramar, was arrested on fraud, grand theft, and misappropriation charges after allegedly diverting **$600,000+** in Hurricane Ian insurance settlements.](https://www.claimsjournal.com/news/national/2026/04/06/336672.htm) His PA license had been previously suspended in 2015 and 2024 — a pattern that went unaddressed until criminal charges.

**Impact:** High-profile enforcement signals intensified DFS and insurer scrutiny of PA credentials. Expect insurers and claimants to vet professional relationships more closely. Fraud cases damage profession-wide reputation and invite regulatory pressure.

### Federal Fraud Actions: ACA Enrollment & Claims Inflation Schemes

[AP of South Florida, LLC (APSF) pleaded guilty to major federal fraud for using street marketers to enroll homeless and vulnerable individuals in ACA plans with falsified income data — generating **$141.5 million in unwarranted federal subsidies**.](https://oig.hhs.gov/fraud/enforcement/national-partnership-of-insurance-brokers-and-its-former-subsidiary-agree-to-pay-over-160-million-for-affordable-care-act-enrollment-fraud-scheme/) Parent company AssuredPartners agreed to pay **$135 million** in civil settlement. While health insurance-focused, this signals broad enforcement attention across the insurance sector.

Additionally, six individuals were charged in February 2026 for a **$1.7 million fraudulent damage claims scheme** involving Florida government — a reminder of continuous DFS/AG monitoring of inflated claims and bad-faith submissions.

**Impact:** Enforcement climate is active and expanding. PAs benefit from transparent documentation and defensive claim practices.

### Citizens Property Insurance: Depopulation Continues at Scale

Citizens began 2026 with approximately **395,000 policies** — down 76% from 2023 peak of 1.41 million. Since 2023, **~1.3 million policies** have migrated to private carriers, with **546,000 transferred in 2025 alone**. Citizens is no longer the state's largest property insurer.

| St. Lucie | **+~6%** (proposed) | — | Highest increase in state; aging housing stock |

**Impact:** Depopulated policyholders moving from Citizens to private carriers represent high-friction claim cohort. These owners often don't understand new carrier relationships — first major loss triggers strong PA engagement probability.

**Caution:** [16 new property insurers approved in early 2026;](https://www.louislawgroup.com/case-law-floir-new-entities-florida-insurance-market-february-2026) four tie to previously-insolvent or restructured entities. Claim payment patterns are the truest indicator of financial discipline — vet new carriers before intake.

### Legislative Protections: HB 399, HB 459, SB 1024

[HB 399 and HB 459 establish a structured administrative path for disputed property claims before court escalation,](https://www.flsenate.gov/Session/Bill/2026/399/Amendment/656240/PDF) adding a mandatory resolution step.

[SB 1024](https://flsenate.gov/Session/Bill/2026/1024/BillText/Filed/HTML) revises Citizens eligibility for residential structures in certain counties; would eliminate mandatory flood insurance as a condition for some Citizens policies.

**Impact:** New claims pathways create alternative dispute resolution opportunities and may reduce litigation velocity.

### Federal NFIP & Flood Insurance Relief

[Senators Moody and Scott introduced the Flood Insurance Relief Act,](https://www.moody.senate.gov/press-releases/sens-ashley-moody-and-rick-scott-introduce-bill-to-provide-tax-relief-for-skyrocketing-flood-insurance-costs/) offering non-refundable tax deductions for NFIP and private flood insurance premiums — direct relief for South Florida homeowners facing escalating flood costs.

[S.1015 / H.R.2822 would extend NFIP through December 31, 2026.](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/1015) The program's authorization remains a perennial legislative deadline — lapses can freeze closings and new flood policies mid-hurricane season.

## Loss Environment & Claims Landscape

### Drought Intensifying Before Anticipated Late-Month Wet Pattern

[Florida faces its most severe drought in 25+ years with hundreds of wildfires in early 2026 in typically fire-resistant areas.](https://www.drought.gov/drought-status-updates/drought-status-update-southeast-2026-04-16) While coastal properties (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach) aren't directly threatened, interior regions face elevated fire-adjacent claims.

**April 22 conditions:** Extreme drought across interior and western South Florida; moderate-to-severe drought in eastern corridor; coastal Broward and Miami-Dade classified as Abnormally Dry.

**Emerging pattern:** Drought-then-rain sequencing historically correlates with surges in plumbing failures, slow-leak claims, and roof deterioration (shrink-swell stress on clay soils, rapid wet-down of compromised seals).

**Impact:** Flag intake triage for elevated plumbing and slow-leak claim volume when anticipated late-month wet pattern arrives. These claims are often misclassified as "neglect" by carriers, creating dispute opportunity.

### Recent Weather: April 10 Severe Event

[The April 10 squall line across Palm Beach, Broward, and Miami-Dade brought heavy rain, strong winds, and multiple tornado warnings.](https://nationaltoday.com/us/fl/palm-beach-gardens/news/2026/04/10/severe-thunderstorms-batter-south-florida/) Claim cycle from that event is currently active. [Small Craft Advisory and High Rip Current Risk affected coastal properties April 8.](https://www.wptv.com/news/treasure-coast)

**Impact:** Active weather-driven claim cycle; document all related losses for intake pipeline.

With 16 new carriers approved and Citizens depopulating at scale, PAs must build a "carriers to watch" intelligence sheet before intake:

**Impact:** Pre-intake carrier vetting protects your firm from binding cases with weak-payers. High denial rates + frustrated clients = negative word-of-mouth and wasted hours.
