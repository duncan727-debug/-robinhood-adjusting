**Pace reality check:**

- **100 clients in 100 days** = 1 new client per day on average.
- **Current rate:** Unclear. If 67 drafts are sent by Friday EOD and generate a typical B2B partnership response rate (2–5%), that's 1–3 conversations by next Monday.
- **To hit 1-per-day from conversations:** Duncan would need a 40–50% conversion rate from conversation to signed client, which is unrealistic for B2B service-provider partnerships.

**The real question:** Is Duncan targeting *direct homeowner clients* (the 100-in-100 goal) or *service-provider referral partnerships* (the current strategy)? These are different funnels:

- **Direct clients:** PA reaches homeowners directly (pre-loss inspections, self-referral, carrier lists). Slower relationship-building, but each one counts toward 100.
- **Referral partnerships:** PA builds relationships with roofers, water mit firms, etc., who refer homeowners when claims happen. Faster relationship-building, but clients don't "belong" to Duncan—they're referred through a partner.

**Evidence suggests Duncan is doing referral partnerships** (the content, outreach, and trends analysis all position him as partnering with service providers, not selling directly to homeowners). This is smarter long-term positioning, but it won't hit "100 clients in 100 days" unless those partnerships start converting fast.

**Verdict:** The pace is realistic *if* Duncan executes consistently and partnerships start closing in weeks 3–4. But there's zero evidence of actual meetings yet, so the risk of drift is real.
