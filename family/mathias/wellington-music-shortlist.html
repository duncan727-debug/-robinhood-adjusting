How to approach music stores

Music stores hire people who are already in the store. Being a regular at open mics is the single biggest advantage you have — use it. The play:
  1. Don't lead with the résumé. Lead with the fact that they already know your face.
  2. Pick a slow weekday (Tue/Wed afternoon). Walk in, say hi, ask who handles hiring or open shifts.
  3. Hand the résumé to whoever owns/manages the floor — not the part-timer at the register.
  4. Mention you play guitar/sax/harmonica and offer to demo if they want — managers love an applicant who can actually play.
  5. For chains (Guitar Center), still apply online — they require it — but follow up in person.

What to Say When You Walk In

"Hey — I'm Mathias, I'm a Wellington High student. I play guitar, sax, and a little harmonica, I'm at open mics around town, and I'm looking for a summer job somewhere I can be around music. Is there anyone here who handles hiring I could talk to, or could I leave my résumé with you?"
