## How to Use This Document

Two types of groups here:
- **Join immediately** — community groups where you can participate, share resources, and build brand presence
- **Engage as expert** — groups where you should answer questions, not advertise; trust > promotion

Rule: Never post a link without adding value first. Answer a question, contribute context, then reference the resource. Get known before you get leads.

## POSTING STRATEGY BY GROUP TYPE

### Community Groups (Tier 1)
- Week 1: Join and comment on 3–5 existing threads. Don't post anything yet.
- Week 2: Share hurricane checklist as helpful resource (not an ad). "Hey all — with hurricane season starting June 1, we put this together for homeowners in Palm Beach County. No signup needed for the basic version."
- Week 3: Answer a question about insurance or contractors. Reference the free guide.
- Ongoing: Monthly resource post. Never more than once per week.

### Real Estate & Investor Groups (Tier 2)
- Join and observe for 3–5 days.
- First post: Market data or analysis (repurpose content from the daily brief).
- Establish credibility as a market intelligence source before pitching PA services.
- Target: Distressed property discussions, title dispute questions, claims history questions.

### Contractor Groups (Tier 3)
- Direct approach: "We work with South Florida contractors on claim documentation. When your customer has an insurance dispute after your repair, we're the call they should make. Happy to make introductions."
- Share the contractor vetting guide as a resource for homeowners — "Your customers can use this to vet you. If you pass all 12 checks, you'll love this document."

### HOA/Property Manager Groups (Tier 4)
- Position as an industry resource, not a service provider.
- Offer to speak at HOA meetings or write a Q&A column for their newsletter.
- Insurance policy review checklist is your leave-behind.
