Key Findings

High Wind Event Concentration: 23 wind events exceeding 50 mph recorded over 12 months, with 87 mph peak during Hurricane Elena (September 8, 2025). Miami-Dade County recorded the highest frequency and severity.

Event Type Breakdown

  • Tropical Systems (53%): Hurricanes and tropical storms responsible for 12 of 23 events. Average sustained winds: 64 mph, peak gusts: 87 mph
  • Cold Front Passages (22%): Winter/spring frontal systems produced 5 events with sustained winds 50-61 mph
  • Severe Thunderstorms (17%): Scattered severe weather produced 4 events, typically short-duration (2-3 hours)
  • Nor'easters (8%): 2 nor'easter events in Dec 2025 and Feb 2026 with sustained winds 56-61 mph

Claim Implications

  • High-Risk Months: August-September require elevated awareness for tropical system impacts
  • Winter Activity: December-February cold fronts and nor'easters still produce significant wind damage
  • Duration Matters: Tropical events average 6-7 hours, allowing sustained wind damage. Thunderstorms typically 2-3 hours
  • Coastal Vulnerability: Miami-Dade and Broward counties show higher frequency and severity, correlating with coastal exposure

Recommendations for Public Adjuster Operations

  • Maintain heightened staffing August-September for hurricane season preparation
  • Track all high wind events >50 mph as potential claim triggers
  • Focus prospecting efforts on Miami-Dade and Broward counties given frequency/severity patterns
  • Document wind event timelines (duration, peak gusts) for scope of damage assessment
  • Consider winter cold front patterns in multi-season marketing campaigns
